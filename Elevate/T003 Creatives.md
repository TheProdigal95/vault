## Sarah Jenkins (Nurse & New Mom)
*Persona: 34, RN, 2 years postpartum. Exhausted, no time for herself, feeling like a stranger in her own body.*

### Model Primary Text (Sarah Jenkins)
*Note: This is the anchor story for the Sarah Jenkins whitelisted ads. All 10 ads for this persona will use variations of this narrative or focus on specific beats.*

I didn't recognize the woman in the nursing station mirror.

It was 3:45 AM, halfway through a double shift at the hospital. 

I was washing my face with that harsh industrial soap, trying to wake myself up, when I caught my reflection.

My skin looked dull. My eyes were tired. But it was more than that.

I looked like a stranger to myself.

I’m 34. I’m a nurse. I’m a mother to a beautiful two-year-old boy. 

But for the last two years, I’ve felt like I’m living in someone else’s body.

The "incident" happened three weeks ago.

I was getting ready for my shift, and I reached for my pre-pregnancy scrubs. 

The ones I’ve kept in the front of my closet as a "goal."

I pulled them on. I held my breath. I reached for the zipper.

*Snap.*

The zipper didn't just snag. It broke.

I sat on the edge of my bed and cried for twenty minutes. 

It wasn't just about the scrubs. It was about everything.

The way I avoid being in family photos. 

The way I feel like I’m "failing" as a mom because I don’t have the energy to chase my son around the park.

By the time I get home from a 12-hour shift, I’m spent. 

I’ve tried everything. 

I spent a fortune on "healthy" meal prep services that just rotted in the fridge because I was too tired to even open the box.

I bought a gym membership that I used exactly twice in six months. 

I tried every caffeine-loaded "energy" supplement on the market. 

Nothing worked. I just felt more exhausted and more defeated.

Then, about a month ago, I was in the breakroom with another RN, Maria. 

She’s been on the night rotation for a decade, and she always seems to have this steady, calm energy.

I asked her how she does it.

She leaned in and told me she’d started a medical weight loss program through Elevate.

I was skeptical. I’ve seen the ads for those expensive medications that cost $1,200 a month. 

I told her I couldn't afford that and my insurance definitely wouldn't cover it.

She just smiled and said, "Sarah, most people who are eligible for medical support don't even know it. And insurance doesn't even enter the picture."

She showed me the Elevate site on her phone. 

It was a 100% online medical review. No doctors' offices. No waiting rooms. 

The best part? It was affordable. 

I took the quiz that night. It took maybe two minutes.

A licensed provider reviewed my medical history and created a personalized care plan for me. 

Everything was delivered discreetly to my door—medication, supplies, even anti-nausea support.

The first results moment happened about ten days in.

I woke up for my shift, and that "heavy" feeling—the one that usually makes it feel like I’m walking through mud—was just gone.

I felt... quiet. 

The "food noise"—that constant mental loop of thinking about what I’d eat on my next break—had finally stopped.

I felt in control of my body for the first time since my son was born.

Last week, I bought a new pair of scrubs. A size smaller than the ones that broke.

But the real transformation happened on my day off.

I spent the whole afternoon at the park with my son. 

I wasn't sitting on the bench watching him. I was running with him.

I didn't feel like a stranger anymore. I felt like me.

I realized I was spending hundreds of dollars a month on "quick fixes" and wasted gym fees that did nothing.

Now, I have a medical care plan that costs me about $58 a week. 

I used Afterpay to get started for $0 down. 

It’s an investment in my health, my career, and my time with my son.

If you’ve felt like you’re doing everything "right" and the scale still won't budge, it might not be a willpower problem. 

It might be a biological one.

Most people don't know they qualify for medical support until they check.

Take the quiz. See if you're eligible. It changed everything for me.
