## 2. Nandrogen — Deep Detail

### 2.1 What It Is (verbatim from site + forum verification)

> "OralOnly is a daily oral testosterone solution prescribed by a licensed physician and dispensed by a licensed compounding pharmacy. It is an alternative route of administration for patients for whom a compounded oral formulation is determined to be appropriate."
> — oralonly.com/the-science

Per ExcelMale user Day_Dreamer's report (Mar 2026), the actual pharmacy label reads:

> "Testosterone LLMD 15 mg/mL"
> LLMD = Liposomal Lipid Matrix Delivery
> Compounded by Center City Pharmacy, Miami, FL
> Form: individual twist-open plastic ampules (user's experience)
> OR pump vials (other users' experience; "mint-flavored" per u/Unique-Yak-2449)

**Note on the ampule-vs-pump discrepancy**: The Meta ads and the FAQ reference "spray under tongue" and the website says "individual packets" or "twist-open ampules" — different users have reported different physical forms. This is a credibility issue for Stage 4 buyers comparing notes. Recommend resolving before scaling.

### 2.2 Mechanism (claimed by site + founder content)

| Step | Claim |
|---|---|
| Delivery | Lipid bilayer ("fat bubbles") wrap testosterone molecules |
| Route | Sublingual — absorbed through oral mucosa directly into bloodstream |
| Liver | Bypasses first-pass liver metabolism (claimed) |
| Lymphatic | Absorbed by lymphatic system (claimed) |
| Peak | ~30 min onset, peak 2-2.5h post-dose (Day_Dreamer lab: >1500 ng/dL at 2h20m) |
| Taper | Claimed to mimic natural diurnal rhythm: high AM, low PM |

**Forum critique**: Several users (FunkOdyssey, intestinus_sturdius) dispute the DHT-stays-physiologic claim and note that "no-ester testosterone sublingually has not been proven bioavailable" outside the company's own data.

## 8. Compliance / Risk Surface

- **FTC**: All ad copy must include "compounded, not FDA-approved" disclosure per site. Skipping = risk.
- **FDA**: Compounded drugs are regulated under §503A. Nandrogen must meet USP standards. No efficacy claims allowed; only structure/function claims.
- **State telehealth**: TelegraMD/MDIntegrations maintain state-specific licensure. 50-state + DC, excluding PR.
- **HIPAA**: All PHI flows through the contracted physician network, not OralOnly LLC directly. OralOnly is the patient management platform, not the provider.
- **Ad creative risk**: AI-generated video ad acknowledged in forum ("the actual product is individual packets, not spray"). If a regulated body (FTC, FDA) scrutinizes, the ad-product mismatch is a known issue.
- **Sub-population exclusions**: "Not for use by women, anyone under 18, or anyone trying to conceive." Any ad placement that broadly targets these groups is a violation.
