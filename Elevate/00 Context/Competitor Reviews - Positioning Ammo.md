# Competitor Reviews -- Positioning Ammo

> **Purpose:** 1-3 star product/service complaints from Elevate's direct competitors, filtered for substantive issues (not just shipping/generic CS). Use to position Elevate against competitor weaknesses in ad creative.

## Competitor 1: Henry Meds

**Trustpilot:** 4.5/5, ~11,000+ reviews
**What they do:** Affordable telehealth GLP-1 (compounded semaglutide/tirzepatide). Similar model to Elevate but larger.
**Price:** ~$99-$297/month for compounded GLP-1

### Fatal Flaw: Customer Service Black Hole

Henry Meds' negative reviews overwhelmingly focus on one thing: you cannot reach anyone when something goes wrong. The company apparently grew faster than its support infrastructure can handle, and when patients need help with billing, medication gaps, or cancellation, they hit a wall.

### Theme A: Unreachable Customer Service

> "They stopped answering their phones. I have been a member for a couple years now and they just don't respond." — JP ON THE ROAD, 1 star

> "8 contact attempts. Only one call connected before being cut off. Hours on hold, no email responses." — Buttercupps, 1 star

> "Phone queues timeout after 15 minutes without answers. No email responses. Company pushes prescription renewals prematurely." — William David, 1 star

> "Cannot cancel online. Automated phone system prevents human contact. Billed nearly a year with no service or medications." — MicheleB, 1 star

> "Company responds faster to Trustpilot reviews than customer emails." — Ricky, 1 star

**Elevate positioning angle:** "At Elevate, you get a real person — not a phone tree. Our support team responds within 24-48 hours, and you can always call us directly during business hours."

### Theme B: Medication Effectiveness Doubts

> "Their medication is nowhere near as effective as genuine pharmaceutical. Provider dismissed concerns." — Lisa Tiscione, 1 star

> "Medication allegedly becomes ineffective after initial vials. Company pressures dose increases." — meechy anderson, 1 star

> "Oral Tirzepatide tablets ineffective — described as placebo. Caused joint and pelvic pain. Paid $890 for unusable three-month supply." — Zarah Wimbleton, 1 star

> "No weight loss after 4 months with no appetite suppression. Suspected insufficient active ingredient concentration." — Aline Fonseca, 2 stars

> "Six months with zero weight loss, suspected placebos or diluted medication." — Larry S, 2 stars

> "Only 5 pounds lost over 4 months using Phentermine. Customer suspects pills were not real." — Deborah Lynch, 2 stars

**Elevate positioning angle:** "Our compounded medications are prepared by licensed 503B pharmacies. We include free blood work to monitor your progress, and our patients lose an average of 15-20% body weight."

### Theme C: Billing Nightmares / Can't Cancel

> "Continued billing ($297/month) for three months post-cancellation. Cancellation system prevents completion unless selecting 'met desired weight.'" — Natalie Eubanks, 1 star

> "Continued charging nine months post-cancellation, including $50 administrative fees monthly." — JB, 1 star

### Theme D: Cold Chain / Shipping Issues

## Competitor 2: Ro (Body Program)

**Trustpilot:** 3.7/5, ~3,131 reviews
**What they do:** Major telehealth platform. GLP-1 weight loss (branded + compounded). $145/month membership + medication costs.
**Price:** $145/month membership + $149-$300+/month medication = $300-$450+/month total

### Fatal Flaw: Hidden Fees & Pricing Deception

Ro's negative reviews are dominated by one theme: the pricing is deliberately misleading. Advertised prices don't include the separate $145/month membership fee, creating a bait-and-switch perception that destroys trust.

### Theme A: Bait-and-Switch Pricing

> "This is a complete rip off. Charges $45 upfront before revealing program costs, withholds pricing information until fee is paid." — Nancy Hudak, 1 star

> "Dishonestly priced. Hidden monthly fees separate from advertised upfront cost. Misleading pricing structure suggesting '$1400 total' while charging additional $145/month." — Ghoul Guy, 1 star

> "The New Big Business Scheme. Bold $149/month display with hidden $145/month membership fee in small print. No refunds." — Todd Hale, 1 star

> "$145 monthly membership plus medication costs not clearly disclosed upfront." — Jennifer Tucker, 2 stars

> "Meds and Fees are WAY TOO MUCH!!! $500 a month for lower doses of compounded semaglutide is absolutely outrageous." — Stefanie Ferguson, 3 stars

> "I felt a little bait and switched on pricing. Tirzepatide increased from $145/mo to $299/mo without explanation. Definitely not building trust." — Nate Jolly, 3 stars

> "Website advertised $145/month but approval only offered $300/month option." — Doug Baker, 1 star

**Elevate positioning angle:** "With Elevate, the price you see is the price you pay. No membership fees. No hidden costs. No bait-and-switch. Medication, blood work, and support — all included."

### Theme B: $45 Non-Refundable Application Fee

> "Had to pay $45 just to apply. Offered compounded semaglutide despite indicating it failed previously. Requires additional payment beyond $149/month membership." — Maryann Palmer, 1 star

> "Signed up and qualified but never received medication. $145/month subscription charge applied automatically. Additional charges required for medication." — Ryan A, 1 star

> "$45 forfeited when rejecting generic GLP1. Unable to deliver Wegovy. Money taken under false pretext." — Trac Laub, 1 star

**Elevate positioning angle:** "At Elevate, if you don't qualify, you get a 100% refund. No application fees. No non-refundable deposits. We don't charge you until a doctor approves your treatment."

### Theme C: No Customer Service When Problems Arise

> "Nonexistent customer service. Automated systems only. Lost shipment unresolved. Dose adjustment requests ignored." — Kumar Agrawal, 2 stars

> "Two months without approval. Poor communication requiring message-based support. Pharmacy authorization issues unresolved." — Clay, 1 star

> "4 months of waiting, one prescription received. Dishonest insurance communication. Prescription sent early then cancelled." — L Shock, 1 star

### Theme D: Medication Delivery Delays & Ineffectiveness

> "Extremely delayed medication after I paid my membership and medication. Second refill approval took 5+ days." — May Rome, 1 star

> "Paid for 3-month supply but received only 2 months. Customer service treated him as new patient." — Brian Walsh, 1 star
