# Script Structure: Collection Failure

Creator pulls worn-out items from a box or pile of past purchases. Each gets a personal failure story and price. A running money total builds across the sequence. No numbered ranking — the emphasis is on money wasted and the one product she stopped replacing. The product is already on her feet or beside her (not in the box).

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Collection reveal hook → personal failure story per item (with price) → running money total → product as "the one I stopped replacing" → reviews → close.
- **VO register:** Female 35-50, frustrated during failures, instant brightening at product. Natural speech rhythm — "I should have kept the receipt" energy.
- **Time to benefit:** ~40s. Most of the body is failure stories; product proof is compressed but earned.
- **Rhythm:** 8-10s per failure. Pull from box → hold → personal story → price → set aside. Running total appears after the last failure.
- **Duration:** 75-90s.
- **Close type:** Social Proof or Value Math.
- **Visual grammar:** Creator pulls shoes from a box of worn-out products. Each gets a red X verdict. Running money total accumulates ($165 → $225 → $355 → $525). Product is NOT in the box — it's already worn in, beside her, or on her feet. Green check at product reveal.
- **Hook defaults:** Collection reveal (dropping failures into box). Swollen feet POV (TK direct clip). Coworker referral ("my coworker saw me limping").
- **Caption strategy:** Native — price per failure, running total, persistent red X / green check verdicts.

### When to Use
- When the buyer has cycled through 3-5 competing products over months/years
- When the total money wasted is a compelling number ($300-$600+)
- When word-of-mouth discovery (coworker, friend) is the natural referral path
- Differentiates from Countdown Ranking by removing the formal numbered structure and adding the money-waste narrative

### Key Rules
- No numbered format (#5, #4, etc.) — this is a personal story, not a ranking show
- Running money total must be accurate (sum of stated prices)
- The product is already worn in — not new, not unboxed. She's been wearing it for months.
- "The first pair I stopped replacing" is the thesis — every failure story reinforces the replacement cycle
- Coworker/friend referral threaded into the narrative adds social proof the ranking format doesn't have
