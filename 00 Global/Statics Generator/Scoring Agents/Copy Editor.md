# Copy Editor

Grammar, banned constructions, and formatting. This dimension has VETO POWER — any mechanical error blocks approval regardless of other scores.

---

## What This Dimension Evaluates

Clean, mechanically correct copy that passes every construction rule in Universal Copy Rules and Headline & Text Hook Criteria. This is the final gate. Strategic brilliance with an em dash is a fail. One spelling error in an otherwise perfect brief is a fail. The copy must be mechanically flawless.

---

## Required Reads

- `00 Global/Criteria/Universal Copy Rules.md` — banned constructions, dollar/number formatting, voice and flow rules, redundancy rules, qualitative copy standards
- `00 Global/Criteria/Headline & Text Hook Criteria.md` — headline-specific formatting rules, register-appropriate punctuation

---

## Scoring Checklist

Every item below is checked. Any failure is a deduction. Banned construction failures trigger the veto.

### Grammar and Mechanics
- Spelling correct throughout (brand names, ingredient names, common words)
- Subject-verb agreement
- Punctuation correct (no misplaced commas, no missing periods)
- Consistent tense within each section
- No sentence fragments that read as unintentional (intentional fragments for pacing are allowed if they pass the "read aloud" test)

### Banned Constructions (from Universal Copy Rules)
- No em dashes anywhere in the copy
- No en dashes anywhere in the copy
- No semicolons in headlines
- No false dichotomies ("That's not X. That's Y.")
- No "It's not X. It's not Y. It's not Z." lists
- No swearing, even censored
- No writerly metaphors or stock transformation beats
- Max one analogy per brief
- No fourth-wall breaks ("That's not a sales pitch," "I know this sounds like an ad")
- No attribution layers ("the women say," "people are saying")
- No noun-phrase fragment stacking without subject + verb bridging

### Number and Dollar Formatting
- Written text: dollar amounts as numerals ($49, $149/month, $599)
- Percentage stats include "of users" or "of people" ("95% of users" not "95%")
- Dosages and percentages as numerals (credibility signals)
- Number consistency within the brief (don't mix "$49" and "forty-nine dollars" in the same text type)

### Brand Name and Product
- Brand name capitalized correctly per brand context
- Product names spelled and formatted consistently
- Ingredient names use correct forms (e.g., "magnesium glycinate" not "magnesium glycinate ")

### Redundancy (from Universal Copy Rules)
- No repeated phrasing between headline variations and body
- No echoed hook-closing phrase at body opening
- No two headline variations that say the same thing in different words
- No duplicate data points across elements (same stat in headline AND pills AND primary text)

## Calibration

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 60 | Multiple mechanical errors. Several banned constructions or grammar issues. | Two em dashes, one spelling error, inconsistent number formatting ($49 in one place, $49.00 in another), a false dichotomy construction |
| 80 | One minor issue. The copy is largely clean with a single inconsistency. | All constructions pass, grammar clean, but one number format inconsistency ("$2.61 a day" in primary text should be "$2.61/day" for written text) |
| 95 | Zero mechanical errors. Every construction passes Universal Copy Rules. Every number formatted correctly. Every brand name capitalized right. | Flawless mechanics across all checklist items |

---

## Veto Power

This dimension has veto authority over the entire evaluation. The veto rule:

**ANY grammar error, spelling error, or banned construction blocks approval regardless of all other dimension scores.** If Persona Fit is 98, Awareness Stage is 95, Messaging Angle is 97, Three Selves is 96, Brand Compliance is 95, Format Compliance is 93 — and Copy Editor finds one em dash — the overall result is VETO, not PASS.

The veto triggers on:
- Any banned construction from Universal Copy Rules
- Any grammar error
- Any spelling error
- Any brand name misspelling or incorrect capitalization

The veto does NOT trigger on:
- Minor number format inconsistencies (these deduct points but don't veto)
- Stylistic choices that are debatable (these are flagged, not vetoed)

---

## Feedback Format

When this dimension scores below 90 or triggers a veto, feedback must include:
1. Every error found, listed individually (not grouped)
2. The exact text containing the error
3. The rule it violates (with reference to the criteria doc section)
4. The corrected text — not "fix the em dash" but "replace 'gut health — and everything that comes with it' with 'gut health, and everything that comes with it'"
