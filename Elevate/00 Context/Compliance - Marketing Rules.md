# Compliance — Marketing Rules for Elevate Health

**MUST BE READ BEFORE WRITING ANY BRIEF, SCRIPT, OR AD COPY FOR ELEVATE.**

Source: Three compliance documents received 2026-04-14 (Best Practices for Marketing, Compounded GLP-1 Marketing Guidelines by Telegra, Creative Update Guide for Compounded GLP-1 Marketing). Original PDFs archived in `/Users/marce/Desktop/Elevate guidelines/`.

The golden rule from the Creative Update Guide:

> "If the creative would still make sense without mentioning GLP-1 at all, it's probably compliant."

---

## 1. Language That Is BANNED

### Drug-related terms

- **"GLP-1" as a product name** — including "GLP-1 that works," "compounded GLP-1," "GLP-1+," "next-gen GLP-1," "stronger GLP-1." The term can appear in educational/biological context (e.g., "GLP-1 receptors" in a mechanism explanation where unavoidable), but NEVER as a product being sold.
- **Brand names** — Wegovy, Ozempic, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza. Never in copy, headlines, primary text, VoC quotes, or image text. Only allowed location: the mandatory brand-ownership disclaimer.
- **"Same active ingredient as [brand]"** — FDA sent a warning letter to Ozempen for this exact phrasing on July 2, 2024. Avoid all variations.
- **"Generic [brand]"** — there is no such thing as generic Wegovy, generic Ozempic, etc. The FDA has not approved any generic GLP-1s. Stating otherwise is a federal law violation.

### Method of administration

- **"Injection(s)" / "inject"** — never use in VO, captions, or copy, especially when paired with any visual that could be interpreted as injection-related. Meta's automated review flags the word + visual combination. This applies even if no syringe/pen is shown — the word itself alongside healthcare visuals triggers review. Source: media buying feedback, T004 (June 2026).

### Results and outcome claims

- **Specific weight numbers** — "Lost 34 lbs," "72 lbs in 13 months," "94 lbs in under a year." Any quantified weight loss in headlines, primary text, VoC quotes, or image overlays.
- **Percentages** — "15-20% body weight," "lose 10% in 3 months." Banned.
- **Time-based promises** — "in 6 months," "by summer," "in your first year."
- **"Guaranteed weight loss"**
- **"Clinically proven results"**
- **"Proven results"**
- **"Before & after"** — framing, wording, or visual.
- **"Results you can see"**

### Safety and efficacy claims

- **"Safe"** — legal term reserved for FDA-approved drugs. Compounded drugs cannot make this claim.
- **"Effective"** — same legal issue.
- **"Well-tolerated"** — no studies have been performed on compounded semaglutide to support this.
- **"Zero side effects"** — implies well-tolerated.
- **"Clinically proven"**
- Any variation that implies FDA-approved-level safety or efficacy testing.

### Florida Board of Medicine (Rule 64B8-11.001) — advertising restrictions

Advertising weight loss treatment is considered false, deceptive, or misleading if it:
- Promises specific results
- Raises unreasonable expectations
- Claims rapid, dramatic, incredible, or safe weight loss
- States or suggests that diets or exercise are not required
- Suggests that weight loss is effortless or magical

## 2. Visuals That Are BANNED

- Drug vials, syringes, pens
- Medication packaging
- Before/after photos
- Side-by-side transformation imagery
- People pulling oversized jeans (or any "used to be big" visual trope)
- Any graphic that implies "old body → new body"
- Fitness "after" bodies used as proof

## 3. What We CAN Use

### Approved creative angles

- Eligibility & access
- Personalized care
- Provider-led evaluation
- Convenience & affordability
- Support & guidance
- Lifestyle improvement (non-quantified)

### Approved language patterns

**Headlines (safe frameworks):**
- "Medical weight loss, personalized for you"
- "Provider-led weight loss care"
- "A weight loss plan made for your body"
- "Prescription weight loss support, if eligible"
- "Medical care designed around you"

**Benefit bullets:**
- "Medication options determined by a licensed provider"
- "Prescription treatment options, if clinically appropriate"
- "Care plans personalized after medical review"
- "Clinically guided weight loss care"
- "Medical support throughout your journey"
- "Provider oversight at every step"

**Outcome language (approved):**
- "Some patients experience appetite changes"
- "Weight loss may occur"
- "Results vary by individual"
- "No two care plans are the same"

**Pain points (approved — lived experiences, not drug claims):**
- Food noise (mental obsession with food)
- Seatbelt click (buckle won't reach)
- Chair with arms (can't fit)
- Shoe-tying stool (loss of mobility)
- Fitting room (can't get jeans on)
- Insurance denial (access barrier)
- Diet cycle fatigue (decades of yo-yo dieting)
- Willpower blame (self-blame for biology)

### Approved CTAs

CTAs must lead to medical review, not checkout. Use:
- "See if you qualify"
- "Get started"
- "Begin your medical review"
- "Check eligibility"
- "Explore care options"

Banned CTAs:
- "Get semaglutide" / "Start GLP-1" / "Order now" / "Lose weight fast"

### Approved pricing language

Pricing must NEVER be tied directly to a drug.

- ✅ "Programs starting at $54/week"
- ✅ "Care plans available with payment options"
- ✅ "Affordable medical weight loss support"
- ❌ "GLP-1 for $54/week"
- ❌ "Semaglutide starting at $54"
- ❌ "Tirzepatide from $54/week"

### Testimonials and VoC

Testimonials must describe experience, not results.

- ✅ "I finally felt supported."
- ✅ "The process was easy and clear."
- ✅ "I liked having a provider involved."
- ✅ "The plan felt personalized."
- ❌ "I lost 34 lbs."
- ❌ "This medication saved me."
- ❌ "GLP-1 changed my life."
- ❌ Any testimonial with weight numbers, timeframes, or specific results.

## 4. Mandatory Disclosures

### Supportive disclosures (use where appropriate)

- "Compounded medications are not FDA-approved"
- "Medications prescribed only if clinically appropriate"
- "Results vary by individual"

### Mandatory brand-ownership disclaimer

> "Disclaimer: FDA-approved medications like Saxenda®, Victoza®, Wegovy®, and Ozempic® are proprietary to Novo Nordisk™, while Mounjaro™ and Zepbound™ are products of Eli Lilly and Company™. This website is not associated with or endorsed by these companies or their products. We do not supply these medications. Individuals interested in these products should consult with their licensed healthcare provider or pharmacist. Compounded drug products are not approved or evaluated for safety, effectiveness, or quality by the FDA."

**Where this disclaimer goes:** In the IMAGE itself, as fine-print legal text at the bottom of the design — same as the existing top spenders (Ads 01-12 all carry their disclaimer in the image, not in the primary text). NOT in the primary text on the platform. Designers add this as small legal type at the bottom of every creative.

**Disclosures do NOT fix non-compliant claims.** They only support compliant creative.

## 5. Pre-Launch Checklist

Before any brief ships to a designer, every concept must pass this checklist:

- ☐ No drug names in headline, subheading, or primary text
- ☐ "GLP-1" not used as product branding anywhere
- ☐ No specific weight numbers or percentages
- ☐ No time-based result promises
- ☐ No "before/after" framing
- ☐ No drug imagery (vials, syringes, packaging)
- ☐ No "injection(s)" / "inject" in copy, VO, or captions (Meta flagging risk)
- ☐ No "safe," "effective," "well-tolerated," "zero side effects," or "clinically proven" language
- ☐ No Florida Rule violations (rapid/dramatic claims, effortless/magical framing, dismissing diet/exercise)
- ☐ Focuses on care, access, eligibility, or lived experience
- ☐ CTA leads to medical review, not checkout
- ☐ Mandatory brand-ownership disclaimer present where ad discusses weight loss medications
- ☐ Pricing framed as "Programs starting at $X/week" — not tied to a drug

If any box is unchecked → revise before launch.

## 6. Quick Reference: What Stays, What Goes

### Still works (preserve the edge)
- Food noise as a symptom/experience
- Pain point imagery and language
- Voice of customer (experience-based)
- Payment plans (decoupled from drug)
- "Medical weight loss" as a category phrase
- "No insurance needed" (access framing)
- Provider-led / personalized care positioning
- Creative + Social VoC, Grimy Poster, VoC Editorial, IG Stories Widget, Premium Frosted Glass, Comparison Table formats (all structurally compliant — just lose drug imagery and numbers)

### Has to go
- Drug vials/syringes in imagery
- Before/after photos
- Specific weight loss numbers (pounds, percentages, timeframes)
- "GLP-1" as a product name
- Brand name references outside the required disclaimer
- "Same active ingredient as [brand]"
- "Safe," "effective," "well-tolerated," "zero side effects"
- Pricing directly tied to a drug
