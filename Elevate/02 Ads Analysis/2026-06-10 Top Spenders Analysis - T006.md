**Date:** 2026-06-10
**Source Data:** Motion API pull — L14 (May 27–Jun 9, 2026), top 50 creatives
**Motion Report:** [Elevate Top Creatives](https://projects.motionapp.com/organization/6214fe5e1ccc60d23897d87b/68c44c43d4196b03cb80d1b0/top/68c44c45bfc760f386bd43b0)
**Media Analysis:** Motion metadata only — no Gemini visual analysis run yet.

## TLDR Key Learnings

> - **Statics convert. Video doesn't (yet).** 13 of 14 creatives in the $50-$850 CPA window are static images. The single video converter is the Shame to Pride Selfie Walk ($521 spend, 1 purchase) — still the only video format that closes.
> - **Financial Accessibility is still the conversion engine.** 7 of 14 converters use it as the primary messaging theme. Price/payment plan framing is the #1 objection-handling mechanism.
> - **"Am I Eligible?" Search Bar is the best reliable converter.** $651 spend, 3 purchases, $217 CPA. Direct, clear, Google-mockup format with payment logos. Highest purchase count and most statistically reliable signal in the window.
> - **Payment Plan AMA is the highest-spend converter.** $1,306 spend, 2 purchases, $653 CPA. Reliable but inefficient — needs iteration.
> - **The Whitelist testimonial batch is dead.** The David + Maria late-May variants burned ~$16K+ over 14 days with minimal to no conversions despite 18-25% CTR. Click magnetism without conversion — the creative attracts non-buyers.
> - **The gap: "Diets failed you but medicine won't."** The strongest converters combine financial accessibility with a reframing that validates the viewer (it wasn't your fault, it was biology) and then offers an affordable medical solution. This message layer is under-represented in the account relative to its performance signal.
> - **Food noise visualization is a proven problem-depiction format** (City Poster Access, $110 CPA, 4.56 ROAS) but hasn't been iterated at scale. Most ads depict the solution without showing the problem first.

### Why These Are Spending

**The Whitelist testimonial batch is eating the account.** Out of the top 20 by spend, approximately 13 are Whitelist native photos (David + Maria scenes from late May). The platform is scaling them to 5-digit spend because they drive massive engagement (18-25% CTR, lots of reactions, comments, shares), but the conversion rate doesn't compensate. The high-CTR, low-conversion pattern is a classic sign that the creative attracts a scroller audience, not a buyer audience — or the landing page isn't closing the promise the creative makes.

**The financial-accessibility statics (#11–16) are scaling more efficiently.** Payment Plan AMA, "This Failed You" split screen, and the search/payment-focused formats are converting at meaningful CPA ($217–$960). The platform isn't pushing them to 5-digit spend because they're narrower in appeal (lower CTR, 2-5%), but the click-to-purchase conversion is 10-20x better than the Whitelist batch.

**The before/after videos (#4, #9) are getting spend but only one converts.** Before/After Transformation V4 ($3,113, 1 purchase) and POV Before/After V2 ($0 conversions). POV format has been dead since T004 — it's the 4th straight analysis confirming this.

### What Converts vs What Doesn't

**Converts:** Financial Accessibility statics (payment plans, price anchors, eligibility/quiz formats), comparison/Us vs Them formats, and the emotional-arc selfie walk video. Every confirmed converter either shows the price or makes the viewer feel they can afford this.

**Gains spend, doesn't convert:** The Whitelist testimonial batch is the #1 offender (~$16K+ across 14+ creatives, mostly 0 purchases). These are real-person photos in real settings (grill, hallway, pharmacy, backyard, meeting room) with long-form testimonial copy. CTR is 18-25% — massive scroll-stop — but the audience clicking isn't converting. The creative promises a relatable story; the landing page asks for a quiz/purchase. The two aren't bridged.

**Dead angles:**
- ❌ **Whitelist native photos** — confirmed dead for the 4th consecutive analysis. Maria Desk alone spent $28K over 14 days with a $4,033 CPP. The format itself doesn't close despite massive attention.
- ❌ **POV Before/After video** — $1,363 spend, 0 purchases. Dead since T004.
- ❌ **Real People Real Results V1** (without offer) — $1,295 spend, 0 purchases. V2 with the offer converts.

### Why These Convert

- **The financial-accessibility conversion kit is non-negotiable.** Every converting static has a payment-related element: "$0 down" language, payment plan logos (Affirm/Afterpay/Klarna), price chunking ($58/week, $8/day), or "See if you qualify" eligibility framing. This is the kit that closes.
- **Processing fluency wins.** The best converters are simple: one idea per frame, legible in 1-2 seconds. The Search Bar mockup, the Offer-First Banner, the Big Headline — all can be understood in a single glance. The Whitelist batch fails this test: long-form testimonial copy requires sustained reading that scroll behavior doesn't support.
- **"Am I Eligible?" works because it's a self-selecting search.** The Google search UI mockup makes the viewer imagine themselves typing it. It's interactive without being interactive — the viewer finishes the thought. "Am I eligible?" → they click the quiz to find out.
- **"This Failed You" converts because it validates before it pitches.** The split screen shows "what failed you" (insurance, diet industry, willpower) vs. "what won't" (Elevate's payment plans). The left side makes the viewer feel understood; the right side provides the affordable solution.

## 4. Creative Anatomy: Top Performer Breakdowns

### Search Bar "Am I Eligible?" — Most Reliable Converter

- **Metrics:** Spend $651 | Purchases 3 | CPA $217 | ROAS 2.73 | CTR 2.7%
- **The engine:** Self-selecting search UI. The Google search bar mockup makes the viewer imagine themselves searching "Am I eligible for affordable weight loss?" The ad doesn't ask a question — it shows the viewer asking their own question, which creates a curiosity gap that only the quiz CTA resolves.
- **Format:** Quiz UI + Native Search mockup
- **Messaging theme:** Financial Accessibility
- **Headline tactic:** Relatability — directly mirrors the viewer's internal thought
- **Why it works:** Zero creative friction. It looks like a Google search result, not an ad. The viewer's brain processes it as "information I was looking for" rather than "something being sold to me."

### City Poster Access (Grimy) — Highest ROAS Signal (Low Spend)

- **Metrics:** Spend $219 | Purchases 2 | CPA $110 | ROAS 4.56 | CTR 4.4%
- **The engine:** Visualizes food noise as a problem (food imagery swirling across the subject's face in a grimy, real-feeling urban setting) while simultaneously providing the medical solution with payment logos (Affirm, Klarna visible). The problem and the solution exist in the same image.
- **Format:** Billboard + Us Vs Them (the problem vs. the cure)
- **Messaging theme:** Financial Accessibility
- **Headline tactic:** Contrast — the gap between "food noise controlling your life" and "medicine that's affordable"
- **Why it works:** It's the only converter that shows the problem. Most ads skip to the solution. Showing food noise makes the viewer feel recognized before being offered the escape.

### Shame to Pride Selfie Walk — Only Converting Video

- **Metrics:** Spend $521 | Purchases 1 | CPA $521 | ROAS 0.94 | CTR 12.1%
- **The engine:** Emotional arc from fear/shame/doubt → confidence/pride/freedom, told through a single continuous selfie walk with text wall captions. The viewer starts scared and skeptical ("I didn't want anyone to know") and ends up empowered and evangelizing ("Now I want to tell everyone"). The trending audio provides emotional lift while text carries the story.
- **Format:** Single continuous take, text wall stacking, persistent captions
- **Messaging theme:** Discreet Delivery (tagged) — but the real theme is the emotional journey from hiding to sharing
- **Why it works:** She's scared, nervous, afraid, skeptical, doubtful, ashamed at first. She didn't want to tell people. But when she saw results, she wanted to tell everyone. That emotional arc is universal for this audience — the shame of medical weight loss, the doubt about whether it would work, the fear of judgment, and the eventual pride and evangelism. The continuous walk format makes it feel authentic and unscripted. The trending audio provides the emotional energy.
- **T005 retention data:** 79% hold, 79% Thruplay, 43/39/26/20 retention quartiles. The audience that starts watching stays watching.

### This Failed You V3 (Split Screen) — Blame Framing Converter

- **Metrics:** Spend $523 | Purchases 1 | CPA $523 | ROAS 2.29 | CTR 3.0%
- **The engine:** Typographic blame framing — the left side names everything that failed the viewer (diets, insurance, willpower supplements), the right side shows what won't (Elevate's medical approach + payment plans). Pure text, no photography. The viewer reads the left side and feels validated; reads the right side and sees the solution.
- **Format:** Split Screen + Us Vs Them
- **Messaging theme:** Product Efficacy
- **Headline tactic:** Contrast
- **Why it works:** It reframes weight loss failure as a system failure, not a personal one. "Diets failed you but medicine won't" — this is exactly the message the audience needs to hear before they can act.

## 5. Cross-Ad Pattern Synthesis

### Format × Performance

**Static images dominate conversion.** 13 of 14 CPA-filtered creatives are images. The single video (Shame to Pride Selfie Walk) converts but at 1 purchase / $521 — not statistically scalable yet. Within statics:
- **Search/Quiz UI mockups** → highest reliable CPA ($217)
- **Offer-First Banners** → multiple conversions, reliable at $200-$1,300 spend
- **Us Vs Them comparison tables** → strong early signals ($176 CPA, 4.23 ROAS)
- **Whitelist native photos** → dead. Attention without conversion.

### Visual Choices × Performance

- **Ads that show the problem** (food noise on City Poster, "failed you" list on split screen) outperform ads that only show the solution.
- **Payment logos in the creative** (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay) appear in most converters. They're a non-negotiable conversion element.
- **Simple, text-dominant creatives** (typographic headlines, search mockups, big numbers) outperform photography-heavy creatives for this account.

### Heuristic Distribution

**Present in converters:**
- **Framing Effect** — "$58/week" and "$8/day" chunking across most converters
- **Processing Fluency** — clean single-message layouts in the top converters
- **Social Proof** — "35,000+ patients" and review elements in several converters

**Absent from the account:**
- **Temporal Discounting** — no ads frame results as immediate ("from your first dose"). The account hasn't tested "see results in X days" language.
- **Affect Heuristic** — beyond the selfie walk, no ads drop the viewer INTO a feeling. The emotional arc is under-represented.
- **Goal Gradient** — no progress bars, no step-by-step visuals. The quiz is the only entry point but nothing shows how close to finishing it is.

### Who Is Shown × Performance

The most efficient converters don't show real people at all — they're typographic/UI mockups (Search Bar, Split Screen, Offer Banner). When people appear, they're either:
- **In UGC overlay format** (selfie with text, converting at $228 CPA)
- **In the selfie walk video** (emotional arc, converting at $521 CPA)

The Whitelist photos show real people in lifestyle settings but DON'T convert. The difference is that the converting "people" creatives pair the person with a clear financial hook; the Whitelist batch pairs the person with a long testimonial that doesn't close.

### What's Missing (Opportunity Gaps)

1. **"Diets failed you but medicine won't" as a full creative campaign** — This Failed You is a strong signal ($523 CPA, 2.29 ROAS, $960 in V1) but only 2 creatives deep. The message needs iteration at volume.
2. **Food noise visualization at scale** — City Poster is the only creative that shows the problem visually. The food-noise-in-your-face visual could be iterated across different settings, demographics, and moments.
3. **Emotional arc in static formats** — The selfie walk's shame→pride arc hasn't been translated to static. A series showing the before/after emotional states (not just bodies) could fill this gap.
4. **Temporal discounting hooks** — Zero ads frame the results as immediate. "Your food noise quiets down by week 1" / "First dose ships in 3 days" — this heuristic stack hasn't been tested.
5. **Video beyond selfie walk** — The selfie walk format is proven but it's the ONLY video format converting. New emotional entry points (anger, disbelief, regret) need video testing.

## 6. Decision Framework

### Replicate (with guardrails)

- **"Am I Eligible?" Search Bar format** — iterate with new search queries, new UI mockups (app store searches, "weight loss near me" etc.). Guardrail: must include payment logos and financial hook.
- **"This Failed You" blame framing split screen** — new blame targets (supplements, diet industry, gym, med spa pricing, branded medication cost). Guardrail: must pair blame with financial accessibility, never blame alone.
- **Selfie walk emotional arc** — expand with new emotional entry points beyond nervous/shame. Anger at insurance, disbelief at price, regret about waiting, skepticism overcome. Guardrail: every script needs a unique IG audio and the full price/payment close.
- **City Poster food-noise visual** — iterate the "problem depicted" approach across different settings. Guardrail: problem visualization is most effective when paired with the solution in the same image.

### Kill (with evidence)

- ❌ **Whitelist native photos (David + Maria, late May batch)** — ~$16K+ spend over 14 days. David Grill alone: $7,110, 0 purchases. Maria Hallway V2: $1,428, 0 purchases. Combined 14 creatives, near-zero conversions. The format gets 18-25% CTR but doesn't close.
- ❌ **POV Before/After video** — $1,363 spend, 0 purchases. Dead since T004. Fourth consecutive analysis confirming.
- ❌ **Real People Real Results V1** (without offer/price) — $1,295 spend, 0 purchases. V2 with the financial offer converts. The financial layer is the converter, not the testimonial alone.

### Test (gaps and opportunities)

- **"Diets failed you" campaign** — expand the blame framing message across multiple formats (split screen, text wall, video talking head). The core message: validate the viewer (it's not your fault) → reframe (it's biology, not discipline) → offer (affordable medicine from $58/week).
- **Food noise visualization at scale** — new visuals depicting what food noise looks like (swirling food thoughts, midnight fridge moments, grocery aisle paralysis) paired with the affordability solution.
- **Emotional arc statics** — 2-frame or 3-frame image sequences showing: fear/shame → trying → pride/confidence. Translate the selfie walk's narrative power to static format.
- **Temporal discounting hooks** — "Your food noise quiets in the first week" / "First dose at your door in 3-7 days" / "Quiz results in 60 seconds."
- **New video formats** — Green screen blame framing, talking head + overlay template, progression timeline. Bring the "This Failed You" energy to video.

### Split Recommendation

| Bucket | Count | What It Must Test |
|---|---|---|
| Blame framing ("diets failed you") | 4-6 | The core T006 message across formats. Pair validation + reframe + financial offer. |
| Selfie walk expansion | 4-5 | Proven format, new emotional entry points. Each needs unique IG audio. |
| Search/quiz UI mockup iteration | 3-4 | The "Am I Eligible?" format with new queries and UI treatments. |
| Food noise visualization | 3-4 | City Poster approach at scale — show the problem, offer the affordable medicine. |
| New video formats (non-selfie-walk) | 3-4 | Blame framing in video, green screen + overlay template, progression timeline. |
| Emotional arc statics | 2-3 | Before/after emotional states in image format. |

### Risks / Guardrails

- **Don't over-index on low-spend ROAS signals.** City Poster ($219 spend, 4.56 ROAS), Denial Letter ($238, 5.03 ROAS), Comparison Table ($352, 4.23 ROAS) are promising but not statistically proven. Scale them cautiously — confirm at $500+ spend before declaring winners.
- **Every creative must include the financial conversion kit.** Payment logos, price chunking ($58/week), $0 down, eligibility CTA. No creative converts without it.
- **No more Whitelist native photos in this format.** The evidence across T003, T004, T005, and T006 L14 data is conclusive: high attention, no conversion.
- **Keep Food Noise as a supporting visual element, not the primary angle.** It works when paired with the financial solution (City Poster) but doesn't convert as a standalone hook.

## 7. Carry-Forward Creative Learnings

> Named facts from the data. Not recommendations — facts.

- **Statics convert at this account. Video doesn't yet.** 13 of 14 CPA $50-$850 creatives in L14 are images. The selfie walk is the sole video exception.
- **The Search Bar "Am I Eligible?" format is the most statistically reliable converter** — $651 spend, 3 purchases, $217 CPA across 14 days. Direct, clear, Google-mockup UI.
- **"This Failed You" blame framing converts at 2.29 ROAS** ($523, 1 purchase). The message that diets/insurance/supplements failed the viewer, but medicine won't, has a stronger close rate than pure price messaging.
- **City Poster visualizing food noise has 4.56 ROAS at $219 spend** — the only creative depicting the problem AND offering the affordable solution in a single image. Promising but low spend.
- **Whitelist native photos are dead for this account.** $28K spend on Maria Desk, 7 purchases = $4,033 CPP. Combined David + Maria variant batch: ~$16K+, near-zero conversions. Fourth consecutive analysis.
- **Shame to Pride Selfie Walk remains the only converting video** — 79% hold, 79% Thruplay in T005 retention data. The emotional arc from shame→pride is the proven video mechanism.
- **Financial Accessibility messaging is in 7 of 14 converters.** Price access is the backbone of every performing creative. Payment logos, "$0 down," eligibility framing are non-negotiable conversion elements.
- **POV Before/After video is dead** — 0 purchases across 14 days, confirmed across T004, T005, T006 L14.
