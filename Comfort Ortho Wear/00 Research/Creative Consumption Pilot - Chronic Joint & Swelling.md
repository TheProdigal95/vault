## 1. Quick Read

- **Organic feed type:** Foot-health authority, adaptive edema footwear demos, hands-free shoe proof, caregiver/senior safety reviews, and lived-condition shoe reviews.
- **Best creative bet:** Show the shoe physically adapting to swelling: open wide, foot enters without forcing, closure adjusts, top/instep does not squeeze, then the wearer walks safely.
- **Do not force:** Generic cute-comfort UGC, AI-looking foot B-roll, broad orthopedic listicles, TikTok Shop keyword stuffing, or disease-treatment claims.
- **Main viewer need:** "Will this still fit when my foot changes, and can I put it on without pain, bending, or help?"
- **Confidence:** High for YouTube health/adaptive-footwear patterns; medium for Reddit/Pinterest problem-language support; low for TikTok/Instagram creator-feed grammar because regular TikTok search repeatedly returned a server-error state in this run.

**Bottom line:** The core direction still holds, but the proper rerun makes it more specific. This persona is not buying "comfort" in the abstract. They are buying footwear that handles changing foot volume, sensitive skin, arthritis stiffness, no-bend entry, and caregiver safety when regular shoes have physically stopped working.

## 2. What Changed In V3

- **The selection was pool-first, not random.** The run collected 153 unique YouTube candidates across 12 persona-derived search terms, deduped them, then selected 26 for engagement, persona fit, and lane coverage.

All showed TikTok's rendered "Something went wrong" server-error state with zero accessible result cards. This is logged as an access failure, not as evidence that TikTok has no results.

## 3. Feed Lanes

### Swelling Health Education

- **Looks like:** Doctor or clinic explains causes of foot/ankle swelling with talking head, medical diagrams, stock footage, symptom lists, and cautionary advice.
- **Viewer wants:** To understand why feet are swelling and what matters before buying another failed shoe.
- **Comfort translation:** Borrow the clarity and diagnostic framing, but keep claims to fit, entry, pressure, stability, and comfort. Do not imply Comfort treats edema, diabetes, circulation, lymphedema, or arthritis.
- **Representative sources:** Michigan Foot Doctors, Talking With Docs.

### Adaptive Edema Shoe Demo

- **Looks like:** Seller or expert opens the shoe wide, pulls back a flap, shows the heel, removes the insole, points to depth/width, and explains how swelling changes fit.
- **Viewer wants:** To see whether the shoe can fit when normal shoes physically cannot.
- **Comfort translation:** Make the opening and adjustment the hero. The foot should enter after the shoe is visibly opened, not after a generic product glamour shot.
- **Representative sources:** DT Footwear, Enovis/Bilby Shoes, Buck and Buck, DB Wider Fit Shoes.

### Hands-Free / No-Bending Proof

- **Looks like:** A regular shoe is hard to put on, then the replacement shoe slips on without bending or fighting with laces.
- **Viewer wants:** Relief from the daily act of getting dressed when knees, back, arthritis, swelling, or mobility make shoes frustrating.
- **Comfort translation:** Show no-bend entry as a daily dignity/safety mechanic, not just a convenience feature.
- **Representative sources:** Orthofeet, FitVille.

### Caregiver / Senior Safety

- **Looks like:** A caregiver or senior-care reviewer explains the purchase for a parent, grandfather, husband, or senior man with swollen feet and fall-risk concerns.
- **Viewer wants:** Confidence that the person they care for can wear shoes without squeezing, slipping, or living in unsafe slippers.
- **Comfort translation:** Make caregiver purchase a standalone concept. Show the caregiver noticing the problem and solving it with easy entry, adjustable fit, and non-slip walking.
- **Representative sources:** Silverts, Graying With Grace, Reddit AgingParents threads.

### Lived-Condition Review

- **Looks like:** A person with RA, lipedema, lymphedema, wide feet, or chronic swelling compares shoes and talks about what works in real life.
- **Viewer wants:** Someone with a similar foot/body problem saying what actually fits.
- **Comfort translation:** Use practical, specific language: half size up, wider through the instep, swollen by the end of the day, hard uppers not working, dressy vs functional tradeoff.
- **Representative sources:** Living with Rheumatoid Arthritis, Sturdy Woman, lymphedema shoe journey videos.

## 4. Creative Rules

### Lead With

- "My shoes fit in the morning, but squeeze by the afternoon."
- "A regular wide shoe still does not help if the top of your foot swells."
- "My foot should not have to fight its way into a shoe."
- "I bought these because my mom's swollen feet stopped fitting regular shoes."
- "$175 specialist shoes still hurt after the grocery store."
- "More cushion is not the same as more room."
- "If bending, tying, or forcing your foot in hurts, the shoe has already failed."

### Show

- Regular shoe squeezing or failing before Comfort appears.
- Hands opening the shoe wide before the foot enters.
- Stretch or adjustable closure around the top and sides of the foot.
- Morning fit vs later-day swelling adjustment.
- Close-up of instep room, toe room, soft interior, removable insole, and non-slip sole if true to product.
- Older buyer, caregiver-assisted dressing, hallway walking, grocery/doctor errand, or safe step test.
- Real feet and real shoes. This persona needs tactile proof.

### Avoid

- "Treats," "cures," "eliminates," "improves circulation," "reduces edema," or "relieves arthritis" unless compliance-approved and substantiated.
- Fake doctor, fake podiatrist, or "podiatrist approved" language without a real disclosed endorsement.
- Overloaded medical keyword lines: diabetic, arthritis, edema, neuropathy, bunion, barefoot, zero-drop all stacked together.
- Generic orthopedic top-10 listicle framing.
- Pure product-feature piles without a lived problem.
- AI-generated feet, fake walking shots, or any visual that makes the product feel less inspectable.

## 5. Best Tests

1. **Evening Swelling Fit Test**
   - **Hook shape:** "If your feet swell by 3pm, a regular wide shoe can still be wrong."
   - **Why it fits:** Edema and adaptive-footwear sources repeatedly emphasize changing volume, instep tightness, and adjustment during the day.

2. **Open-Wide Entry / No Squeeze Demo**
   - **Hook shape:** "Your foot should not have to fight its way into a shoe."
   - **Why it fits:** The strongest repeated visual is the shoe opening before the foot goes in, then closing around the foot without pressure.

3. **$175 Specialist Shoe Failed**
   - **Hook shape:** "My specialist shoes cost $175 and still hurt after errands."
   - **Why it fits:** Comfort's own review language matches the search/feed lane where people are tired of expensive medical-looking shoes that still do not solve the daily problem.

4. **Caregiver Safety Buyer**
   - **Hook shape:** "I bought these because my mom's swollen feet stopped fitting regular shoes."
   - **Why it fits:** Caregiver language appears in Comfort reviews, senior-care reviews, and Reddit threads. It naturally supports non-slip, easy-on, and no-squeeze proof.

5. **Pressure Vs Cushion**
   - **Hook shape:** "More cushion does not fix a shoe that squeezes the top of your foot."
   - **Why it fits:** Lets Comfort borrow the authority/myth format while staying focused on fit mechanics instead of medical treatment.

6. **Adjustable Through The Day**
   - **Hook shape:** "One shoe fit, two swelling moments."
   - **Why it fits:** The category repeatedly frames swelling as variable. The ad can show the same shoe adapting at home in the morning and after an errand later.

## 6. Organic Vs Paid Tension

- **Organic feed:** Slower, more practical, and often expert-led. Viewers tolerate longer explanations because they are trying to solve a real condition/frustration.
- **Paid direction:** Can open sharper, but the body must slow down enough to prove the mechanism.
- **Tension:** A generic 15-second comfort ad will not carry enough proof. A long doctor explainer will not work as a paid ad by itself.
- **Opportunity:** Open with a tight lived problem, then immediately show physical proof: open, foot enters, adjust, no squeeze, safe walk.
