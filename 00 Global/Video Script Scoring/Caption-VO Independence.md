# Caption-VO Independence

Scores how well on-screen captions and voiceover work as two independent channels throughout the entire script — hooks, body, and close.

---

## What This Scores

Captions and VO are two separate hooks on two channels (sound-on vs sound-off). This is the single most persistent quality issue across all brands and batches. The caption must create its own message, never repeat the VO verbatim, never compress the VO into fewer words, and never carry the same psychological payoff in different words. A viewer watching on mute should get a complete, independent experience from captions alone. This applies to hooks, body, AND close — not just hooks.

## Calibration Table

| Score Range | Description |
|---|---|
| 90-100 | Every hook caption uses a different core type from its VO. Body captions add information the VO doesn't cover (e.g., VO explains mechanism while caption shows stat or social proof). Close caption stacks proof points while VO delivers emotional beat. No verbatim overlap, no angle overlap, no compression anywhere in the script. |
| 70-89 | Hooks avoid verbatim but share the same angle (different words, same payoff). OR: body captions are functional subtitles — they track the VO's message instead of adding a second layer. OR: close caption just restates the CTA instead of stacking proof. |
| 50-69 | Near-verbatim overlap in 1-2 hooks. Body captions read as subtitles. Viewer gets the same message on both channels. |
| Below 50 | Verbatim repetition in multiple hooks. Captions are subtitles throughout. Two channels doing one job. |

---

## Instant-Fail Triggers

- **Verbatim VO-caption match in any hook.** Auto-score 0 for that hook, which caps the dimension at 59.
- **All three hook captions share the same core type as their respective VOs.** Systematic same-type pairing = two-channel strategy not used. Auto-caps at 69.

---

## Scoring Checklist

- **Hook A/B/C core types:** Identify the core type of the VO and caption for each hook per Headline & Text Hook Criteria. Are they different types?
- **Hook mute test:** Remove the VO. Does each caption create its own curiosity gap or land its own payoff independently?
- **Hook felt-experience test:** Does each caption name something the viewer FEELS, not just something technically true?
- **Body caption independence:** Do body captions add information the VO doesn't cover? (Stats, social proof, feature pills, objection handling — while VO carries the narrative/emotional thread.)
- **Close caption stacking:** Does the close caption stack proof points (customer count, ratings, guarantee, certifications) while VO delivers the emotional close?
- **Price-forward concept check:** If the concept IS the price, are the two channels carrying different aspects of the price story? (e.g., VO reframes the price while caption shows the payment breakdown — not both naming "$58/week.")
- **Compression check:** Does any caption shorten the VO into fewer words without changing the angle?
- **Multi-card formatting:** Multi-card captions separated with `<br><br>`, never " - " dash.
- **Failed/working alternatives:** Correct symbols and formatting per Video Script Criteria.

---

## Feedback Format

When scoring below 90:
- The specific hook variation (A/B/C), body beat, or close section that fails
- The VO text and caption text side by side
- Which core type each uses (per Headline & Text Hook Criteria) and why they overlap
- A concrete rewrite of the caption using a different core type, with the new type named
