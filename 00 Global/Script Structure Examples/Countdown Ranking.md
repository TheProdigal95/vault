# Script Structure: Countdown Ranking

Numbered 5-to-1 (or 4-to-1) ranking where a creator physically holds up each competitor, delivers a one-line failure with a time-stamped knock, and drops or dismisses it. Persistent verdict captions (red X) accumulate on screen. Product revealed as #1 with a green check.

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Collection reveal hook → numbered competitor teardown (worst to best) → product as #1 → brief proof beat → close.
- **VO register:** Female creator, warm but urgent, "I've been there" energy. Frustrated during failures, instant brightening at product reveal.
- **Time to benefit:** ~40s. Most of the body is competitor teardown; product proof is compressed.
- **Rhythm:** 7-10s per competitor. Hold-up → one-line knock → drop → next. Visual gesture consistent across all competitors.
- **Duration:** 75-90s.
- **Close type:** Social Proof (reviews + customer count).
- **Visual grammar:** Creator holds each shoe using a consistent gesture (hold up, turn sole to camera, drop/dismiss). Persistent red X verdict card stays on screen per competitor segment. Green check at product reveal. Phone-quality, no color grading.
- **Hook defaults:** Collection reveal — creator holds up 5 shoe boxes rapidly. "$500+ spent" money-waste framing. Or foot massage / swollen feet TK clip as pattern interrupt.
- **Caption strategy:** Persistent verdict captions. Bold numbered headers per competitor (**#5 CR0CS**, **#4 SK3CHERS**). ❌ for failures, ✅ for product.

### When to Use
- Physical products with 4-5 identifiable competitors the buyer has cycled through
- When the "$X spent and nothing worked" framing is relatable
- Categories where progressive failure (hour 3, hour 6, hour 8) creates a compelling cascade

### Key Rules
- Each competitor gets a unique time-stamped failure — never the same knock twice
- The product is NOT dropped — it's set down gently, tried on, or held with care. Visual contrast with the dismissed competitors.
- Max 1 CGI insert (3-5s) for a mechanism visualization. Physical demos preferred.
- The gesture must be consistent: same hold-up, turn, drop motion for every competitor. The repetition IS the format.
