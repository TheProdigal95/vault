## Patterns

### 1. The Efficiency Hierarchy

| Tier | Ads | Avg ROAS | Avg AOV | Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Converters** | Crossout (0.92), QuoteDream (0.67), Iceberg (0.62), $1.37 Background (0.38), Tony Sale (0.36), Tony HRT (0.28) | 0.54 | $579 | Mixed |
| **Awareness engines** | 1 in 3 Men (0.06), Drained to Driven (0.02), Tony Core (0.00), Before/After (0.00) | 0.02 | $56 | TRT-focused |
| **Zero converters** | Side by Side, We're Sorry, Ashley P, Andrea UGC, Tony Effective, Sara UGC, QuoteGoal, Sale Phone | 0.00 | — | Mixed |

The top converters share one trait: **they give the viewer a logical framework for making a decision** (comparison, reframe, metaphor). The awareness engines get attention but provide no decision mechanism. The zero converters are either quote-only ads, feature walkthroughs, or formats with funnel mismatch.

### 2. Comparison Format Dominance

The three comparison-style ads outperform everything:

| Ad | Mechanism | ROAS | AOV |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Crossout** (internet → bloodwork) | Behavior reframe | 0.92 | $324 |
| **Iceberg** (they track vs we track) | Visual metaphor | 0.62 | $599 |
| **$1.37 Background** (25 cents/day more) | Price differential | 0.38 | $669 |

These are the only ads consistently driving membership-level AOVs ($324-$669). They work because they target people who are already evaluating options — the comparison helps them choose Lifeforce. Every other format is trying to create awareness or desire, but these formats close.

### 3. Tony Quote Ads: Only Work With Offers

| Ad | Quote | Paired With | ROAS |
|---|---|---|---|
| QuoteDream | "A million dreams" | FREE blood test + 4 features | 0.67 |
| QuoteGoal | "Affordable and accessible" | FREE blood test + 4 features | 0.00 |
| Effective Health Solution | "Most effective solutions" | Nothing | 0.00 |

The format is identical. The features are identical. The only variable is the quote — and "a million dreams" is an order of magnitude more powerful than the other two. The lesson: Tony quote ads only convert when the quote creates an emotional shift. Brand-speak quotes ("affordable and accessible," "most effective solutions") are dead on arrival.

### 4. The Women's Creative Gap

Three ads target women/moms: We're Sorry (1.93% CTR, 0 purchases), Sara UGC (1.26% CTR, 0 purchases), and the extras (You book 47, Mom C1). Combined: $2,559 spend, 0 purchases.

The creative is strong — the "We're Sorry" letter has the highest CTR among all non-UGC image ads. Sara's "14 markers" hook is the most specific medical narrative in the library. These aren't failing creatively. They're failing structurally: the Lifeforce funnel is built for men and TRT. Women clicking these ads likely land on pages optimized for testosterone, not hormonal panels for post-pregnancy recovery. This is a funnel problem, not a creative problem.

### 5. Short Silent Videos: Attention Without Conversion

| Ad | Length | VO | CTR | ROAS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 in 3 Men Stat Overlay | 7s | None | 1.33% | 0.06 |
| Before & After | 16s | None | 1.46% | 0.00 |
| Problem Solution Pop Up | 9s | None | N/A | N/A |

All three are silent, autoplay-optimized, under 20 seconds. They perform like social-native content but convert like ambient noise. The missing element: narrative. Without VO, there's no story arc, no objection handling, no mechanism explanation. These formats can SHOW the problem and SHOW the result, but can't EXPLAIN why Lifeforce bridges the gap. They need to be used as top-of-funnel feed-stoppers paired with retargeting creative that does the explaining.

### 6. The Per-Day Pricing Effect

Two ads use per-day pricing: $1.37 Background (0.38 ROAS) and We're Sorry ($1.37/day). The Side by Side comparison uses annual pricing ($100 difference) and has 0.00 ROAS. Same comparison framework. Same premium positioning. Dramatically different results. Per-day pricing makes the premium feel negligible. Annual pricing makes it feel real.

### 7. UGC Structure: Pain-First vs Feature-First

| Creator | Hook | Structure | ROAS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sara | "14 markers, 6 things wrong" | Pain → medical dismissal → specific markers → resolution | 0.00 (but only $811 spend) |
| Andrea | "Changed my expectations" | Claim → feature walkthrough → close | 0.00 ($1,150 spend) |

Sara's script is structurally superior. It opens with specific pain ("14 markers... 6 things wrong"), names the enemy ("Your blood work looks normal"), lists the missing tests by name (estrogen, progesterone, cortisol), and resolves through identity ("the mom I've always wanted to be"). Andrea's script is a polished feature tour with no emotional tension. For the male audience, there are ZERO UGC testimonials — this is the biggest creative gap.

## T003 Implications

### What to Scale (Existing)

1. **Crossout - Supps + Bloodwork** — 0.92 ROAS at only $1.4K spend. This is the single most under-tested winner. Give it 5-10x budget immediately.
2. **$1.37 Background** — 0.38 ROAS, $669 AOV. Already spending ($8.7K) but room to scale further. The per-day pricing mechanism is proven.
3. **QuoteDream** — 0.67 ROAS, $1,448 AOV at low spend. Test whether this scales or was a one-buyer fluke.
4. **Iceberg** — 0.62 ROAS, $599 AOV. The educational metaphor drives membership-level purchases. Test more spend.

### What to Create (New for T003)

1. **More crossout/reframe ads.** The "~~internet~~ my bloodwork" mechanism is the best performer. Create variants: "~~my supplement stack~~ my biomarker data," "~~Dr. Google~~ a real clinician," "~~TRT clinics~~ full hormone optimization."

2. **Comparison ads with per-day pricing.** The $1.37 Background format works. Create new comparisons: Lifeforce vs. standard doctor visits, Lifeforce vs. supplement stacks, Lifeforce vs. TRT-only clinics. Always use per-day pricing, never annual.

3. **Male UGC testimonials.** Zero exist in the current library. Use Sara's pain-first structure: specific medical dismissal → named biomarkers → transformation. Target: man told his testosterone was "normal" at 350, Lifeforce found it plus cortisol, vitamin D, thyroid issues.

4. **Sara-style scripts for TRT audience.** Adapt the "14 markers vs 50+" hook for men: "My doctor ran a testosterone test and said I was fine at 380. Lifeforce tested 50+ markers and found my free testosterone, DHEA, cortisol, and thyroid were all off."

5. **Educational metaphor ads.** The Iceberg works because it teaches, not sells. Create more: the "tip of the iceberg" concept applied to different categories (supplements, standard blood panels, gym performance).

6. **Silent split-screen + VO hybrid.** Take the Before/After visual format (strong) and layer on Tony's 225→700 narrative (strong). The combination would have both the visual punch AND the mechanism explanation.

### What to Fix

1. **Tony Core ($5.7K, 0 ROAS)** — Change end card from "Become a Member" to "$49 test" CTA. The membership ask is too high-commitment for cold traffic.
2. **Side by Side ($2.1K, 0 ROAS)** — Switch from annual pricing ("$100") to per-day pricing ("27 cents"). Same creative, same comparison, different framing.
3. **Women's funnel** — The mom creative (We're Sorry, Sara, You book 47) is generating engagement but 0 purchases. Build or verify a women's-specific landing page before creating more women's creative.

### What to Kill

1. **Tony Effective Health Solution** — Quote-only with no offer or features. 0 conversions. Remove from rotation.
2. **QuoteGoal** — Identical format to QuoteDream but with a generic quote. 0 conversions. Remove.
3. **Before & After (silent)** — Strong visual concept but no conversion mechanism. Either add VO or repurpose as a retargeting-only asset.
