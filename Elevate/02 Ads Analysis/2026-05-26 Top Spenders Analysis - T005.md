## Per-Ad Breakdowns

### #1 — This Failed You (Split Screen)

**$12,040 spend | 15 purchases | $803 CPP | 0.69 ROAS**

**Layout:** 9:16 vertical, pure typographic — no photography, no product, no people. Vertical white divider line splits two headline blocks. Top ~50% headline zone, middle sub-headline + CTA, bottom white footer with payment logos.

**Imagery:** None. Deliberate choice — the creative is 100% about the financial access message. Background is a smooth vertical gradient from saturated sky blue (#0088FF) at top to lighter cyan/ice blue (#A8E0F0) at bottom, meeting a solid white footer. "Clear sky" / optimism after frustration.

**Text on image:**
- **Headline left (faded, serif, ~60% opacity):** "Your Insurance Failed You" — right-aligned to center divider
- **Headline right (solid, sans-serif bold, 100% opacity):** "Our Payment Plans Won't" — left-aligned from center divider
- **Sub-headline:** "$0 DOWN. SHIPS IN 3-7 Days." — white, centered, ALL CAPS
- **CTA:** "Medical Weight Loss →" — dark text in white pill button with soft drop shadow
- **Payment badges:** Affirm, Klarna (pink badge), Afterpay, HSA/FSA Eligible — horizontal row in white footer
- **Brand logo:** Elevate Health, bottom-left, small and subordinate
- **Disclaimer:** Standard Rx + lending disclosure, tiny gray text

**Design:** The typography IS the creative. The opacity contrast between left ("failed" = faded) and right ("won't" = solid) does the emotional work that photography would normally do. Serif vs sans-serif reinforces the old/broken vs modern/reliable split. The vertical white divider line is the structural spine — it's the "before | after" metaphor. Extremely stripped design — no borders, no decorative elements, no icons beyond logos.

**Why it converts:** Textbook Pain-Agitate-Solve compressed into one headline. The enemy is the insurance system, not the viewer's weight. This reframes the purchase from "Do I deserve this?" to "I was wronged, here's my workaround." Targets solution-aware viewers who already want GLP-1s but got denied or priced out. The ad meets them at the exact friction point. Processing fluency is maximal — one idea, one frame, under 2 seconds to process. Footer logos (Affirm/Klarna/Afterpay/HSA-FSA) create a wall of credibility without a single testimonial. $0 DOWN + SHIPS IN 3-7 Days triggers temporal discounting — the reward is this week, not abstract.

### #10 — Eligibility Payment Logos (T002)

**$986 spend | 4 purchases | $247 CPP | 1.51 ROAS**

**Layout:** 9:16 vertical, three-zone stack — headline top (~35%), hero photo + price badge middle (~40%), objection handling + CTA bottom (~25%).

**Imagery:** Woman ~40-55, realistic body type (not a model), calm/hopeful expression, gazing up and off-camera (aspirational direction). Dark teal athletic tank, studio-lit, soft diffused key light. She looks like the target customer.

**Text on image:**
- **Headline:** "MEDICAL WEIGHT LOSS COULD BE AN OPTION. MOST PEOPLE DON'T REALISE IT." — white, extra bold, ALL CAPS, spans ~90% width
- **Price badge:** "From $58/week" — black text inside bright yellow organic splat shape with salmon drop-shadow
- **Subhead:** "$0 down. No insurance needed." — white, semi-bold
- **CTA:** "SEE IF YOU QUALIFY" — white text in salmon pill button
- **Payment badges:** Affirm, Afterpay, Klarna, HSA/FSA Eligible — white rounded pills, horizontal row

**Design:** Teal gradient background (bright top → darker bottom). Brand logo (stylized plus/S curve) top-center. Disclaimer in low-contrast teal-on-teal at bottom.

**Why it converts:** Information gap headline ("most people don't realise it") + eligibility reframe (it's an *option* for you) → aspirational identification (real-looking woman in target demo) → price chunking ($58/week vs monthly) → systematic objection demolition ($0 down, no insurance, payment plans, HSA/FSA) → low-friction CTA (checking eligibility, not committing). Every element answers the next objection in sequence.

### #27 — Real People Real Results V2

**$378 spend | 1 purchase | $378 CPP | 3.97 ROAS**

**Layout:** 9:16 vertical, pure typographic — zero photography, zero product shots, zero people. Massive hero price text dominates top ~55%, supporting copy middle ~20%, CTA + brand footer bottom ~25%.

**Imagery:** None. Background is a deep navy-blue to blue-purple gradient with subtle film grain texture. The grain adds editorial/premium feel and prevents the flat-color look of cheap graphics. No lifestyle imagery, no models — the price IS the hero.

**Text on image:**
- **Hero price:** "$8" — enormous white text, ~60% of frame height. The dollar sign and number are the single dominant visual element
- **Sub-price:** "/Day. That's It." — white, bold, large but subordinate to the $8. Period after each word adds weight/finality
- **Supporting copy:** "Medical weight loss for less than a lunch. $0 upfront." — white, medium weight, centered. Two sentences, two price reframes
- **CTA:** "See If You're Eligible →" — white text, no button/pill, just underlined text-link style with arrow. Minimal, editorial
- **Brand logo:** Elevate Health, bottom-left, small teal icon + white text
- **Disclaimer:** Standard Rx disclaimer, tiny white text at bottom

**Design:** Extreme minimalism. The $8 fills the frame like a billboard — impossible to scroll past without registering the price. Film grain on the gradient prevents it from looking like a Canva template. No button on the CTA is deliberate — it reads like editorial content, not an ad. The "/Day. That's It." construction with periods after each word creates a rhythmic stop that emphasizes simplicity.

**Why it converts:** Pure price minimization through framing effect — $8/day reframes what's likely $240/month into lunch money. "That's It." shuts down the "what's the catch?" objection before it forms. "$0 upfront" eliminates the barrier to starting. The eligibility CTA maintains the low-commitment frame (checking, not buying). At $378 CPP with 3.97 ROAS on low spend, this is the most efficient converter in the top 20 — it just hasn't gotten scale yet. The text-only format is infinitely iterable: swap the price frame, swap the comparison anchor, same template.

## Patterns

**What converts (L7 purchases):**
- Financial accessibility is the only angle that converts at scale. 90% of spend, all purchases except Shame to Pride's 2. Insurance denial is a sub-angle within this — "your insurance failed you" is just the sharpest version of the financial access message.
- Every converting static has payment logos (Affirm/Klarna/Afterpay/HSA-FSA) + $0 down + price chunking ($8/day or $58/week). This is the non-negotiable conversion kit.
- Eligibility framing ("See if you qualify," "Most people don't realise it's an option") outperforms direct purchase CTAs. Low-commitment entry point.
- Pure typographic statics (no photography) are the highest-converting format: This Failed You ($803 CPP at $12K spend) and Real People Results V2 ($378 CPP, 3.97 ROAS). They're also the cheapest to produce and iterate.
- The one converting video (Shame to Pride Selfie Walk) works through a completely different mechanism: emotional entry (vulnerability hook) + inspirational IG audio driving completion + text wall maintaining reading engagement + shame-to-pride arc resolving into the offer. Hold 79, Thruplay 79% — nothing else comes close.

**What gains spend but doesn't convert:**
- Whitelist native photos (Maria Desk, Maria Lunch) get massive CTR (23-29%) and spend ($11K+, $2.6K, $1.3K) but zero purchases. High engagement ≠ purchase intent. Already killed.
- Lifestyle/experience imagery (Summer Bod, City Poster) gets moderate spend but no conversions. The aspirational framing without a financial access hook doesn't close.
- VoC Frosted Glass gets spend ($5.5K) with poor efficiency ($2,749 CPP). The format works for attention but not conversion without the payment/eligibility layer on top.

**Format signals:**
- Split screen / "us vs them" typography — proven spend getter + converter (This Failed You is the #1 ad in the account)
- Three-zone stack with real person + payment logos — best CPP ($247) on lower spend (Eligibility Payment Logos)
- Selfie walk with inspirational audio — only video format that converts; retention curve barely drops through 50%
- Pure price-hero typographic — highest ROAS (3.97) on small spend, untested at scale

**What's missing:**
- No video converts at scale. Shame to Pride is efficient ($301 CPP, 3.07 ROAS) but at only $602 spend. The video side of the account needs a scalable winner.
- No converting creative uses health/wellness angles (holistic health, craving control, food noise). These are sub-$1K spend with zero purchases. The audience buys on price/access, not health transformation messaging.
- The Insurance Testimonial (broader: $15.7K spend, 13 purchases) shows real UGC testimonials with overlay templates can work, but at $1,208 CPP it's a spend vehicle, not an efficiency play.

## Implications for T005

**Video (Part 1 — 15 scripts, delegated):**
- Scale the selfie walk format with different emotional entry points. The structure is proven (inspirational IG audio + text wall + aspirational b-roll + shame-to-pride arc). What varies: the opening vulnerability ("I was so nervous" → other emotional hooks), the specific fear addressed, and the transformation moment.
- Bring the blame/insurance framing into video. "This Failed You" is the #1 static — the anger-at-the-system narrative hasn't been tested in video yet. Price comparison narratives (us vs insurance, us vs other weight loss) are natural extensions.
- Financial accessibility angle on every script. 90% of account spend is here. No script should exist without a price point, payment plan mention, or eligibility CTA.
- Overlay template from Insurance Testimonial is replicable — pink/red banners + payment logo grid appearing instantly over any talking-head footage. Even with AI-generated UGC, the overlay-heavy format could maintain credibility.

**Statics (Part 2 — AI-generated, in-house):**
- Iterate on "This Failed You" split screen — new headline pairings on the same typographic format. Different blame targets (insurance, diet industry, willpower myth), same us-vs-them structure.
- Iterate on the price-hero format (Real People Results V2) — $8/day is the current frame. Test other price comparisons: less than a coffee, less than your gym membership, less than what insurance charges. Same minimalist gradient + film grain template.
- Eligibility + payment logos format — test new information-gap headlines with the same three-zone stack layout. The $247 CPP proves the conversion mechanism; it needs more headline variations to find a scalable winner.
- Skip non-converting formats: no frosted glass without payment overlay, no lifestyle/experience imagery without financial access hook, no native photo whitelist.
