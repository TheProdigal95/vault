## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

Full analysis: [[2026-06-10 Top Spenders Analysis - T006]]

### What the L14 data says (Jun 10, 2026)

- **Financial Accessibility = dominant converter.** 7 of 14 creatives with CPA $50-$850 lean on payment access as the core messaging theme. Price objection is the #1 barrier and the best ads address it head-on.
- **Statics are crushing video.** 13 of 14 converters are images; only 1 video converts (Shame to Pride Selfie Walk at $219 CPA, 1 purchase). The account converts on clear, readable static formats where the message is legible in 1-2 seconds.
- **The Whitelist testimonial batch is a disaster.** David (grill, receipt, pharmacy, denial, backyard) + Maria (hallway V2/V3, shoes, morning, lunch) from late May have spent ~$14,856 combined, all with 18-25% CTR, zero conversions. High engagement without conversion — the problem is the click-to-close gap, not attention.
- **"Am I Eligible?" Search Bar is the most reliable converter** ($651 spend, 3 purchases, $217 CPA, 2.73 ROAS). It works because it's direct and clear — a Google search mockup that says "affordable weight loss" and includes payment logos. Low creative friction, high processing fluency.
- **Payment Plan AMA is the highest-spend converter** ($1,306 spend, 2 purchases, $653 CPA, 0.90 ROAS). Financial Accessibility headline, always-on offer, budget-conscious audience. Reliable but not efficient — needs iteration to improve efficiency.
- **The emotional arc is underexploited in statics.** Shame to Pride Selfie Walk works because it goes from scared/nervous/ashamed → confident/proud/wanting to tell everyone. That arc hasn't been fully replicated in static formats.
- **City Poster Access works because of the food-noise visualization** — food swirling across the guy's face, grimy/city texture that feels real, plus Affirm/Klarna logos. It depicts the problem (food noise) and offers the medical solution (price access) in one image.
- **"This Failed You" split screen converts** ($523, 1 purchase, $523 CPA, 2.29 ROAS) — "diets failed you but medicine won't" is strong messaging that hasn't been fully explored in this batch.

### Key insights from strategizing

- The account thrives on **direct, clear messaging**. Google search mockups work because they literally show someone searching "affordable weight loss." A girl in a car with coffee works because she looks happy and at ease. No clever metaphors required.
- The **emotional arc** (from shame/fear/skepticism → confidence/pride/freedom) is the connective tissue that makes the selfie walk work. The best statics hint at this arc visually.
- **Food noise as the visible problem** + **medicine as the affordable solution** — this is the core pairing. The City Poster visualizes food noise; most other ads don't depict the problem, only the solution.
- The gap: **"Diets failed you but medicine won't"** — this message combines emotional validation (you tried, it wasn't your fault) + reframing (this isn't willpower, it's medicine) + affordability ($58/week with payment plans). This is the batch's strategic spine.

### Dead angles (don't test)

- ❌ Whitelist native photo format (high CTR, high engagement, zero conversion — confirmed again in T006 L14 data, ~$14.8K burned across David + Maria variants)
- ❌ Kids/Longevity as primary hook (dead since T004)
- ❌ Education/mechanism videos (dead since T004)
- ❌ Aspirational/POV dancing (dead since T004)
- ❌ Holistic health improvement (declining, no efficiency)
- ❌ Craving control / food noise as PRIMARY angle (sub-$1K as a standalone). Food noise works as a visual element in the problem depiction, but not as the hook.

**Primary: Cost-Conscious Seeker** — Every converting creative targets someone who wants GLP-1s but can access through insurance/cost. The emotional arc and affordability messaging are for this person.

**Secondary: GLP-1 Continuer** — Insurance stopped covering their branded medication. "Diets failed you, insurance failed you, but medicine won't" targets them.

### Personas NOT in T006

- Postpartum/Life-Change haven't appeared in converting creatives.
- Skeptic hasn't appeared in converting creatives.

## Batch Setup

**Product:** Elevate Health Medical Weight Loss
**Product page:** [joinelevate.com](https://joinelevate.com)
**Platform:** Meta
**Personas:** Cost-Conscious Seeker (primary), GLP-1 Continuer (secondary)
**Core archetype:** The person who tried everything, felt like food was always louder than their discipline, and discovered that the problem was never willpower — it was a biology that medicine can help with, and the care plan is genuinely affordable.
**Strategic spine:** "Your diets, supplements, and willpower failed you. Our payment plans won't." Blame framing + emotional arc + food noise visualization + financial accessibility — all compliant with Elevate's compounded GLP-1 marketing guidelines.

### Compliance guardrails (read before every brief/script)

Full doc: [[Compliance - Marketing Rules]]

- **No body claims.** No "what happens to your body" framing, no weight numbers, no percentages, no time-based outcome promises.
- **No before/after imagery.** Visual before↔after transformation imagery is banned. Emotional transformation must be told through sequential captions/text, not paired before-state / after-state photos.
- **No drug branding.** No Wegovy, Ozempic, Mounjaro, Zepbound, "GLP-1" as a product name. "Medical weight loss" as a category is fine.
- **No "injection"** in copy, VO, or captions — Meta auto-flags the word even without visual triggers.
- **Blame framing is compliant when the blame target is a system/access barrier**, not when it's a medical claim. "Your diets failed you" = approved pain point (diet cycle fatigue). "Our payment plans won't" = access/payment promise. "Medicine won't" = borderline — avoid, reframe to payment plans or care access.
- **Progression timelines must be emotional milestones** (ashamed → hiding it → felt a shift → wanted to tell everyone), not physical results milestones.
- **Every brief must pass the pre-launch checklist** in the compliance doc before it ships.

## Batch Plan

### Allocation reasoning

- **Static-first (~13-15 concepts)** because statics are where this account has conversion proof. The highest-spend converter (Payment Plan AMA, $1,306 / 2 purchases) is static. The most reliable converter (Search Bar, $651 / 3 purchases) is static. Statics are also faster to produce in-house — no talent, no shooting, no creator dependencies. The strategic spine of blame framing, food-noise visualization, and search UI mockups all work best in static format.
- **Video second (~8-11 scripts)** because video is the gap this account needs to close. The only video that converts in the last 4 batches is Shame to Pride Selfie Walk (79% hold, 79% Thruplay, 1 purchase in L14 at $521). It needs volume and new emotional entry points. Insurance Testimonial UGC emerged this window ($2,880, 2 purchases) — a second video signal worth testing. Everything here has to be in formats that don't require cast talent (selfie walk, text-wall overlays, progression timelines).
