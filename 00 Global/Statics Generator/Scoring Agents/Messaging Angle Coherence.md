# Messaging Angle Coherence

Scores whether copy delivers the specified messaging angle's core truth without dilution or drift.

---

## What This Dimension Evaluates

A messaging angle is the core truth for a specific pain x persona intersection. It is conversational, human, and specific — not a tagline, not a feature list. The copy must deliver THIS angle's truth, not a diluted version that tries to cover adjacent angles. A single ad serves one angle. Dilution across multiple angles makes none of them land.

## Scoring Criteria

### What earns points

1. **Core truth comes through clearly** — a viewer could articulate after reading the ad what single truth it communicates, and it matches the assigned angle
2. **Angle-specific language** — the copy uses language tied to THIS angle's core truth, not generic product benefits
3. **Single-angle discipline** — every element (headline, subheading, body, CTA) serves the same angle
4. **Specificity** — the angle is delivered with concrete details, not vague platitudes that could apply to any angle
5. **Natural delivery** — the angle's truth emerges through the copy's narrative, not stated as a thesis

### What loses points

1. **Angle dilution** — the assigned angle is present but the copy also tries to serve 1-2 adjacent angles, weakening all of them
2. **Generic version of the angle** — the topic area is correct but the specific core truth is missing (e.g., angle is "your dermatologist wrecked your skin" but copy just says "natural skincare is better")
3. **Drift mid-copy** — headline serves the correct angle but body drifts to a different pain or truth
4. **Feature-first instead of angle-first** — product features listed without connecting them to the angle's core truth
5. **Angle stated but not felt** — the angle's words appear but the copy doesn't make the viewer feel the truth (listing vs. storytelling)

## Calibration

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 60 | Angle topic present but diluted across multiple angles. The general territory is right, but the copy tries to cover too much ground. | Angle is "melatonin stopped working three months ago" but copy covers sleep hygiene tips, stress management, AND melatonin tolerance — three angles in one ad |
| 80 | Angle is clear and identifiable, minor drift. A viewer would get the right message, but one element (usually the body or CTA) wobbles toward an adjacent angle. | Angle is "melatonin stopped working" — headline and body deliver this truth, but the close drifts into "better than every sleep supplement" (Alternative Callout angle territory) |
| 95 | Single angle delivered with specificity and discipline. No audience member would confuse which angle this ad serves. Every element reinforces the same core truth. | Angle is "melatonin stopped working" — headline names the tolerance problem, body explains WHY it stops working (mechanism for this specific angle), CTA invites the viewer to solve THIS problem specifically |

---

## Instant Fail (score capped at 59)

- **Copy serves a different angle entirely than what was assigned.** If the batch plan assigns "your dermatologist wrecked your skin" and the copy delivers "natural ingredients are safer than prescriptions," that's a different angle. The assignment was about the specific betrayal experience, not the general natural-vs-prescription debate.

---

## Feedback Format

When score is below 90, feedback must include:
1. The assigned angle's core truth (from the angle dump or batch plan)
2. What angle the copy actually delivers, and where the drift occurs
3. Which specific lines need to change and what they should say to re-center on the assigned angle's core truth
