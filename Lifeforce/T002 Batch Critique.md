### Resolved Issues

All of the following were identified, critiqued, and fixed in the briefs:
- Tweet copy trimmed to 35-50 words (was 48-93)
- Profanity removed
- False dichotomies ("That's not X. That's Y.") removed
- "It's not X" lists removed
- Broken sentence in Ad 2 fixed
- Primary texts rewritten in third person (was first person — ads run from company page)
- Paragraphs broken for scannability (avg 17-23 words/para, was 35-57)
- Price given own line in all ads (Ad 11 was missing price — added)
- All written-out prices converted to numerals ($49, $149/month, etc.)
- Word counts reduced to 615-787 range (was 725-1,041)
- ApoB testimonial made exclusive to Ad 14 (was appearing in 5 ads)
- Analogy overload reduced to max 1 per ad (was 2-3 per ad)
- AI-detection signals fixed: staccato closes, tagline closes, "let me" openings, "here's the thing" overuse
- Tweet variation similarity fixed in Ads 2, 7, 13 (each now has a structurally different variation)
- Tweet tone pass for casual/real-tweet energy

### Notes for Future Batches

- **Primary text openings converge on fear+stat.** Ads 1, 4, 7, and 13 all open with a version of "here's a scary health stat." Not a problem for this batch since viewers won't see all 15, but future batches should lean into the non-statistical openings that are actually stronger: empathy (Ad 4), humor (Ad 8), verbatim doctor quotes (Ad 11), dramatic tension (Ad 14).
- **Three "woman found it for him" discovery mechanisms.** Wife (#4), sister (#11), wife's friend (#14). Realistic and fine for this batch, but future batches should include discovery mechanisms where the man finds it himself.
- **UGC variety gap remains open.** This batch is entirely image ads. A companion video batch with 3-5 UGC testimonials using these same stories would test whether the narratives convert in video.
- **Supplement-specific positioning gap remains open.** No ad sells a specific Lifeforce supplement product — they all sell the testing/clinician service. Future batch should test individual supplement positioning.
- **Younger male persona (28-32) not represented.** Every male persona is 36-59. Future batch should test "optimization" framing (vs "decline" framing) for younger men.
