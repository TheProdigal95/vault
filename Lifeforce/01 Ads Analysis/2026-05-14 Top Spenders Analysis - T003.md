## Key Findings & Data Patterns

### 1. The "Stop Guessing" Winner (Highest ROAS)
The **"Reach - Core - Crossout - Supps + Bloodwork"** ad is the outlier for efficiency (0.92 ROAS). It likely visualizes the cost/inefficiency of a large supplement stack vs. the precision of bloodwork. 
- **T003 Takeaway:** We must adapt this to Peptides. "You're taking Peptides but still guessing your biomarkers" is the strongest efficiency angle we have.

### 2. The High-Volume TRT Trap
The **"1 in 3 Men"** stat overlay continues to spend heavily ($10.6k) but with dismal ROAS (0.06). It stops the scroll but attracts "window shoppers" or people confused by the offer.
- **T003 Takeaway:** Our video rebuild of this (Ad #6 in the plan) needs to introduce the **membership value** much earlier to qualify the audience. We can't just lead with the scary stat; we have to bridge to the *clinical surveillance* earlier.

### 3. High AOV Tony Closers
The **"QuoteDream"** and **"Iceberg"** ads have lower CTRs but significantly higher ROAS and AOV ($1,448 and $599). These are likely "system-focused" creatives that explain the full Lifeforce membership rather than just the $49 test.
- **T003 Takeaway:** For the Peptides batch, we need at least 3 "System Explainer" videos that don't just sell the $49 hook, but the $149/mo "Optimization Stack" (Intervention + Clinician + Retesting).

### 4. Us vs Them Efficiency
The **"$1.37 Background"** (likely pricing-focused) and **"Side by Side"** ads get the highest CTRs (1.68%).
- **T003 Takeaway:** We need a "Peptides vs Supplements" or "Lifeforce vs TRT Clinic" split-screen comparison video.
