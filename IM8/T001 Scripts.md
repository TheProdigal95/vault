## Script #1 — 90-Day Joint & Recovery Timeline

**Persona:** Fitness Enthusiast / Athlete
**Structure:** Progression timeline
**Duration:** ~49 seconds
**Word count:** ~122 words (VO at ~149 wpm)
**Caption style:** Ad 01 style: bold sans-serif body + stylized milestone cards on dark red bg. Yellow text for milestone markers ("After 9 Days").

### Concept

The viewer trains hard but recovers slow. Joints ache longer than they used to. This ad borrows Ad 01's temporal discounting structure — escalating milestones from first sip to Day 90 — but compresses it to 50 seconds and names the specific ingredient responsible at each stage. IM8 appears in the hook (as Ad 01 does), paired with a curiosity gap and aspirational gym visuals. The timeline makes transformation feel inevitable: first sip eases discomfort, Day 9 loosens joints, Day 21 rebuilds tissue, Day 90 transforms mobility.

| Proof barrage close | Ad 01 | Compressed to 2-3 proof points: NSF + Beckham + clinical trial. |
| Product in hook | Ad 01 | IM8 named in hook VO (Ad 01 does this). |

## Script #3 — Muscle Recovery Mechanism

**Persona:** Fitness Enthusiast / Athlete
**Structure:** Talking-head creator + CGI
**Duration:** ~48 seconds
**Word count:** ~120 words (VO at ~150 wpm)
**Caption style:** Ad 02 style: two-tier — sans-serif narration on dark red blocks + bold SERIF all-caps for keyword callouts (ingredient names).

### Concept

The viewer's recovery is getting slower. They blame age. This ad reframes: the problem isn't age, it's elevated cortisol from training destroying muscle overnight. The mechanism is the hook — CGI muscle fibers under strain open the ad as a pattern interrupt. The creator explains HOW cortisol breaks down muscle, how adaptogens reduce it, and how amino acids rebuild. Light temporal discounting only ("overnight recovery" as one beat). Mechanism-first, not timeline-first.
