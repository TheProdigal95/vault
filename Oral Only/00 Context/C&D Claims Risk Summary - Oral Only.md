## Main Allegations in the Letter

The letter alleges Oral Only is unlawfully marketing compounded oral testosterone by making unsubstantiated / misleading claims under the FDCA and FTC Act. It specifically challenges claims around:

- No suppression of natural function / not tied to TRT for life.
- "Significantly fewer side effects" and "side effects will not appear at all."
- Hematocrit / estrogen markers remaining unchanged.
- Mimicking natural testosterone or circadian rhythm.
- Avoiding dangerous spikes or unnatural flatline levels from injections.
- SHBG not spiking / benefits not fading after 60 days / perpetual effectiveness.
- Peer-reviewed research backing Oral Only's specific product when studies may involve other testosterone products or delivery routes.
- 30/60/90-day outcome timelines including stable energy, better sleep quality, improved mental clarity, and full adaptation.
- Liposomal delivery claims that imply proven systemic delivery / bloodstream release.
- Symptom fix lists such as irritability, poor sleep, energy crashes, hormone fluctuations, and feeling off.

## Practical Creative Guardrails — Active

Apply these constraints to Oral Only creative unless the brand provides approved substantiation/language that clears a specific claim. Do not over-hedge by flattening the creative; preserve competitive edge through Dr Jaquish's approved talking points, but avoid the disputed claim buckets below.

Do not write ads that say or imply:

- "No side effects," "side effects won't appear," or "significantly fewer side effects" as a blanket claim.
- "No suppression," "fertility preserved," or "not tied to TRT for life" without approved sourcing/disclaimer language.
- "Hematocrit/estrogen remain unchanged" as a guaranteed outcome.
- "Benefits don't fade," "works forever," "perpetual effectiveness," or similar permanence language.
- 30/60/90-day guaranteed outcomes for energy, sleep, clarity, adaptation, strength, or body composition.
- "Backed by peer-reviewed research" unless the research is explicitly about Oral Only's actual compounded formulation and delivery route.
- Superiority claims vs injections/Kyzatrex/other TRT routes unless the exact comparative claim has approved support.

Safer competitive framing direction:

- Position as a **physician-guided, compounded oral TRT option** for eligible men.
- Use **route/convenience** carefully: no needles, daily oral routine, online care, labs, consult, shipping.
- Use **customer/problem language** around injection fatigue and desire for alternatives, without promising clinical outcomes.
- Include required compounded/not-FDA-approved disclosure where the format allows or where compliance requires.

## Letter Disposition / Required Action

The letter demands Oral Only cease the challenged conduct and respond by June 12, 2026, and states Marius intends to share the letter with FDA, state Boards of Pharmacy, state Attorneys General, and FTC within 7 days after June 12.
