## Winning Format Patterns

Four deep-dived creatives reveal three format archetypes driving the account:

### 1. Authority Rating Format
- Senior Kashif (#3, $19K) and Kyro390v1 (#11, $9K)
- Doctor/podiatrist in scrubs rates 5-6 competitors with specific clinical knocks
- Each competitor gets a unique failure (no two share the same knock)
- Slow authoritative first half → fast proof-stacked second half
- Single-camera talking head with product overlays — no complex production
- Competitor names masked in captions (H\*kas, Sk\*tchers, etc.)

### 2. Creator-Led Contrarian Hook
- COM49 Teacher Travel (#22, $4.6K, 6.58% CTR) and Kyro373 (#1, $52.8K, 6.96% CTR)
- Opens with contrarian statement: "Please don't wear your Skechers to Europe" / "The worst travel advice I ever got"
- Creator on camera with product in hand from frame 1
- Real travel B-roll as proof shots (cobblestones, archways, walking shots)
- Comment stitch UI makes it look like native TikTok
- Two creators, two roles: storyteller + nurse authority for product demo
- Phone-quality throughout — deliberately low production value

### 3. Competitor Countdown (5-to-1 Ranking)
- Spanish Aubrey (#6, $10.6K, 7.72% CTR) and English Aubrey (#13, $7.3K, 6.82% CTR)
- Nurse physically holds up each shoe brand in sequence
- Progressive failure timestamps: hour 3, hour 6, hour 8, never fails
- Each competitor gets a specific clinical knock + price anchor
- Red X for losers, green checkmark for ComfortWear
- Directly adaptable to Spanish — same structure, native VO talent, reuse end card

### Common Traits Across All Winners
- Real footage, not AI-generated B-roll
- Competitor names in the hook (targeting tactic, not just comparison)
- Specific clinical language that earns authority
- Simple production: single camera, phone-quality, no CGI
- Universal close stack: 40% off + 45-day guarantee + free shipping + 500K+ customers + doctor-recommended

## Strategic Implications for T004

### 1. Format Priorities
The account runs three winning format archetypes. T004 should mirror them:
- **Authority rating** (podiatrist/doctor rates competitors) — for senior and nurse angles
- **Creator-led contrarian** (comment stitch, "don't wear X") — for travel angles
- **5-to-1 ranking countdown** — for nurse angles + Spanish adaptations

### 2. Angle Rebalancing
Travel carries the #1 spender and is under-indexed by creative volume. Senior is #3 and #10 — scaling hard. Spanish is #6 and #18 with the highest CTR in the entire set. Our RD creatives are 100% nurse and getting buried.

### 3. Production Style
Every winner uses real footage, not AI B-roll. Single-camera talking heads, phone-quality B-roll, and simple overlays (scores, red X, green check). No CGI mechanism animations. No color-graded pain states. No consistent AI characters.

### 4. Competitor Naming as Targeting
The top 30 is dominated by creatives that name competitors in the hook. This is creative targeting: "H#kas — 5.6 out of 10" self-selects every Hoka owner in the feed. T004 hooks should lead with competitor names.

### 5. Spanish Adaptation
Proven model: adapt strongest English concepts with native VO talent, reuse end card. The main cost is VO recording. The auction competition is lower and the audience is underserved.

### 6. Close Architecture
Universal across all winners: 40% off + 45-day guarantee + free shipping + 500K+ customers + doctor-recommended. Don't reinvent this.
