#### Language Patterns to Mirror in Copy

- **Cost language:** "affordable," "payment plans," "can't afford," "insurance won't cover," "out of pocket," "too expensive," "cheaper than"

#### Problem Statements

- I heard about Ozempic from my friend who lost 40 lbs but when I checked the price, it was over $1,000/month and my insurance won't cover it.
- My doctor recommended GLP-1 but my insurance requires a 6-month prior authorization process and even then it might get denied.
- I've been spending $200-$300/month at a local weight loss clinic and getting mediocre results. There has to be a better way.
- I know this medication works — I've seen the results in people around me. I just need an affordable way to access it.

#### Existing Beliefs

- GLP-1 medications are only for rich people or people with good insurance.
- Compounded medications might not be as good as the "real" ones.
- Online telehealth services are probably scams.
- If it's cheaper, there must be a catch.
- Payment plans probably have hidden interest or fees.

#### Solutions They've Tried

- **Diet and exercise** — Worked temporarily, weight always comes back. The cycle of lose-regain has been going for years.
- **OTC diet pills/supplements** — Spent money on Amazon products that didn't work
- **Local weight loss clinics / med spas** — Too expensive ($400-$600/month for compounded GLP-1)
- **Insurance prior authorization** — Denied or stuck in bureaucratic limbo
- **Doctor's office GLP-1** — Prescription in hand but can't fill it at the pharmacy price

#### Key Drivers to Change & Psychological Triggers

- **Payment plan availability (The Unlock):** "$0 down" removes the financial barrier entirely. She doesn't need $349 in her account today — she needs $0 today and $29/month. This is the conversion mechanism.
- **Social proof at scale:** "35,000+ patients" and "2,000+ reviews" signals that this is not a fly-by-night operation. The volume makes it feel safe.
- **Named rep connection:** When Tyler or Chris calls to walk her through the process, she feels cared for. This personal touch converts skeptics.
- **Fast results from peers:** Facebook comments and reviews showing 20-30 lbs in 12 weeks create urgency. "If she can do it, I can do it."
- **No-risk positioning:** "100% refund if not approved" removes the fear of wasting money on something she doesn't qualify for.

## 2. The Postpartum / Life-Change Weight Gainer — "Since Having My Baby, Nothing Works"

### Part 2: Deep Customer Profile

She's 28-42. Had a baby 1-3 years ago and the weight won't come off. Or she hit perimenopause at 45 and everything changed. Or she went through a divorce, a stressful job change, COVID weight gain. The trigger varies, but the pattern is the same: a life event caused weight gain, and diet/exercise can't undo it.

She feels disconnected from her body. She doesn't recognize herself in the mirror. She doesn't fit in her clothes. Her confidence is gone.

#### How They Actually Talk About It

> — Crazy Southern, Trustpilot (5 stars)

> "Was so discouraged and couldn't lose weight being 45 and pre menopause. Started this journey in August and I'm down 21 pounds."
> — Christine Laird, Trustpilot (5 stars)

> "I am so thankful for GLP-1 medications and what they offer for weight loss and regaining confidence, especially after having children."
> — Lindsey Shoultes, Trustpilot (5 stars)

> "The first time I put on my favorite jeans after losing 15 lbs, I cried. Not because they didn't fit, but because they finally did."
> — Amanda Y, verified patient

#### Language Patterns to Mirror in Copy

- **Body disconnect:** "don't recognize myself," "nothing fits," "the scale keeps going up," "I've tried everything"
- **Identity loss:** "I want to feel like myself again," "lost my confidence," "no energy for my kids"
- **Frustration cycle:** "diet and exercise don't work anymore," "the weight won't budge," "I'm doing everything right"
- **Recovery:** "getting my life back," "feel like the old me," "fit in my clothes again," "finally"

#### Problem Statements

- I had my second baby two years ago and I've gained 50 lbs that won't come off no matter what I do.
- I'm 45 and premenopausal. My metabolism has completely changed. Diet and exercise used to work — now they don't.
- I don't have the energy or time to spend hours at the gym. I have three kids and work full time.
- I haven't worn my favorite jeans in three years. I've bought four sizes up and I hate looking in the mirror.

#### Key Drivers to Change

- **Emotional transformation:** "Getting my life back" and "feeling like myself again" are more powerful motivators than the number on the scale. Creative should lead with identity recovery, not pounds lost.
- **Before/after milestone:** "Fitting into my favorite clothes" is the #1 qualitative transformation marker in reviews. It's tactile, relatable, and shareable.
- **Peer proof:** Other moms losing weight creates "if she can, I can" motivation. Use mom-specific testimonials.
- **Time efficiency:** "For anybody who just doesn't have time, this is for you. I have 3 jobs and am still managing to lose weight!" — The fully online, no-visit model is perfect for busy parents.

- **Dose continuity:** Elevate accommodates existing doses. "Show us your prescription label" and they'll continue at your current dose. This is critical — dose regression causes weight regain.
- **Speed:** These patients need medication quickly to avoid gaps. Elevate's 7-10 day delivery meets this need.
