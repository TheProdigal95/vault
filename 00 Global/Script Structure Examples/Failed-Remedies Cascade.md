# Script Structure: Failed-Remedies Cascade

"I tried everything." The body stacks 3-5 failed solutions the viewer has also tried, each with a specific failure and price. The frustration builds until the product is introduced as the thing that finally worked. The cascade IS the body — not a brief mention of alternatives.

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Problem trigger (brief) → failed remedy 1 → failed remedy 2 → failed remedy 3 → (optional: 4, 5) → product as the one that finally worked → proof → close.
- **VO register:** Female 35-55, frustrated and embodied. Not calm narration — in-the-moment frustration. The pivot to solution is grudging at first ("I almost didn't try them"), then genuine surprise.
- **Time to benefit:** ~28s. The cascade takes up most of the body. Product proof is compressed but earned by the buildup.
- **Rhythm:** 3-5s per failed remedy. Each: name the remedy → specific failure → move on. The speed builds as the cascade stacks. The product beat slows down — it's the relief after the frustration.
- **Duration:** 60-75s.
- **Close type:** Guarantee-forward.
- **Visual grammar:** Creator on couch, ticking items off on fingers. Frustrated energy building with each failure. Brief B-roll inserts between remedies. Cobblestone/terrain B-roll for proof section. Phone-quality throughout.
- **Hook defaults:** Contrarian ("The worst travel advice I ever got"). Couch confession ("I tried everything"). TK direct clip (soaked shoes, swollen feet).
- **Caption strategy:** ❌ prefix per failed remedy with specific failure. ✅ at product. Captions stack the failures visually so sound-off viewers see the cascade.

### When to Use
- When the buyer has tried 3-5 things before finding this product (physical therapy, insoles, expensive competitors, home remedies)
- When the journey of failed solutions IS the trust signal ("she's tried what I've tried")
- When the product's advantage is specifically that it solves what the alternatives couldn't
- Differentiates from Authority Rating by using first-person experience instead of third-person clinical judgment

### Key Rules
- Each failed remedy gets a SPECIFIC failure — not "it didn't work" but "physio helped for about a week" or "insoles shifted the pain from my heel to my arch"
- Include prices where known — they compound into the money-waste narrative
- "I was just moving the pain around" is the insight that ties the cascade together: every remedy moved the problem, none solved it
- The product intro must include initial skepticism ("I almost didn't try them. Sixty dollars.") — the cascade earns the right to be skeptical
- Coworker/friend referral as the discovery mechanism adds credibility after the cascade of professional remedies failed
