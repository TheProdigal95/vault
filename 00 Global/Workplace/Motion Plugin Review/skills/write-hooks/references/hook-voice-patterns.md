# Hook Voice Patterns Library

Proven hook templates organized by emotional function. These are structural patterns from real high-performing social content — not invented frameworks. Use them as sentence-level blueprints, swapping in brand-specific variables.

## How to Use This Library

1. Identify the **psychological trigger** you want to activate
2. Find a matching **pattern cluster** below
3. Swap the bracketed variables for your product, persona, and pain
4. Pair with the **Hook Tactics** reference for strategic framing
5. Pair with the **Hook Writing** skill for awareness stage deployment

---

## Cluster 1: Confession & Gatekeeping

**Triggers:** Exclusivity, Curiosity Gap, Social Proof

Templates:
- "I have been gatekeeping something that is so niche..."
- "Ok I've been keeping this to myself for way too long..."
- "I wasn't going to share this but [number] of you asked..."
- "This is the [product category] secret nobody talks about..."
- "I've been hiding this from my friends because I don't want it to sell out..."

**When to use:** When the product feels like insider knowledge. Best for solution-aware and product-aware stages where exclusivity creates desire.

**Variables:** `[product category]`, `[number of people]`, `[the secret/thing]`

---

## Cluster 2: Confusion & Discovery

**Triggers:** Curiosity Gap, Pattern Interrupt, Relatability

Templates:
- "Who was gonna tell me that [surprising fact about product]..."
- "Wait, so you're telling me [unexpected benefit] this whole time?"
- "I just found out [common thing] has been [causing problem] and I'm shook"
- "Nobody told me [product] could actually [unexpected use case]"
- "How did I not know about this until now?"

**When to use:** When the product solves a problem people didn't know had a solution. Best for unaware and problem-aware stages.

**Variables:** `[surprising fact]`, `[unexpected benefit]`, `[common thing]`, `[problem]`, `[unexpected use case]`

---

## Cluster 3: Hot Takes & Contrarian

**Triggers:** Pattern Interrupt, Contrarian/Myth-Busting

Templates:
- "My hot take is that [common practice] is actually [negative consequence]"
- "Unpopular opinion: [widely accepted thing] is the worst thing you can do for [goal]"
- "I will die on this hill: [strong contrarian position]"
- "Everyone's doing [trend] and nobody's talking about why it's [problem]"
- "Stop [common advice]. It's making your [problem] worse."

**When to use:** When challenging conventional wisdom creates an opening for your product. Best for solution-aware stage.

**Variables:** `[common practice]`, `[negative consequence]`, `[widely accepted thing]`, `[goal]`, `[contrarian position]`, `[trend]`

---

## Cluster 4: Specific Moment Recognition

**Triggers:** Pain Agitation, Identity Call-Out, Relatability

Templates:
- "You know that feeling when [hyper-specific moment]? Yeah..."
- "It's [time of day] and you're [specific relatable action]. Sound familiar?"
- "POV: You just [specific frustrating moment] for the third time this week"
- "Tell me you [experience problem] without telling me you [experience problem]"
- "Me at [time] trying to [specific struggle]: [relatable description]"

**When to use:** When naming the exact lived experience creates instant recognition. Best for problem-aware stage. The more specific the moment, the stronger the hook.

**Variables:** `[hyper-specific moment]`, `[time of day]`, `[specific relatable action]`, `[specific frustrating moment]`, `[specific struggle]`

---

## Cluster 5: Authority Confession

**Triggers:** Social Proof, Pattern Interrupt, Confession

Templates:
- "I'm a [professional title] and I would never [common recommendation in their field]"
- "As a [expert], here's what I actually use instead of what I recommend to clients"
- "I've been a [profession] for [years] and this is the first time I've seen [result]"
- "[Professional title] here. [Common advice] is outdated. Here's what actually works."
- "I spent [years/money] studying [field] and the answer was simpler than anyone thinks"

**When to use:** When expertise creates credibility for a contrarian position. Best for solution-aware and product-aware stages.

**Variables:** `[professional title]`, `[common recommendation]`, `[expert type]`, `[years]`, `[result]`, `[field]`

---

## Cluster 6: Transformation & Before/After

**Triggers:** Aspiration, Social Proof, Curiosity Gap

Templates:
- "6 months ago I couldn't [struggle]. Now I [achievement]. Here's what changed."
- "The difference between [before state] and [after state] was one thing..."
- "I went from [relatable low point] to [aspirational state] in [timeframe]"
- "This is what [timeframe] of [product/habit] actually looks like"
- "Everyone asks me how I [achievement]. It's not what you think."

**When to use:** When the transformation is dramatic and relatable. Best for problem-aware through product-aware stages. Specific timelines and outcomes create credibility.

**Variables:** `[struggle]`, `[achievement]`, `[before state]`, `[after state]`, `[timeframe]`, `[relatable low point]`, `[aspirational state]`

---

## Cluster 7: Warning & Consequence

**Triggers:** Urgency/Stakes, Pain Agitation, Pattern Interrupt

Templates:
- "If you're still [common behavior], please stop. Here's why."
- "Warning: [popular product/practice] is [specific negative effect]"
- "You need to check your [product] right now. I'll wait."
- "The [thing] you're using every day is [negative consequence] and you don't even know it"
- "[Number]% of people are making this mistake with their [product category]"

**When to use:** When the status quo has hidden consequences. Best for unaware and problem-aware stages. The warning creates urgency to learn more.

**Variables:** `[common behavior]`, `[popular product/practice]`, `[specific negative effect]`, `[thing]`, `[negative consequence]`, `[number]`, `[product category]`

---

## Cluster 8: Social Proof Cascade

**Triggers:** Social Proof, FOMO, Urgency

Templates:
- "[Number] people switched to this last month. Here's why."
- "Ok so apparently everyone has been using [product] except me"
- "My [friend/sister/coworker] made me try this and now I can't stop"
- "I finally understand why [product] has [number] five-star reviews"
- "I was skeptical until [specific person or group] told me to try it"

**When to use:** When volume of social proof is the strongest signal. Best for product-aware and most-aware stages.

**Variables:** `[number]`, `[product]`, `[friend/sister/coworker]`, `[specific person or group]`

---

## Cluster 9: Price & Value Reframe

**Triggers:** Pattern Interrupt, Contrast, Social Proof

Templates:
- "People think this costs [high price]. It's actually [low price]."
- "I replaced [expensive thing] with [product] and save [amount] a month"
- "The [cheap alternative] that outperforms the [expensive brand name]"
- "Hear me out: [low price] for [impressive benefit list]"
- "Why am I just finding out [product category] doesn't have to cost [high price]?"

**When to use:** When price objection is the primary barrier. Best for product-aware and most-aware stages.

**Variables:** `[high price]`, `[low price]`, `[expensive thing]`, `[product]`, `[amount]`, `[cheap alternative]`, `[expensive brand name]`, `[benefit list]`

---

## Cluster 10: Platform-Native Formats

**Triggers:** Varies — leverages platform cultural containers

Templates:
- "Things that just make sense: [product solving obvious problem]"
- "Rating [product category] I've tried from worst to best"
- "Put a finger down if [relatable problem]. Now let me show you the fix."
- "[Product] check. [Feature 1]: check. [Feature 2]: check. [Surprising feature]: check."
- "Day [number] of using [product] until [goal/someone notices/it stops working]"

**When to use:** When platform-native framing increases stop rate. The cultural container provides built-in resonance — viewers already know the format, which lowers resistance. Works across all awareness stages depending on content.

**Variables:** `[product]`, `[product category]`, `[relatable problem]`, `[features]`, `[number]`, `[goal]`

## Maintenance

This library should grow. When you encounter a new high-performing hook pattern:
1. Identify the trigger pairing
2. Abstract the structure (replace specifics with variables)
3. Place it in the matching cluster or create a new one
4. Add 3-4 template variations
