**Key insight — Hook score vs. hold rate vs. ROAS:**
- **Sabalenka (#14)** has the highest hook score (82) but below-average ROAS (0.77). Great at stopping the scroll, but the celebrity content doesn't close.
- **Whiteboard (#1)** has a moderate hook (59) but the highest hold rate (14.8%) and top-tier ROAS (0.94). Doesn't stop as many people, but those who stay watch through and buy.
- **Timeline formats (#2, #5)** have strong hooks (58-69) but very low hold rates (3.4-3.8%), yet strong ROAS (0.77-0.90). Most viewers drop early, but survivors are highly motivated buyers. 30-day still outperforms 90-day on ROAS despite lower hold.

- **Chernow (#17)** is the outlier: extreme CTR (9.09%), strong hold (13.0%), AND 1.00 ROAS. The KOL format stops, retains, and converts.
- **The pattern:** High hook + high hold = scalable winner. High hook + low hold = body doesn't deliver on the hook's promise. Low hook + high hold = filters for committed viewers but limits reach.

**Spend:** $84,567 | **ROAS:** 0.90 | **Format:** Video, ~1:34

**Hook:** "This is what happens when you drink IM8 every day for 30 days." Same hook formula as the 90-day version (Ad #2) but compressed timeframe. The 30-day frame lowers the commitment threshold — 90 days feels like a big ask, 30 days feels achievable.

**Structure — 6 milestones:**
1. Day 1 — Gut: 10B probiotics, prebiotics, postbiotics. Bloating calms within hours. (0:00-0:14)
2. Day 3 — Digestion: food moves easier, heavy feeling fades. (0:14-0:22)
3. Day 7 — Brain fog lifts: gut produces feel-good chemicals, B vitamins give clean energy. (0:22-0:34)
4. Day 14 — Energy: afternoon crash gone, CoQ10 + B/C/D vitamins convert food to all-day energy. (0:34-0:48)
5. Day 21 — Inflammation: MSM repairs gut lining, bloating/cramps ease. (0:48-1:00)
6. Day 30 — Full transformation: better gut, more energy, sharper. Replaced supplements with 92 nutrients. (1:00-1:13)
7. Proof + CTA: 6,000+ five-star reviews, Beckham/Sabalenka, money-back guarantee, welcome kit. (1:13-1:34)

**Critical learning from user notes — SIMPLIFY THE LANGUAGE:**

> "The language we used in our scripts for the previous batch was a little bit too complicated at times. We try to fit so much into them. If you take a look at the transcript of these ads, it is simple, easy-to-understand language they use to make you understand the benefits. They talk about common pain points, in simple language, with easy to understand benefits."

Comparing this script to our T001 scripts, the difference is clear. This ad says things like:
- "The bloating starts calming down within hours"
- "Food moves through more easily"
- "That heavy, uncomfortable feeling after meals? Starting to fade."
- "The brain fog lifts"
- "That afternoon energy crash? Gone."

No jargon-first language. No complex mechanisms upfront. They name the pain point in plain words, then introduce the ingredient as the reason. Our T001 scripts often led with the mechanism ("the 13-amino renewal complex with Boron, L-glutamine, and Malic acid") before establishing the relatable benefit. These top spenders reverse that order: relatable pain → simple benefit → ingredient name as proof.

**What makes it work:** The 30-day compressed timeframe + simple language combo. Each milestone follows the same micro-pattern: relatable pain point → benefit in plain words → one ingredient/mechanism as the "why." The visual style (multiple creators, 3D animations of gut/vitamins, product shots) keeps it dynamic. The 0.90 ROAS vs. the 90-day version's 0.77 suggests the shorter commitment frame converts better.
