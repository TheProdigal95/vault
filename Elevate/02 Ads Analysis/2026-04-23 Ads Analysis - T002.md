## Top Spenders

Motion report: https://projects.motionapp.com/organization/6214fe5e1ccc60d23897d87b/68c44c43d4196b03cb80d1b0/top/68c44c45bfc760f386bd43b0
CSV: ![[Top creatives-6.csv]]

An interesting pattern that seems to have arisen from the compliance rules that I'm noticing here is tackling the eligibility question about medical weight loss. I see creatives around people being eligible but not knowing it so they direct you to the quiz, which is also consistent with what we had seen about price objections handling being good performers in the account.

I notice ads that get good spend but have poor roas, they don't seem to mention the payment plan providers or show their logos. 

### Patterns Across Top Spenders

**1. Eligibility is the compliance-safe superpower.** Three of four top spenders drive to the quiz through "you might be eligible and not know it." This is the angle that lets Elevate sell without naming the drug — the quiz gates the conversation, compliance stays clean.

**2. Payment logos = ROAS.** Ads without payment plan logos or price anchors (#6, #10) get spend but poor-to-zero ROAS. Ads that mention payment plans (#8) convert better. The hypothesis: the eligibility angle stops the scroll, but price/payment closes the sale. One without the other leaves money on the table.

**3. UGC selfie + trending audio is a format, not a one-off.** #10 and #11 are the same template (selfie walk + motivational audio + persistent caption). #11's caption arc is better (shame → pride, experience language, food noise mention, features). #10 has stronger thumbstop (41%) but zero purchases. The format works for attention; the caption content determines conversion.

**4. Existing ads have compliance gaps we can't replicate.** #8 uses "GLP-1" as a product name and claims "30 lbs down." #10 claims "20lbs down." #8 uses $54/week (our verified floor is $58/week). Our versions need experience language, correct pricing, and no drug naming.

**5. Two video structures emerged.** (a) UGC talking head with insurance/eligibility hook → quiz CTA (#8). (b) UGC selfie walk with emotional audio + caption story (#10, #11). Both are low-production, high-authenticity. The account's static winners from T001 (payment plans, frosted glass, IG Stories Widget) are still spending — videos are additive, not replacement.

Simple image ad, shows overweight woman dissatisfied with her figure in the photo, calls out most people don't know they're eligible for medical weight loss and invites you to take the quiz. Good spender, poor roas.

**Preview:** [Static Image](https://motionaccountassets.blob.core.windows.net/68c44c43bfc760f386bd4372/creativeassetfacebook/6904d03252ed4a2ffc9105fa/image.jpeg)
**Performance:** $3,457 spend / 0.36 ROAS / $1,729 CPP / 2 purchases

**Visual Breakdown:**
- Vertical mobile format. Blue gradient top section (~40%) with bold white sans-serif headline. Photo fills bottom ~60%.
- Headline: "Most people eligible for medical weight loss don't know it."
- Photo: Plus-size woman in sage athleisure (sports bra + biker shorts), viewed from behind, looking at herself in a full-length gold-framed mirror. Real-life setting: bright room, natural light, minimalist decor. Introspective pose, hands on hips.
- Pink CTA button overlaid on photo: "Curious if you qualify? Check here →"
- Elevate Health logo bottom-left. Standard disclaimer fine print bottom-right.
- **No payment plan logos anywhere.** No price mentioned in the image.
- Element count: 4 (headline, photo, CTA, logo/disclaimer). Very clean composition.

**Strategic Analysis:**
- The eligibility angle is the clearest compliance-safe hook in the account. Names the problem without naming the drug, directs to quiz.
- The photo is the right kind of real-life imagery — relatable woman, not a model, introspective moment. Matches our imagery rules.
- However: $3.5k spend with only 2 purchases (0.36 ROAS). The ad drives attention but doesn't convert. The missing piece is clear: no price, no payment plan logos, no "$0 down." The viewer clicks, sees pricing on the landing page, and bounces. This directly supports the hypothesis that payment logo presence correlates with ROAS.
- **What we take:** The eligibility headline angle + real-life mirror imagery + clean composition. **What we fix:** Add payment plan logos + price anchor ("Programs from $58/week / $0 down") to close the conversion gap.

- **What we take:** The format itself — UGC selfie walk + trending audio + persistent caption overlay. The two-channel architecture (audio carries emotion, caption carries story). **What we fix:** Caption needs experience language (not weight numbers), and must include a conversion lever (price/payment/quiz CTA).
