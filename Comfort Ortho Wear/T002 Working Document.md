## Concept Validation

> Runs after the Batch Plan is drafted, before script/brief assignments begin. Each concept passes the 7 tests individually; the differentiation rule is a batch-level cross-check.

### 7-Test Quality Bar (per concept)

1. **Pain point specificity** — built around a specific human tension, not a product feature. Complete: "This concept exists because [specific person] is struggling with [specific experience]." If the pain could apply to any brand in the category, not specific enough.
2. **Strategic coherence** — audience, message, structure, and hook work together. No component feels bolted on or generic.
3. **Differentiation** — couldn't be used by a competitor without modification. Specific to this brand's positioning, product, or audience.
4. **Format ambition** — visual format chosen intentionally, not by default. The format meaningfully contributes to attention or persuasion.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — the hook's execution creates felt tension (how it presents the pain, not what the pain is). Names a problem, challenges a belief, or makes the viewer feel personally implicated.
6. **Testable hypothesis** — "Why it can work" is grounded in behavioral or pattern insights. Specific enough to succeed or fail clearly.
7. **Producible** — a creative team can execute without major clarification.

### Differentiation Rule (batch-level cross-check)

Each concept must shift at least 2 of these 4 axes from every other concept in the batch:

- Intended audience (persona)
- Messaging angle
- Visual format / structure
- Hook tactic

## Script Criteria

### Compliance

See `00 Context/Compliance & Guardrails - Comfort Ortho Wear.md` for full rules. Key points:

- **Approved trust badge:** "500,000+ Happy Customers" — exact phrasing for on-screen text/captions. VO can reference the target audience (e.g., "Over 500,000 nurses and healthcare workers have already made the switch") since spoken language targets the viewer.
- **Approved guarantee language:** "Feel the difference in 45 days, or get 100% of your money back." — exact phrasing only
- **Approved authority framing:** "Expert-recommended" (approved by client). "Doctor-recommended comfort" and "Designed to have orthopedic-like support" — brand-positioned, not clinical claims. Avoid "podiatrist approved."
- **Named conditions (PDP-approved):** Foot Pain, Plantar Fasciitis, Bunions, Neuropathy, Flat Feet, Hammer Toes, Morton's Neuroma, Tendonitis, Back Pain
- **HSA/FSA eligible** — approved claim
- **Slip-resistant outsole** — confirmed for hospitals, kitchens, outdoor paths (FAQ-sourced)
- **No named clinical studies, no named doctors, no certifications** — none exist on-page; do not fabricate
- **"Free returns"** — never use without noting exceptions (HI, PR, AK, GU, VI, and international excluded)
- **Discount claims** — confirm current active promotion before scripting
- **Competitor brand names** — can be spoken in VO; must use # in on-screen text (e.g., "SK#TCHERS", "H#KAS"); logos must be blurred in footage

- Every video runs persistent on-screen captions — no format in this account succeeds without them
- Captions never repeat VO verbatim when both channels are active — two channels, two messages
- Competitor shoe names captioned but logos blurred in footage

### Editing Styles (from T001 Top Spenders + IM8 Learnings)

- **Shot pacing:** 2-3 seconds per shot. Fast cuts, no transitions. Motion in first frame of every shot.
- **Competitor treatment:** Named in VO and captions, logos blurred in footage. Each competitor gets one specific failure shown (Skechers = foam compresses, Hoka = too heavy/bulky, Nike = too narrow/made for running).
- **Animated overlays:** Foot anatomy (X-ray arch support, toe-spread arrows, pain-point red glow) for mechanism beats. 3D bone/joint overlays on product shots.
- **Color-coded states:** B&W or desaturated for pain visualization. Full color, vibrant for solution/relief moments.
- **Social proof screenshots:** Review comments pop up over B-roll mid-video. 2-3 screenshots per concept where used. Authentic grammar/formatting preserved.
- **End card (standardized):** Brand logo → 3-4 shoe colorways in a row → badge stack (💰 45 Days Money-Back Guarantee / ✈️ Free Shipping / ⚕️ Doctor-Recommended). Editor handles, not scripted.
- **Audio:** Background music for B-roll/educational formats. No music for UGC/first-person (authenticity). 110-120 BPM upbeat instrumental for timelines.

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

1. Guarantee language exact: "45 days, 100% money back" — not "30 days" or "60 days"
2. Trust badge exact: "500,000+ Happy Customers" — not "half a million" or "thousands"
3. Approved conditions only — no conditions beyond the PDP list
4. No fabricated doctor names, study citations, or certifications
5. Slip-resistance claim tied to setting (hospitals/kitchens/outdoor) — not generic "all surfaces"
6. Pain-first language — every script opens with the felt pain before naming mechanisms
7. Discount claims verified against current active promotion at time of writing
8. Single-focus concepts stay single-focus — don't let agents add other mechanisms into the body
9. Competitors named in VO, logos blurred in footage, asterisked in on-screen text (match account pattern + compliance doc)
10. Hook caption variety — not all hooks in the batch use the same angle. Mix across proven patterns: pain-first, curiosity-gap, data/survey, result-first
11. Social proof screenshots sourced from real reviews — never fabricated or paraphrased into perfection. Authentic grammar preserved
12. Product named before the close — ComfortWear must appear in body at minimum. Close alone is insufficient
