## Batch Thesis

This batch introduces Samir as the founder/trust layer for ComfortWear. The account has already shown that real footage, authority/rating structures, competitor/value framing, and product-specific proof outperform polished AI-style creative. Founder footage gives us a new authenticity asset: the product was not invented from a spreadsheet; it came from watching healthcare workers in Samir's life come home with aching feet, destroyed shoes, and expensive replacement cycles.

Core creative move: use Samir's A-roll as the human reason to believe, then cut quickly into real product, nurse, review, competitor, and offer B-roll so each ad still behaves like a native performance ad. The strongest Samir beats are reusable modules; they can appear in more than one concept as long as each ad has a different narrative spine and the repeated beat is doing a clear job.

## Batch Criteria / Carry-Forward Rules

- Video only. No static briefs planned for this batch unless strategy changes.
- Target duration: **25-35 seconds** for most ads. Allow **35-45 seconds** only when the concept needs a fuller founder/customer story. Do not intentionally brief 15-25 second cutdowns for this batch.
- Every ad should have enough narrative beats to feel complete: hook/tension → Samir founder beat → proof B-roll / product or customer support → offer/guarantee/CTA.
- Every ad must cite actual Drive/Motion/source links in its Footage section; no local `.md` links.
- Every script must begin with a **SUPER** identifying the speaker as the founder **without relying on Samir's name**. Default: `SUPER: Founder of ComfortWear`.
- Audience-facing hooks, captions, supers, and close lines should not name-drop `Samir`. Viewers do not know who he is yet. Hooks should lead with the audience pain, the problem solved, contrarian category beliefs, competition/value tension, or an expectation gap.
- Every script must include this production note bullet near the top: **Please do not use AI footage. Use only real b-roll from the brand or source from TikTok.**
- Every script must include this editor pacing note near the top: **Cut all silences, pauses, false starts, and dead air. Trim Samir to the precise words needed so the edit feels snappy and paced like a native ad.**
- Competitor logos must be blurred. Competitor names in on-screen captions must be masked with one letter: `Sk*tchers`, `H*ka`, etc. Competitor names can be spoken in VO.
- Avoid `podiatrist approved`; use `expert recommended` only where needed.
- Keep universal close stack available where relevant: 40% off, 45-day guarantee, free shipping, 500K+ customers, expert/doctor-recommended language only if source-safe.
- Use real product/nurse/studio/competitor b-roll heavily. Samir A-roll is the anchor, not the whole edit.

## Editing Style System

Use editing style intentionally. We do **not** need one consistent screen treatment for the entire ad; the screen treatment should change based on the job of the beat.

| Editing treatment | When to use | Why it works | Example beats |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full-screen Samir talking head | First 2-6 seconds of an emotional/founder hook, direct nurse address, founder confession, guarantee challenge | Establishes founder presence and trust before cutting away | `IMG_0641` healthcare-workers-called-heroes line, `IMG_0668/0669` nurse address, `IMG_0662` too-cheap objection |
| Samir A-roll with B-roll overlays | When Samir's audio is strong but the viewer needs visual proof, product context, or pace | Keeps founder voice while preventing the ad from feeling like a static monologue | $150-$200 shoes → overlay competitor shoes/prices; paycheck-to-paycheck → overlay nurse shift/payday visuals |
| Split screen: Samir top / B-roll bottom | When Samir is explaining or reacting while the bottom half shows concrete proof | Lets editors preserve founder authenticity and show evidence simultaneously; useful for value, teardown, guarantee, and customer-story beats | Samir says expensive shoes collapse while bottom shows ripped/collapsed shoes; Samir explains experts while bottom shows product/insole shots |
| Full B-roll with Samir audio continuing | Mechanism/proof sections where the image matters more than his face | Gives the edit motion and lets product/nurse visuals carry the claim while Samir remains the voice | Product close-ups, nurse walking, shoe pile, wet-floor/product grip, offer/end card |
| Text/review overlay on B-roll | Customer story, guarantee, offer, or value math sections | Clarifies sensitive claims and makes the proof scannable sound-off | `45-day guarantee`, `500K+ customers`, customer review screenshot, $60 vs $150-$200 price stack |

Default edit rhythm: open with the founder or a bold text hook, cut to proof within the first 4-6 seconds, then alternate founder A-roll and B-roll every 2-4 seconds. Avoid long uninterrupted founder monologues. Editors should cut out every silence/pause/false start and stitch only the exact words needed; the final A-roll should feel intentionally paced, not like raw interview footage.

Caption/hook rule: do not open with `Samir says...` or `Meet Samir...`. Open with the viewer's problem or a contradiction, then let the founder identity become the reason to believe. Strong caption hooks should create curiosity or an expectation gap, e.g. `The $180 shoes were the ones failing first`, `Nurses were called heroes. Their feet were ignored.`, `Only $60? That was the point.`, or `Your shift may not be the real problem.`
