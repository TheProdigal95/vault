# T002 — IM8 AppLovin Script Batch

## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

We need to learn from the Top Spenders analysis but for this batch, given that we launch an App Lovin and our Top Spenders analysis is from Meta, what I'm seeing that performs on App Lovin is primarily menopause. So this is the concept split for this batch:

- 10 for Menopause
- 3 for GLP-1
- 2 for Gut Health

For this batch let's avoid briefing content that requires creator shooting. Only things we can do with AI voiceover and b-roll.

### Learnings from Top Spenders

- **Simplify the language.** Pain point first, ingredient as proof second. Not the other way around like T001.
- **"$72 in Welcome Gifts — On Us" as opening caption** on some videos to test ROAS lift, even if the script isn't about the offer
- **Cortisol reframe structure (Ad #8).** Adapt for menopause: common frustration → unexpected cause → IM8 solves the real cause
- **"Within a day or two" temporal discounting (Ad #9).** Adapt for menopause: "Within the first few days..."
- **Green screen narrator-over-B-roll format (Ad #4).** Half review, half green screen. Feels native, not produced. Two variants in top 20.
- **AI animated character format (Ad #11).** Long-form GLP-1 listicle, sardonic tone, zero production cost. Consider for one of the 3 GLP-1 scripts.
- **30-day timelines convert better than 90-day** (0.90 vs. 0.77 ROAS). Use shorter timeframes.
- **Our menopause scripts already outperform the pre-existing ones on AppLovin.** Keep widening the gap.
- **The "math problem" technique (Ad #4).** "I needed 90 grams of protein but could barely get 30 in." Creates a logical trap that makes IM8 the obvious answer. Adaptable: "Your body needs X nutrients during menopause but you're only getting Y from food."
- **Micro-details sell the story.** "I can open jars again" (Ad #4) is worth more than any stat. One tiny, concrete moment makes the whole testimonial believable.
- **Competitive positioning without naming brands.** "Unlike that green powder every podcaster is pushing" (Ad #11). Use solution categories ("probiotics," "greens powders") not brand names.
- **Specificity is the top persuasion technique across the board.** 16 of 20 ads use exact numbers, doses, or stats. Never "powerful nutrients" — always "90 nutrients" or "733% more B12."
- **Reframe structure is adaptable across all personas.** Menopause: "Those hot flashes aren't just hormones." GLP-1: "That muscle loss isn't just about protein." Gut: "That bloating isn't a food problem." Same pattern every time.
- **Temporal milestones for menopause.** "What happens when you drink IM8 every day through menopause for 30 days." Milestones tied to menopause symptoms: sleep improves, hot flashes reduce, mood stabilizes, energy returns.

## Batch Plan

All scripts: AI voiceover + B-roll only. Exception: #9 can require shooting (whiteboard footage). Duration: **30-60 seconds** (AppLovin requirement). 30-day framing preferred over 90-day.

**Language rule — pain first, mechanism second:**

| T001 (mechanism-first — avoid) | Top spender Ad #5 (pain-first — do this) |
|---|---|
| "27 raw superfoods like turmeric and tart cherry may help ease joint discomfort, because they target the inflammation" | "That heavy, uncomfortable feeling after meals? Starting to fade." |
| "Your cells have power plants called mitochondria. They need B12 and CoQ10 to produce energy." | "That afternoon energy crash? Gone." |
| "B12 at 8,333% of daily value helps convert food into real fuel. CoQ10 at 100mg supports your mitochondria directly" | "The brain fog lifts." |

All 15 scripts use simplified language. Scripts #5, #9, #11 (AI character or authority formats) can include more technical phrases where the format supports it — but never mechanism-first. Name the pain, then the ingredient as proof.

**Reframe = tactic, not structure.** Use as a hook type or body beat (4-5 seconds). It shouldn't define the script's architecture.

**Competitive disqualification = beat, not structure.** Use as a 10-15 second body section within numbered reasons, narrative, or educational formats.

**Different visual per hook variation.** Each Hook A, B, C gets a distinct visual treatment. This triples visual variety and testing surface.

**"$72 in Welcome Gifts — On Us" text hook test:** Scripts marked with 🎁 get one hook variation where this runs as the on-screen text hook simultaneous with the verbal hook and visual hook from second 0. It replaces the normal caption for that variation.

## Script Criteria

> **Baseline:** `00 Global/Criteria/` — Universal Copy Rules, Video Script Criteria, Headline & Text Hook Criteria. Everything below is IM8-specific overrides only.

### Compliance

Full guardrails: [[Guardrails & Claims - IM8]]. Script-specific rules:

- **Don't over-hedge.** "Supports" is the default confident tone for structure/function claims. "May support" or "may help" only when the claim could be interpreted as treating or curing. Agents tend to spray "may help support" on everything — that reads as uncertain, not compliant. Most of IM8's claims are standard structure/function and need no hedging at all.
- Approved stats — exact phrasing only: "95% of users felt more energy," "85% of users felt better digestion," "80% of users felt they slept better," "75% of users noticed sharper focus." Never abbreviate ("95% more energy" is a different claim).
- Beckham = "co-founder." Sabalenka = "shareholder and ambassador." Never "endorses" or "spokesperson."
- "Replaces 16+" and "90 clinically dosed ingredients" and "NSF Certified for Sport" — all approved.
- No competitor brand names in ads. Use solution categories: "probiotics," "greens powders," "single-ingredient supplements."
- No disease treatment claims. No "works like Ozempic." No specific weight loss numbers.

### Platform Constraints

- **Duration: 30-60 seconds.** AppLovin requirement. All scripts must fit this window at 250 spm.
- Offer elements (welcome kit, guarantee) go in the platform end card, not the script.

### Product Specifics

- **Ingredient count: 90** (confirmed by client April 2026). Not 92 (homepage number).
- **Product page: get.im8health.com** — source of truth for dosages, claims, and specs.
- Use "nutrients" not "ingredients" when talking about what the body needs.

### Taste Beat

- Not mandatory. Must earn its place — if timing is tight or the concept doesn't naturally lead to a sip moment, cut it.
- When included, vary: spoken line ("and it actually tastes amazing"), reaction shot, or mixing moment.

### Production Constraints (T002-specific)

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

### Hook-to-Body Transition Checkpoint

For every script, all 3 hook variations must transition cleanly into the shared body. Verification method: read each hook's last VO line → body's first VO line back-to-back. If the tone, subject, or energy level doesn't flow, the hook fails QA.

Agents must write each hook's last VO line and the body's first VO line side by side at the end of every script for easy verification.
