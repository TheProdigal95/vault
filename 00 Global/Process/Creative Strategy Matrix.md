# Creative Strategy Matrix

Strategic framework for organizing creative strategy around specific pain × persona intersections, deployed across awareness stages. Provides **structure, not execution** — maps where creative bets live, not the content of the bets.

**Companion docs:** [[Headline & Text Hook Criteria]] (hook writing) · [[Video Script Criteria]] (script structures) · [[Creative Image Ad Criteria]] (static formats) · [[Batch Template]] (batch planning) · [[Creative Diversity Audit]] (portfolio balance)

---

## Core Hierarchy

```
Pain/Desire (Primary Anchor)
    ↓
Persona (Secondary — mapped to each pain/desire)
    ↓
Messaging Angle (Core truth for this intersection)
    ↓
5 Awareness Stages (Unaware → Most-Aware)
    ↓
Creative Mechanic (How the concept delivers the truth)
    ↓
Hook (Opening line — see Headline & Text Hook Criteria)
    ↓
Visual Format (Execution — see Video Script Criteria structures)
```

---

## Step 1: Determine Your Primary Anchor

**Default: Pain.** Exception: Desire for aspirational/luxury products with no functional problem to solve.

**Why pain/desire is primary:** Every product solves a problem or achieves a desire. Pain/desire creates messaging that resonates because it communicates value. Demographics alone don't.

❌ "Hey, are you a busy mom? You should get this water bottle."
✅ "Do you need a water bottle that doesn't leak all over your car while you're running errands with your kids?"

**Pain-first (default):** Functional products solving specific, searchable problems. "Cystic acne," "poor sleep quality," "bulky wallet," "pill fatigue."

**Desire-first (exception):** Aspirational products without acute functional problems. "Quiet luxury," "collector credibility," "elevated everyday moments."

---

## Step 2: Map Personas to Your Anchor

Personas are always secondary — different life contexts in which people experience the same pain or desire.

**The many-to-many relationship:**
- One pain → multiple personas (cystic acne → Professional, Stay-at-Home Mom, Bride)
- One persona → multiple pains (Professional → acne, fatigue, poor sleep)
- Each intersection = unique messaging angle opportunity

```
                Professional    Stay-Home Mom    Bride
Cystic Acne        ✓                ✓             ✓
Folliculitis       ✓                ✓           
Boils                               ✓             ✓
```

**Persona naming:** Always by pain/trigger, never by demographics (per [[Brand Research]] §7.1).

## Step 3: Define Messaging Angles

A messaging angle is the core truth for a specific pain × persona intersection. Conversational, human — not marketing copy, not a tagline.

| Pain/Desire | Persona | Messaging Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Bulky Wallet | Minimalist professional | "Your wallet sucks" |
| Bored Cat | Cat owner with shredded furniture | "Your cat isn't broken, they're bored" |
| Poor Sleep | Exhausted professional | "Melatonin stopped working three months ago" |
| Cystic Acne | Professional burned by dermatologist | "Your dermatologist wrecked your skin" |

Same pain + different persona = different angle.

**How to define — four questions per intersection:**
1. **Use case:** How does THIS person experience THIS pain?
2. **Deepest desire:** What do they REALLY want (beyond surface wants)?
3. **Feature/benefit priorities:** Which product aspects matter most to THEM?
4. **Top objections:** What would THEY specifically doubt?

From those answers, craft a punchy statement. "Your dermatologist wrecked your skin" — not "Professional-grade natural healing without prescription side effects."

---

## Step 4: Deploy Across 5 Awareness Stages

The messaging angle stays the same. What changes is the expression based on where the viewer sits.

### Stage 1: Unaware
**State:** Don't know they have a problem or that a solution exists.
**Goal:** Make them aware the problem exists.
**Approach:** Education, revelation, pattern recognition.

### Stage 2: Problem-Aware
**State:** Know they have a problem, don't know solutions exist.
**Goal:** Agitate the pain, validate their experience.
**Approach:** Empathy, pain callouts, "tired of...?" questions.

### Stage 3: Solution-Aware
**State:** Know solutions exist, exploring options.
**Goal:** Position your solution category, show differentiation.
**Approach:** Category education, comparison, "there's a better way."

### Stage 4: Product-Aware
**State:** Know your product exists, considering it vs alternatives.
**Goal:** Overcome objections, prove superiority.
**Approach:** Proof, comparisons, guarantees, reviews.

### Stage 5: Most-Aware
**State:** Ready to purchase, need final push.
**Goal:** Close the sale.
**Approach:** Urgency, guarantees, direct CTA, limited offers.

### How a messaging angle deploys across stages

**Angle:** "Your dermatologist wrecked your skin"
- **Unaware:** "If your skin is worse after seeing the dermatologist..."
- **Problem-Aware:** "Why does your prescription burn more than your acne?"
- **Solution-Aware:** "Natural treatments that heal without destroying your skin barrier"
- **Product-Aware:** "[Brand] vs your dermatologist's prescription"
- **Most-Aware:** "Get [Brand] with our 30-day guarantee"

### Budget and stage focus

- **Lower budgets:** Start with Problem-Aware and Solution-Aware — find people already searching.
- **Scaling budgets:** Invest heavily in Unaware content — grow the top of funnel.
- **Key to scaling:** Lock in early-stage messaging across diverse pain × persona intersections.

**Critical rule:** Each ad serves ONE stage. Blending stages makes the ad unfocusable.

## Step 5: Creative Mechanics

Before choosing format, define the creative mechanic — how the viewer arrives at the truth.

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| Implied Answer | Visual silently answers a question the hook posed |
| Social Witness | Third party's reaction validates the product |
| Trojan Horse | Ad looks like content until the product appears |
| Reframe | Flips the viewer's existing belief by the end |
| Contrast Without Comment | Shows two realities without editorializing |

Mechanics sit between messaging angles and hooks. They shape what kind of hook to write and what visual format best executes the concept.

---

## Step 6: Visual Formats

Match the format's purpose to the awareness stage's goal:

| Format Purpose | Best Stages |
|---|---|
| Educate and reveal | Unaware, Problem-Aware |
| Compare and demonstrate | Solution-Aware, Product-Aware |
| Prove and build trust | Problem-Aware through Most-Aware |
| Drive action | Product-Aware, Most-Aware |

For specific structures, see [[Video Script Criteria]] and [[Creative Image Ad Criteria]].

## Three Selves Integration

Three Selves (Actual/Ideal/Ought from self-discrepancy theory) is an orthogonal targeting axis — a per-concept decision applied within each matrix cell, not an axis of the matrix itself.

| Self | Language Pattern | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Ought** | Imperative. Obligation. "You should." "Stop doing X." | When they need a nudge to act on something they already believe. |
| **Actual** | Reflective. Validation. "You're not wrong." "This is happening." | When they need recognition before they can act. |
| **Ideal** | Aspirational. Identity. "The person who..." "Your best..." | When they want to see the version of themselves the product creates. |

**The ratio is a strategic decision per persona.** Too much Ought with a buyer who needs validation = tone-deaf. Too much Ideal with a buyer in crisis = dismissive.

**Distribution guidance:** Actual Self is overused (~60% of most ad libraries). Ought Self is massively underused (~5%) and produces high-intent, high-LTV buyers. Ideal Self must tap base human desires, not product-centric aspiration.

## The Complete Matrix

```
Pain/Desire Buckets
    × Personas per bucket
    × Messaging Angles per intersection
    × 5 Awareness Stages per angle
    × Creative Mechanics
    × Hooks (see Headline & Text Hook Criteria)
    × Visual Formats (see Video Script Criteria structures)

    Three Selves (Actual/Ideal/Ought) = orthogonal lens applied per concept, not a matrix dimension
```

## When to Use This Framework

- **Batch planning:** Map which matrix cells this batch covers; identify gaps from prior batches. The Concept Validation gate (see [[Batch Template]]) runs after the plan is drafted.
- **Gap analysis:** Audit which pain × persona × awareness stage cells have been tested vs untested.
- **Creative diversity audit:** See [[Creative Diversity Audit]] for portfolio-level balance checks across the matrix.
- **Strategic alignment:** Ensure each concept has a clear cell assignment before writing begins.

## How to Read the Matrix for Batch Planning

1. List all pain × persona cells from Persona Context
2. Tag each prior batch's concepts to their cells
3. Identify tested cells (proven), adjacent cells (likely responsive), untested cells (exploration)
4. Assign this batch's concepts to target specific cells — each concept maps to a row in the Batch Plan table: Persona column = matrix persona, Concept column = pain/desire anchor, Structure column = creative mechanic, Key Mechanism = messaging angle, Notes = awareness stage + Three Selves lens
5. Run the Concept Validation gate (see [[Batch Template]]) before writing
