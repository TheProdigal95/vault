## Native/X close direction

For Oral Only route-switcher/native X statics, closers should feel decisive and conversion-oriented, not soft consideration language.

Prefer this direction:
- Time to switch to Oral TRT.
- Time to switch to daily Oral TRT.
- Time to give Oral Only TRT a try.
- That is the whole point of Oral TRT.
- This is the case for fast-acting Oral TRT.
- This is why fast-acting Oral TRT exists.

Avoid as default close language:
- belongs on the shortlist
- on the table
- deserves a rethink
- worth a closer look
- worth exploring
- long doctor/clinic/legalese phrasing like “ask your clinic whether...”
- clunky price-mechanics phrasing like “The 3-month plan brings Oral TRT to $126/month.”

## Offer-close tests

Test the offer in at least one variation per native/X concept when it fits naturally. Do not cram the full stack into every tweet.

Approved test phrasings from T001:
- Try 2 months of Oral Only, get 1 free.
- Time to switch to Oral TRT. Third month is free on Oral Only.
- Oral Only now includes free NAD+ with subscription.
- Time to give Oral Only TRT a try. NAD+ is free right now with their sub.

Use free NAD+ selectively. It works best as a direct add-on to a try/switch close or as a standalone offer close.

## Compliance guardrails for closes

Read `C&D Claims Risk Summary - Oral Only.md` before new creative.

Do not use spike/flatline language unless Oral Only provides approved/source-backed language. Avoid:
- violent spikes
- dangerous spikes
- unnatural flatlines
- avoids spikes
- prevents flatlines

Also avoid disputed claim buckets in closes:
- no side effects / fewer side effects
- no suppression / fertility preserved
- hematocrit or estrogen guaranteed unchanged
- natural/circadian mimicry
- guaranteed 30/60/90-day outcomes
- benefits do not fade / works forever
- superiority vs injections or other TRT routes
