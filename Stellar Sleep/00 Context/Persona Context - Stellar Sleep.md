## The Skeptical Psychology Convert — "I didn't think an app could rewire my brain, but it did"

**Audience share:** 40.7% of reviews (50 tagged). Primary persona. Precision: see Review Analysis §4.
**Top 3 pains:** Tried every physical fix with no lasting results, skeptical that psychology could address a sleep problem, worried other apps are just sound machines

---

### Part 1 — How Stellar Sleep Connects

This persona arrives convinced insomnia is a physical problem — a broken sleep mechanism requiring a physical solution. They've tried pills, supplements, noise machines, sleep hygiene routines. When those fail, they reach a point where they reluctantly try "the psychology thing." Stellar Sleep's CBT-I mechanism directly addresses the cognitive and behavioral drivers of insomnia that physical fixes ignore: catastrophic thinking about sleep, conditioned arousal in bed, hypervigilance at night. The app's Harvard provenance provides the scientific credibility this persona needs to give psychology a fair shot. The 10-minutes-a-day structure reduces the perceived investment required to test the hypothesis. What converts them is the 2-week inflection point — when they start actually sleeping and realize the mechanism is real, not placebo.

**Source:** Product Context — mechanism and proof sections; reviews.jsonl Skeptical Psychology Convert cluster.

This persona is defined by exhaustion — not just sleep deprivation but the exhaustion of trying. They've spent years, sometimes decades, systematically testing every available intervention: melatonin, Ambien, sleep hygiene, meditation, exercise, restriction schedules. Each failed. The app's core message — chronic insomnia is a psychological condition that requires a psychological treatment — is precisely what the Everything-Failer has been missing. The Harvard credibility plus the clinical framing makes this feel like a medical intervention rather than another wellness product to disappoint them.

## The Sleep Anxiety Spiraler — "I wasn't just awake — I was dreading bedtime hours before it happened"

**Audience share:** 16.3% of reviews (20 tagged). Secondary persona. Precision: see Review Analysis §4.
**Top 3 pains:** Fear of bedtime / dreading the night ahead, mind that won't shut off despite physical exhaustion, anxiety about not sleeping that makes not sleeping worse

---

### Part 1 — How Stellar Sleep Connects

The Sleep Anxiety Spiraler's insomnia is a feedback loop: they can't sleep → they fear not sleeping → the fear makes them unable to sleep. Unlike the Everything-Failer, these users often identify the psychological component themselves ("I knew it had become mental at this point"). They arrive knowing they need something that works on the brain — they just haven't found the right tool. Meditation apps don't work because the anxious mind isn't calmed by soundscapes; the cognitive component is too active. CBT-I's specific techniques — cognitive restructuring for sleep-related catastrophic thoughts, paradoxical intention to break the effort-cycle, sleep restriction to rebuild genuine sleep pressure — are precisely matched to this persona's actual mechanism. The results rate for this persona is exceptional: 4.80 avg rating, nearly all 5★, and the language in positive reviews is consistently "changed my life" level.

### Part 1 — How Stellar Sleep Connects

The Overwhelmed New Parent's insomnia was triggered by a specific external event — young children — but the problem outlasts the original cause. Even on nights when the baby sleeps, they can't. That's CBT-I's domain: the conditioned hyperarousal that persists after the stressor. Unlike the Chronic Everything-Failer, this persona hasn't been fighting insomnia for years — but the timing is catastrophic. The 10-minutes-a-day structure is non-negotiable for this persona — anything requiring more time will be abandoned. The structured daily sequence (versus a content library to browse) removes decision overhead from someone already depleted. Results at 2 weeks matter because they can't sustain effort without early evidence.
