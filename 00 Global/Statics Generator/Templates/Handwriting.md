# Handwriting

## What this format is

Handwritten note format where a casual, first-person message on a physical writing surface is the hero element. The product is present but secondary -- the handwriting does the selling. Wins when the brand voice is personal, founder-driven, or community-intimate. The scroll-stopper is the cognitive break: everything else in the feed is typeset, and this looks like a human wrote it by hand. GPT Image 2 only -- NB2 cannot render convincing handwriting.

## Styles

### Style A: Post-It Note

A yellow Post-It note stuck to the product or surface next to it. The handwritten message is the dominant visual element. Feels like a human sent it -- a founder's recommendation, a customer's tip, a friend's "try this." The smallest, most casual format in the set.

#### Copy template

- **Handwritten note:** All lowercase, casual, under 20 words. Written in first person as if from the founder or a customer. End with a signature initial (e.g., "xo B" or "-- B").
- **CTA button:** 2-4 words, ALL CAPS.
- **Kicker:** Context line, ALL CAPS, small monospace (e.g., "HANDWRITTEN BY [NAME]" or "[PRODUCT] IN [SHADE COUNT] SHADES").

#### Strategic inputs

- **BRAND NAME** — from Brand Context
- **PRODUCT NAME** — from Product Context
- **PERSONA** — pain-trigger name, never demographic
- **AWARENESS STAGE** — from Creative Strategy Matrix
- **THREE SELVES TARGET** — Actual / Ideal / Ought
- **PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION** — from brand brief or Visual Style Card

### Style B: Handwritten Letter

Same concept elevated to a personal letter. A sheet of cream letter paper on a warm wooden kitchen table, cursive handwriting in black ink. Slightly longer copy (up to 30 words). Feels like finding a personal letter from someone who cares about you -- more intimate and considered than the Post-It. Good for founder stories, emotional hooks, and gratitude-driven messaging.

#### Copy template

- **Handwritten note:** Cursive, casual but slightly more composed than the Post-It. Under 30 words. Written in first person. End with a full first-name signature (e.g., "with love, Brianna" or "yours, Marco").
- **CTA button:** 2-4 words, ALL CAPS.
- **Kicker:** Context line, ALL CAPS, small monospace.

### Style C: Whiteboard

A small white whiteboard leaning against a warm parchment wall. Black dry-erase marker. Block handwriting. More informal and casual than the letter -- good for playful or educational hooks. The whiteboard signals "someone just wrote this for you" with a teaching-slash-sharing energy.

#### Copy template

- **Handwritten note:** Block letters, casual, under 15 words. Can include simple drawn elements (arrows, underlines, circles around key words). Signature optional -- skip if the message is educational rather than personal.
- **CTA button:** 2-4 words, ALL CAPS.
- **Kicker:** Context line, ALL CAPS, small monospace.

## Global rules for Handwriting format

- **Handwriting must look human.** Natural ink pressure variation, imperfect letter heights, slight line drift, letter-spacing inconsistency. NEVER uniform spacing. NEVER a handwriting font. The entire format breaks if the handwriting looks computer-generated.
- **Notes are first-person and casual.** Written as if from the founder, a customer, or a friend. "try this. it actually works. -- B" not "This product delivers results."
- **Under 20 words for Post-It and Whiteboard, under 30 for Letter.** Longer kills the format. The brevity is the hook.
- **Surface extends to all edges.** The Post-It / paper / whiteboard sits ON the surface. The surface is the continuous background plane per the E+ Safe Zone Block rules.
- **Product is secondary.** The handwritten note is the hero. Product is present for brand anchoring but does not compete with the note for attention.
- **Signature is mandatory for Post-It and Letter.** Post-It gets an initial ("xo B", "-- B"). Letter gets a full first name ("with love, Brianna"). Whiteboard can skip signature if the message is educational rather than personal.
- **Physical surface objects are the exception.** Post-It notes, letter paper, and whiteboards are allowed despite the E+ Safe Zone Block's no-paper rule. They are the intended subject of the format. The rule still applies to the background -- no inset cards, borders, or frames behind or around the writing surface.
- **GPT Image 2 only.** NB2 cannot render convincing handwriting. Do not attempt this format with NanoBanana 2.
