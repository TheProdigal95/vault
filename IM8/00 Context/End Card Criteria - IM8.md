# End Card Criteria — IM8

Derived from T003 end card batch (30+ selected compositions across 14 concepts). These rules codify what worked and what got rejected, so future batches maintain brand identity while pushing visual variety.

---

## 1. The 5-Layer Vertical Stack

Every IM8 end card follows this top-to-bottom structure. The layers and their proportions are non-negotiable — visual variety happens *within* this framework, not outside it.

| Layer | Content | % of Frame |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **A — Authority** | IM8 logo, capsule label ("CLINICALLY PROVEN", "OUR PROMISE"), or press row | 5–8% |
| **B — Headline** | Primary headline + sub-headline | 20–35% |
| **C — Hero Visual** | B-roll window, product display, flat-lay, stat number, or composite | 25–45% |
| **D — Proof Zone** | 3 trust/ingredient pills or badges (occasionally 2x2 for 4 items) | 10–15% |
| **E — CTA + Disclaimer** | Full-width button, disclaimer text, IM8 wordmark | 10–12% |

Remaining ~5–8% is breathing room (top margin above A, bottom margin below E, gaps between layers). Nothing touches the frame edges.

**The weight shifts by archetype.** Some cards are visual-led (B-roll window or flat-lay dominates at 40–45%), others are text-led (headline or giant stat number dominates at 30–35%, with a smaller visual below). Both are valid. The rule is that every card has one clear visual anchor — but it's not always the photography. A giant "90" or "$2.61" can be the anchor on a typographic card. A long confrontational headline can be the anchor on a stat-reframe card. What matters is hierarchy: one thing dominates, everything else supports.

## 2. Two Base Palettes + Three Texture Worlds

### Base palettes

| Palette | Background | Text | CTA Button | Badge Style |
|---------|-----------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Light** | Warm cream #F5F2F3 | Dark maroon #4A282D / #4A1A22 | Dark maroon bg, white text | White rounded pills, subtle shadow, dark maroon text |
| **Dark** | Deep maroon #4A1A22 | White / cream #E0DEDD | Cream bg, dark maroon text | Darker maroon #5B2931 cards, inner shadow, white text |

### Texture worlds (for variety within and across batches)

| World | Background | Text | Feeling |
|-------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Blue molecular** | Deep blue + translucent glass bubble structures, spherical shapes | White, with dark blue inside seals/badges | Scientific, clinical, trust |
| **Red organic** | Mushroom gill macro texture, vivid red-coral ridges filling frame | White, with maroon inside seals | Alive, warm, premium-natural |
| **Terracotta** | Warm red-brown tiles with grout lines, natural daylight, real shadows | White with drop shadows for legibility | Editorial, tactile, warm |

**Batch diversity rule:** No more than 3 consecutive cards in the same base palette. Each batch of 6+ end cards should use at least 2 base palettes and 1 texture world. Batches of 10+ should use all 5 color systems.

## 3. Eight Composition Types

Visual variety comes from switching between these compositions while maintaining the 5-layer stack. Each type has distinct rules.

### 3A — B-Roll Hero Window (the foundation)
- Photography window takes ~40% of frame
- 18px rounded corners
- On flat backgrounds: clean edges, no border or subtle shadow
- On textured backgrounds: subtle white border glow, floating-on-top depth
- B&W photography for confrontational/symptom-specific cards (dramatic)
- Color photography for aspirational/trust/lifestyle cards
- Window can be swapped for video — always design with animation in mind

### 3B — Product Bundle Display
- Products arranged on flat cream surface
- Occupies ~40–50% of frame
- Warm studio lighting, slightly elevated 3/4 angle
- Each gift item gets a small white price tag (rounded rectangle, dark text)
- No window or frame — products sit directly on the background

### 3C — Overhead Flat-Lay
- Camera looking straight down at a surface
- Surface provides the texture (terracotta tiles, marble, wood — never flat color)
- Products, glass of drink, sachets, mint leaves scattered naturally
- Natural daylight casting real diagonal shadows
- White text overlays with drop shadows for legibility
- Feels like editorial magazine photography

### 3D — Typographic Hero
- A single giant stat number or word IS the visual ("90", "30%", "80%", "$2.61")
- Number takes ~30–40% of frame height
- No photographs, no product shots, no people
- Serif numerals for elegant feel, sans-serif for bold/direct
- Background is either flat dark maroon or maroon-with-subtle-cellular-texture

### 3E — Portrait-Over-Texture Composite
- Person blended INTO the organic texture (semi-transparent edge blending)
- The portrait and texture MERGE — not a window, not a cutout
- Warm golden light on skin, texture bleeds through at edges
- Minimal text — let the visual composite breathe
- Reserve for brand-campaign-level cards, not utility cards

### 3F — Textured Background + Floating Window
- Organic/molecular texture fills entire frame as background
- B-roll window floats on top with subtle glow border
- Combines the richness of texture with the animation-ready window
- Best approach for offer cards that need visual polish + video capability

### 3G — Textured Background + Seal/List
- Organic texture as full background
- Typography or a circular seal/badge as the hero element (no window)
- Works for guarantee cards, authority cards, simple offer lists
- The texture provides all visual richness; the typography provides the message

### 3H — Infographic Grid
- 2x2 or 2x3 card grid as the hero element
- Each cell contains an icon/number + label
- Requires premium execution: refined typography, intentional size variation between cells (one anchor cell can be larger), brand-native color palette within the grid
- Works for full-offer-stack cards and multi-benefit summaries
- The grid must feel like an editorial data visualization, not a SaaS feature comparison — execution quality is what separates these

**Batch composition rule:** Each batch of 6+ end cards should use at least 3 distinct composition types. No more than 4 consecutive cards of the same type.

## 4. Headline Typography

### Two type registers

| Register | Typeface | Use for | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Bold sans-serif** | All-caps, stacked, wide letter-spacing | Stat-led, confrontational, clinical | "JOINT STIFFNESS DURING MENOPAUSE IS NOT INEVITABLE." |
| **Elegant serif** | Mixed case, classic proportion | Emotional, testimonial, promise, cost | "Better Sleep.", "Risk-Free.", "My Doctor Asked What Changed" |

### Rules
- Headlines take ~20–30% of frame, never more
- Statement headlines end with a period
- Sub-headlines use the opposite weight from the headline (serif headline → sans-serif sub, and vice versa)
- Sub-headlines are always a step down in contrast: muted gray #806A6D on light bg, off-white #E0DEDD on dark bg
- On textured backgrounds, headlines get subtle drop shadows for legibility
- On flat backgrounds, no shadows needed

## 5. Proof Zone Badges

Always 3 items in a horizontal row. Exception: 2x2 grid (4 items) only for full-offer-stack cards.

### Three badge styles

| Context | Style |
|---------|-------|
| Light flat bg | White rounded pills, subtle drop shadow, dark maroon text, optional small icon on left |
| Dark flat bg | Darker maroon #5B2931 rounded rectangles, inner shadow, white text |
| Textured bg | White rounded pills, subtle shadow, dark text — floating on the texture |

### Content types
- **Ingredient badges:** Dosage-first ("MSM 1,500MG", "COQ10 100MG", "SAFFRON 30MG")
- **Trust badges:** Certification-first ("NSF CERTIFIED", "CLINICALLY PROVEN", "6,000+ REVIEWS")
- **Offer badges:** Value-first ("$72 WELCOME GIFTS", "FREE SHIPPING", "90-DAY GUARANTEE")

Never mix badge content types on the same card. Ingredient cards get ingredient badges. Trust cards get trust badges. Offer cards get offer badges.

---

## 6. CTA Button

- Full width with side margins (~10% inset from each edge)
- ~30px rounded corners
- Single line of bold uppercase text
- Light bg → dark maroon #4A1A22 button, white text
- Dark bg → cream/white button, dark maroon text
- Blue bg → white button, dark blue text
- Always second-to-last element (disclaimer below, wordmark at very bottom)
- CTA text matches the card's intent: "TRY IM8 TODAY" (general), "CLAIM YOUR GIFT" (offer), "TRY IM8 RISK-FREE" (guarantee), "GET STARTED" (value), "START YOUR TRIAL" (clinical)

## 7. Human Subjects

| Card type | Gender | Age | Setting | Photography |
|-----------|--------|-----|---------|------------|
| Menopause-specific | Women | 40s+ | Gym, running, yoga, post-workout | B&W for symptom cards, color for aspirational |
| General/sport | Men or women | 30s+ | Gym (deadlifts, bar), trail running, studio | Color for trust, B&W for stat-led |
| Offer/lifestyle | Women | 30s–40s | Kitchen, home, morning routine | Always color, warm natural light |
| Testimonial | Women | 40s | Kitchen (pouring IM8), candid moment | Always color, editorial style |

### Non-negotiables
- Always fit, healthy, confident — never tired, struggling, or sick-looking
- Athletic activity shown is always powerful and controlled (pull-ups, deadlifts, running mid-stride, yoga hold — never sitting or resting)
- Warm golden light on skin, especially in composite and lifestyle cards
- When composited into texture: semi-transparent edge blending, figure clearly visible but texture shows through at edges

## 8. Product Photography

- **Red packaging is the product signature.** IM8 box, sachets, water bottle, storage case, frother — all in the brand's red maroon
- **Glass of red-pink liquid** anchors the "daily ritual" signal — include whenever space allows
- Product as secondary element: ~15–25% of frame, centered with generous margins
- Product as hero (flat-lay): ~40–60% of frame, spread across the surface
- Warm-toned product photography, even against cool blue molecular backgrounds (the temperature contrast is intentional)
- Price tags for individual items: small white rounded rectangles with dark text, positioned near each item

---

## 9. Capsule Labels

Small rounded-rectangle labels that sit between the logo and the headline. Used for authority framing.

| Label | Use for |
|-------|---------|
| "CLINICALLY PROVEN" | Clinical stat cards |
| "12 WEEK CLINICAL TRIAL" | Specific trial reference cards |
| "OUR PROMISE" | Guarantee / risk-reversal cards |
| "YOUR FIRST ORDER INCLUDES" | Full offer stack cards |
| "AS FEATURED IN" + press logos | Media authority cards |

Style: Thin border (red #CD3F3C on dark bg, dark maroon on light bg), transparent fill, small uppercase text. Never large — this is a qualifier, not a headline.

---

## 10. Disclaimer & Wordmark

- FDA disclaimer present on every card with health claims: *"These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."*
- Offer condition asterisks: "*90 Day Subscribers.", "*Quarterly plan.", "*New customers only."
- Small, muted, never competing with CTA
- IM8 wordmark centered at very bottom, small, serving as brand stamp
- On textured backgrounds: wordmark in white with no tagline
- On flat backgrounds: wordmark in the opposite contrast color

## 11. What Gets Rejected (Anti-Patterns)

These patterns were tried and cut during T003 iteration. Don't repeat them.

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Failed |
|-------------|--------------|
| Offer cards that look identical to non-offer cards | Offer cards need their own visual identity — different compositions, not just different headlines on the same template |
| Color monotony (4+ consecutive maroon/cream cards) | The brand has 5 color systems; using only 2 reads as lazy, not on-brand |
| Poorly executed infographic grids (generic icons, equal-weight cells, no visual hierarchy) | The grid format itself is valid — but it demands premium execution: refined typography, intentional sizing variation between cells, and the same production value as any other IM8 composition. A grid that reads as a SaaS pricing page instead of a wellness brand failed on craft, not format |
| Offer messaging buried under artistic composites | If the offer isn't immediately readable at phone size, the card failed its job |
| Portrait composites for utility cards | The composite technique is editorial brand art — using it for a "$72 welcome gift" card wastes the technique and loses the offer |
| No IM8 logo / unrecognizable palette | Every card must be immediately identifiable as IM8 even without reading the text |
| Flat maroon background on every offer card | Offer cards especially benefit from texture worlds — flat maroon makes them look like an afterthought |
| Gold stars, 3D effects, glossy badges | IM8 is premium and flat. Any 3D rendering, gloss, or heavy gold reads as infomercial |

## 12. Batch Planning Checklist

Before generating a batch of end cards, verify:

- [ ] At least 2 base palettes (light + dark) are represented
- [ ] At least 1 texture world (blue molecular, red organic, or terracotta) is used
- [ ] At least 3 composition types are used across the batch
- [ ] No more than 3 consecutive cards share the same palette
- [ ] No more than 4 consecutive cards share the same composition type
- [ ] Every card follows the 5-layer stack (authority → headline → hero → proof → CTA)
- [ ] Hero visual is the largest single element on every card
- [ ] Badge content type matches card intent (ingredients / trust / offer)
- [ ] CTA text matches card intent
- [ ] IM8 logo or wordmark is present and prominent on every card
- [ ] FDA disclaimer present on cards with health claims
- [ ] Offer cards are visually distinct from non-offer cards in the same batch

## 13. Content Filter Notes (GPT Image 2)

When generating via GPT Image 2 / fal.ai:

**Blocked:** Specific media brand names in endorsement context ("NBC", "Today", "Fortune", "Fast Company"), certain gym equipment descriptions ("cable exercises"), CGI gut/intestinal system overlays

**Workarounds:** Generic press language ("AS FEATURED IN" + "simplified media logo silhouettes"), outdoor settings instead of specific gym equipment, generic authority headlines ("THE SUPPLEMENT MAKING HEADLINES")

**Product rendering:** Use pre-generated B-roll scenes as reference images, not raw product photos. GPT Image 2 reinterprets raw photos unpredictably; using scenes that already contain the product in context produces more accurate results.
