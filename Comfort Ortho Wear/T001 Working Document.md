## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

Split the concepts in:
* 8 videos
* 7 statics

### Batch Constraints

- Educational formats must have a structural backbone to increase retention — a countdown, a competition, or an explicit promise that pays off at the end. No "interesting info" without a reason to stay.
- Single-focus per concept in 3 concepts: one mechanism per video (arch only, or wide toe only, or slip-resistance only). Remaining concepts can use wider feature variety.
- Past-perfect milestone framing for the benefits timeline: "your arch HAS rebuilt its natural support" not "your arch starts feeling stronger."

### Video Structures to Test (8 concepts)

- Benefits timeline of 60 days (for nurses)
- 1 text wall / B-roll + caption ad (similar to Elevate)
- AI Nurse Character greenscreen video (similar to IM8 menopause ones)
- Ranked comparison — 3 competitors (Hoka, Nike, $300 orthotics) → ComfortWear wins. Shorter, tighter version of what's spending $70K in the account.

### Static Formats to Test (7 concepts)

- Comparison table — ComfortWear $60 vs. Hoka $165 vs. Nike $120 vs. $300 orthotics. Static version of the ranked comparison format.
- Persona-label + feature pills / symptom self-ID checklist — "12-HOUR SHIFT SHOES" headline + condition pills (Plantar Fasciitis ✅ / 12-Hour Shifts ✅ / Wide Feet ✅). Builds on the 1.62–1.67 ROAS static already in-account.
- Native screenshot (Facebook comment format for cold) — unedited nurse reviews as native-looking comment screenshots + primary text selling underneath. Port from the 3.30 ROAS RTG format.
- Search bar format — "Best shoes for plantar fasciitis 2026" → ComfortWear = #1 result. Ranking insight in a single-scan static.
- VoC editorial / quote pull — authentic nurse review in editorial style, minimal branding.
- "Don't X unless Y" reverse psychology — "Don't buy these shoes unless you stand for 12+ hours." Pattern interrupt that pre-qualifies.
- 1 additional format TBD based on what emerges from the batch plan.

### Personas and Messaging Focus

- Nurses doing 12-Hour shifts
	- Helping them with foot pain
	- Asking 500 nurses about plantar fasciitis and foot pain — great hook
	- First-person narrative about having solved foot pain — at least 3 concepts
	- Gifting/social frame — "my best friend on the floor made me try the shoes that fixed my foot pain for good" / nurse-to-nurse recommendation angle

### Visual Formats for Hooks

- Split screen of nurse walking and CGI of foot with red flashes of pain (similar to IM8)
- Split screen of nurse and animated foot anatomy overlays (X-ray arch support, wide toe box visualization)

### Verbal Hooks to Include in the Mix (not exhaustive — also need curiosity-gap, result-first, and data hooks)

- How this nurse stopped limping to her car after 12-hour shifts
- I'm a nurse working 12-hour shifts and my best friend on the floor made me try the shoes that fixed my foot pain for good (gifting/social frame)
- Sneakers are making your foot pain worse and you don't even know it, that's why nurses and 12-hour shift workers are switching to this
- $60 vs. $300 podiatrist orthotics — why paying less can mean better foot pain relief

### Creative Elements to Include

- Social proof: screenshots popping up in some videos, test in 3 concepts
- $300 orthotics anchor
- Competitor names mentioned in VO with logos blurred in footage (per account pattern)

## Script Criteria

> **Baseline:** All rules in `00 Global/Criteria/` apply automatically. Read Universal Copy Rules + the format-specific doc(s) for this batch before writing.

### Compliance

No dedicated compliance/guardrails file in `00 Context/` — use only brand-positioned claims confirmed in Product Context and Brand Context. Key guardrails:

- **Approved trust badge:** "500,000+ Happy Customers" — exact phrasing only
- **Approved guarantee language:** "Feel the difference in 45 days, or get 100% of your money back." — exact phrasing only
- **Approved authority framing:** "Doctor-recommended comfort" and "Designed to have orthopedic-like support" — brand-positioned, not clinical claims
- **Named conditions (PDP-approved):** Foot Pain, Plantar Fasciitis, Bunions, Neuropathy, Flat Feet, Hammer Toes, Morton's Neuroma, Tendonitis, Back Pain
- **HSA/FSA eligible** — approved claim
- **Slip-resistant outsole** — confirmed for hospitals, kitchens, outdoor paths (FAQ-sourced)
- **No named clinical studies, no named doctors, no certifications** — none exist on-page; do not fabricate
- **"Free returns"** — never use without noting exceptions (HI, PR, AK, GU, VI, and international excluded)
- **Discount claims** — confirm current active promotion before scripting; "40% OFF" is active as of 2026-04-23 but subject to change

### Product Specifics

**Three hero mechanisms (named on PDP):**
1. **ORTHO-WEAR Heel-Hugging Tech** — absorbs shock, softens each step, reduces pressure on heel
2. **ULTIMATE INSOLES** — lifts and stabilizes arch, relieves joint stress, improves foot alignment
3. **Wide Toe Box** — toes spread naturally, reduces discomfort, all-day wearability

**Material:** Breathable Stretch-Fit knit — accommodates swelling, bunions, wide feet without pressure points

**Outsole:** Slip-resistant rubber — ideal for hospitals, kitchens, outdoor paths

**Sizing note:** Brand recommends half a size up — acknowledge in scripts for Long-Shift persona if fit objection surfaces

### Platform Constraints

- **Platform:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram)
- **Video duration:** 60–120 seconds. Top performers in this account: 1:00–1:51. The 2:17 origin story (0.98 ROAS) confirms longer formats break without tight structure.
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 primary (Reels/Stories), 1:1 for feed placements
- **Statics:** 1080×1080 (feed) or 1080×1920 (Stories)
- **End card:** Standardized — brand logo + 3-4 shoe colorways + badge stack (45-day guarantee, free shipping, doctor-recommended). Handled by editor, not scripted in detail.

### Caption Style Reference (from Top Spenders)

| Style | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Bold ALL-CAPS, verbatim VO mirroring | Ranked comparisons, educational walkthroughs | "NUMBER 1: COMFORTWEAR ORTHO STRETCH SCORES 9.2 OUT OF 10" |
| Orange/yellow highlight for scores and offers | Score reveals, price anchors, discount callouts | "9.2 OUT OF 10" / "40% OFF" / "$60" in colored text |
| Native social captions (lowercase, casual) | First-person narratives, UGC-style, B-roll + caption | "I spent $60 on these shoes for my nursing job" |
| Persistent single overlay (no VO) | B-roll + caption format | One caption block on screen for entire duration |
| Two-tier: sans-serif narration + bold keyword pop | Mechanism explainers, AI greenscreen | Standard text for narration, BOLD SERIF for key terms that pop in |

**Rules:**
- Every video runs persistent on-screen captions — no format in this account succeeds without them
- Captions never repeat VO verbatim when both channels are active — two channels, two messages
- Competitor shoe names captioned but logos blurred in footage

### Editing Styles (from Top Spenders + IM8 Learnings)

- **Shot pacing:** 2-3 seconds per shot. Fast cuts, no transitions. Motion in first frame of every shot.
- **Competitor treatment:** Named in VO and captions, logos blurred in footage. Each competitor gets one specific failure shown (Skechers = foam compresses, Hoka = too heavy/bulky, Nike = too narrow/made for running).
- **Animated overlays:** Foot anatomy (X-ray arch support, toe-spread arrows, pain-point red glow) for mechanism beats. 3D bone/joint overlays on product shots.
- **Color-coded states:** B&W or desaturated for pain visualization. Full color, vibrant for solution/relief moments. (Ported from IM8 top spenders.)
- **Social proof screenshots:** Review comments pop up over B-roll mid-video. 2-3 screenshots per concept where used. Authentic grammar/formatting preserved.
- **End card (standardized):** Brand logo → 3-4 shoe colorways in a row → badge stack (💰 45 Days Money-Back Guarantee / ✈️ Free Shipping / ⚕️ Doctor-Recommended). Editor handles, not scripted.
- **Audio:** Background music for B-roll/educational formats. No music for UGC/first-person (authenticity). 110-120 BPM upbeat instrumental for timelines.

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

1. Guarantee language exact: "45 days, 100% money back" — not "30 days" or "60 days"
2. Trust badge exact: "500,000+ Happy Customers" — not "half a million" or "thousands"
3. Approved conditions only — no conditions beyond the PDP list
4. No fabricated doctor names, study citations, or certifications
5. Slip-resistance claim tied to setting (hospitals/kitchens/outdoor) — not generic "all surfaces"
6. Pain-first language — every script opens with the felt pain before naming mechanisms
7. Discount claims verified against current active promotion at time of writing
8. Single-focus concepts stay single-focus — don't let agents add other mechanisms into the body
9. Competitors named in VO, logos blurred in footage (match account pattern)
10. Hook caption variety — not all hooks in the batch use the same angle (all pain, all data, all curiosity). Mix across the 4 proven patterns: pain-first, curiosity-gap, data/survey, result-first
11. Social proof screenshots sourced from real reviews — never fabricated or paraphrased into perfection. Authentic grammar preserved
12. Product named before the close — ComfortWear must appear in body at minimum. Close alone is insufficient
