## Competitor 1: Function Health

**Trustpilot:** 4.0/5, ~772 reviews
**What they do:** Comprehensive biomarker testing (110+ markers). No clinician consults, no prescriptions, no coaching. Data-only.
**Price:** ~$499/year

### Fatal Flaw: "So What Do I Do Now?"

The single most common complaint across all Function Health negative reviews is that the service gives you data but no actionable guidance. This is the exact gap Lifeforce fills.

### Theme A: No Clinical Guidance — Just Data

> "I have no idea what to do next." — James Sapp, 1★

> "Expecting more advice on treatment of blood levels that were not in range." — Mike, 3★

> "So, what am I supposed to do next? What do I do now that I know my results? That's what's lacking in this service." — Cori Avery, 3★

> "The clinician notes merely analyze test pros/cons, ultimately recommending physician consultation." — P.S., 3★

> "I guess I need a bit of simplification or some sort of plan to work through." — Christine D Osborn, 3★

> "Expected deeper insights but received pretty basic recommendations comparable to primary care guidance." — Sophia Yelkin, 3★

> "TOO EXPENSIVE. You get data but no one to help you understand what to do with it." — Gene Ferry, 3★

**Lifeforce positioning angle:** "Function Health tells you what's wrong. Lifeforce tells you what's wrong AND gives you a doctor, a plan, and the prescriptions to fix it."

### Theme B: AI-Generated Reports, Not Real Doctors

> "Low quality AI reports — glaring inaccuracies that made me think it was AI generated and not carefully reviewed by a human." — Andrew, 1★

> "Shift to AI medical summaries reduced value. Prefers human clinician analysis over algorithmic insights." — Megan Mitchell, 2★

> "All of it is an AI language model." — John McGee, 2★

> "Clinician feedback appears AI-generated rather than from actual healthcare providers." — Jose, 3★

> "Assessment is a lot of canned information." — "No Thank You", 2★

> "Data is interesting, clinicians report is unhelpful. Customer support directs users to AI rather than medical professionals." — Emma K, 2★

**Lifeforce positioning angle:** "Your health deserves a real doctor, not an AI summary. Lifeforce gives you 45 minutes with a board-certified clinician, not an algorithm."

### Theme C: Follow-Up Tests Are a Downgrade

> "Function Health is misleading in that for the second blood test you only get 25% of the biomarkers that were in the first test." — John McGee, 2★

> "Follow-up exams excluded markers with poor initial results, preventing progress tracking. Additional marker inclusion requires extra payment." — María del Pilar, 3★

> "Second test, the provider omitted half the requested tests." — Kate White, 3★

**Lifeforce positioning angle:** "Lifeforce tests 50+ biomarkers EVERY quarter. Not 25% on the follow-up. Every test, every time."

### Theme D: No Prescription Access

Function Health can identify problems but cannot prescribe solutions. Members with out-of-range hormones, cholesterol, or metabolic markers are told to "see your doctor."

**Lifeforce positioning angle:** "We don't just diagnose. We treat. Supplements, hormones, peptides, and pharmaceuticals — prescribed by your clinician and delivered to your door."
