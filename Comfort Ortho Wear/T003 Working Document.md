## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

T003 is evergreen video only. We are planning the 15 delegated videos first; no Memorial Day, no seasonal-sale concepts, no static concepts in this planning pass.

### Data Read → Allocation Decisions

| Signal | What It Means | T003 Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Pain Elimination is the highest-spend scaling Motion angle | The account is still buying direct relief from foot / heel / back pain, not broad comfort | Give nurse / long-shift pain the largest block: **6 of 15 videos** |
| Authenticity & Trust is scaling fastest by WoW | The market needs proof that a $60 orthopedic-style shoe is real, not another cheap comfort ad | Give trust/proof a dedicated block: **3 of 15 videos** |
| Affordability & Value is also scaling fast | Price is not just an offer; it is a persuasion angle against Hokas, replacement cycles, and orthotics | Give value a dedicated block: **3 of 15 videos** |
| Designed For Professionals is scaling | Nurse specificity should remain the credibility engine even when the angle is value or proof | Keep nurse / shift context threaded through the first 12 concepts |
| Travel spend is tiny but owner wants more testing | Travel is not data-justified as a co-primary angle, but T002 showed a strong travel story performer | Keep travel controlled: **3 of 15 videos**, not 4-5 |
| Limited Time Offer is not scaling meaningfully for evergreen | Memorial Day already ran; seasonal urgency would muddy the read | **0 seasonal videos** in T003 |
| Retargeting quick-action works but is not the current strategic ask | RTG is useful, but the owner-requested third travel concept needs the slot | **0 dedicated RTG videos** in the delegated batch |

### Final Split

| Bucket | Count | Why This Count |
|---|---:|---|
| Nurse / long-shift pain elimination | 6 | Core spend signal + strongest historical persona; enough room to test multiple structures without starving other scaling angles |
| Authenticity / trust / social proof | 3 | Dedicated proof block because trust is scaling; enough concepts to test review proof, skepticism, and receipt-style validation |
| Affordability / value | 3 | Dedicated value block because value is scaling; enough concepts to test $60 vs Hokas, $300 orthotics, and replacement-cycle economics |
| Travel evergreen | 3 | Owner-requested expansion; enough to test travel seriously while respecting weak current Motion spend |

### What Each Bucket Must Prove

- **Nurse / long-shift pain** — prove we can keep scaling pain relief without simply remaking the same wrong-shoe debunking ad.
- **Trust / proof** — prove skepticism can be converted with real reviews, imperfect proof, and "too cheap to be real" objection handling.
- **Value** — prove $60 can carry the body of a video, not just the close.
- **Travel** — prove travel works outside one winning story arc and without over-relying on the nurse-friend bridge.

### Format Decisions

- **Keep three-problem debunking** — but evolve the problem set beyond the same Hoka/Skechers/foam sequence.
- **Keep first-person narrative** — but make features appear as story beats, not lists.
- **Use one authority/rating format max in T003** — compliance risk is manageable only with advisory framing.
- **Use one caption-only / montage test** — useful for AI-native production, but not enough evidence to over-allocate.
- **Use one long-form timeline max** — strong engagement potential, but past origin/history executions got too long before product reveal.

### Exclusions

- **Cap nurse-friend/coworker discovery** — use sparingly after T002 saturation.
- **Cap foam-collapse framing** — use durability, shift-test, and price/value frames too.
- **No founder story as a default layer** — use only where it directly answers price skepticism.
- **Evergreen CTAs only** — no Memorial Day, no deadline tied to 5/27.

## Concept Validation

> Runs after the Batch Plan is drafted, before script assignments begin. Each concept passes the 7 tests individually; the differentiation rule is a batch-level cross-check.

### 7-Test Quality Bar (per concept)

1. **Pain point specificity** — built around a specific human tension, not a product feature.
2. **Strategic coherence** — audience, message, structure, and hook work together.
3. **Differentiation** — couldn't be used by a competitor without modification.
4. **Format ambition** — visual format chosen intentionally, not by default.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — hook execution creates felt tension.
6. **Testable hypothesis** — grounded enough to succeed or fail clearly.
7. **Producible** — creative team can execute without major clarification.

### Differentiation Rule

Each concept must shift at least 2 of these 4 axes from every other concept in the batch:

- Intended audience / angle
- Messaging angle
- Visual format / structure
- Hook tactic

## Script Criteria

> **Baseline:** All rules in `00 Global/Criteria/` apply automatically. Read Universal Copy Rules + the format-specific doc(s) for this batch before writing.

### Compliance

See `00 Context/Compliance & Guardrails - Comfort Ortho Wear.md` for full rules. Key points:

- **Approved trust badge:** "500,000+ Happy Customers" — exact phrasing for on-screen text/captions. VO can reference the target audience (e.g., "Over 500,000 nurses and healthcare workers have already made the switch") since spoken language targets the viewer.
- **Approved guarantee language:** "Feel the difference in 45 days, or get 100% of your money back." — exact phrasing only
- **Approved authority framing:** "Expert-recommended" (approved by client). "Doctor-recommended comfort" and "Designed to have orthopedic-like support" — brand-positioned, not clinical claims. Avoid "podiatrist approved."
- **Named conditions (PDP-approved):** Foot Pain, Plantar Fasciitis, Bunions, Neuropathy, Flat Feet, Hammer Toes, Morton's Neuroma, Tendonitis, Back Pain
- **HSA/FSA eligible** — approved claim
- **Slip-resistant outsole** — confirmed for hospitals, kitchens, outdoor paths (FAQ-sourced)
- **No named clinical studies, no named doctors, no certifications** — none exist on-page; do not fabricate
- **"Free returns"** — never use without noting exceptions (HI, PR, AK, GU, VI, and international excluded)
- **Discount claims** — confirm current active promotion before scripting
- **Competitor brand names** — can be spoken in VO; must use # in on-screen text (e.g., "SK#TCHERS", "H#KAS"); logos must be blurred in footage

### Product Specifics

**Three hero mechanisms (named on PDP):**
1. **ORTHO-WEAR Heel-Hugging Tech** — absorbs shock, softens each step, reduces pressure on heel
2. **ULTIMATE INSOLES** — lifts and stabilizes arch, relieves joint stress, improves foot alignment
3. **Wide Toe Box** — toes spread naturally, reduces discomfort, all-day wearability

**Material:** Breathable Stretch-Fit knit — accommodates swelling, bunions, wide feet without pressure points

**Outsole:** Slip-resistant rubber — ideal for hospitals, kitchens, outdoor paths

**Sizing note:** Brand recommends half a size up — acknowledge in scripts for Long-Shift persona if fit objection surfaces

### Platform Constraints

- **Platform:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram)
- **Video duration:** 60–120 seconds. Top performers in this account: 1:00–1:51.
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 primary (Reels/Stories), 1:1 for feed placements
- **End card:** Standardized — brand logo + 3-4 shoe colorways + badge stack (45-day guarantee, free shipping, doctor-recommended). Editor handles, not scripted in detail.

### Caption Style Reference

| Style | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Bold ALL-CAPS, verbatim VO mirroring | Ranked comparisons, educational walkthroughs | "NUMBER 1: COMFORTWEAR ORTHO STRETCH SCORES 9.2 OUT OF 10" |
| Orange/yellow highlight for scores and offers | Score reveals, price anchors, discount callouts | "9.2 OUT OF 10" / "$60" |
| Native social captions | First-person narratives, UGC-style, B-roll + caption | "I spent $60 on these shoes for my nursing job" |
| Persistent single overlay | B-roll + caption format | One caption block on screen for entire duration |
| Two-tier: sans-serif narration + bold keyword pop | Mechanism explainers, AI greenscreen | Standard narration text + bold mechanism pops |

**Rules:**
- Every video runs persistent on-screen captions — no format in this account succeeds without them
- Captions never repeat VO verbatim when both channels are active — two channels, two messages
- Competitor shoe names captioned but logos blurred in footage

### Editing Styles

- **Shot pacing:** 2-3 seconds per shot. Fast cuts, no transitions. Motion in first frame of every shot.
- **Competitor treatment:** Named in VO and captions, logos blurred in footage. Each competitor gets one specific failure shown.
- **Animated overlays:** Foot anatomy, arch support, toe-spread arrows, pain-point red glow, and 3D joint overlays for mechanism beats.
- **Color-coded states:** B&W or desaturated for pain visualization. Full color, vibrant for solution/relief moments.
- **Social proof screenshots:** Review comments pop up over B-roll mid-video. Authentic grammar/formatting preserved.
- **Audio:** Background music for B-roll/educational formats. No music for UGC/first-person. 110-120 BPM upbeat instrumental for timelines.

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

1. Guarantee language exact: "Feel the difference in 45 days, or get 100% of your money back."
2. Trust badge exact: "500,000+ Happy Customers" — not "half a million" or "thousands"
3. Approved conditions only — no conditions beyond the PDP list
4. No fabricated doctor names, study citations, or certifications
5. Slip-resistance claim tied to setting (hospitals/kitchens/outdoor) — not generic "all surfaces"
6. Pain-first language — every script opens with felt pain before naming mechanisms
7. Discount claims verified against current active promotion at time of writing
8. Single-focus concepts stay single-focus — don't let agents add other mechanisms into the body
9. Competitors named in VO, logos blurred in footage, asterisked in on-screen text
10. Hook caption variety — not all hooks in the batch use the same angle
11. Social proof screenshots sourced from real reviews — never fabricated or paraphrased into perfection
12. Product named before the close — ComfortWear must appear in body at minimum
13. Founder story is optional in T003 — use only when it answers price skepticism or trust skepticism
14. Pacing within tolerance is a pass — VO at or below tolerance ceiling does not need flagging
15. ULTIMATE INSOLES comparison frame variety — max 3 scripts use foam-collapse framing
16. Nurse-friend/coworker discovery bridge — max 3 scripts in T003
17. Evergreen only — no Memorial Day, no dated sale windows, no 5/27 urgency
