# T003 — Elevate Meta Whitelisting Batch

---

## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

> T003 focuses on whitelisted persona pages (real-name accounts) using a combination of high-stakes long-form storytelling and native-feel iPhone photography. This mirrors the successful "workhorse" pattern from FitSleeps.

**Key Learnings from T002 & FitSleeps:**
- **Eligibility is the hook:** "Most people don't know they qualify" is the compliance-safe scroll-stop.
- **Payment plans = Conversion:** $0 down and payment plan logos ($58/week) must be present to close the ROAS gap.
- **Native imagery:** Photos must look like they were taken by a real person on an iPhone, not designed.
- **Emotional Arc:** Shift from "shame/food noise" to "pride/freedom" without claiming specific lbs lost.
- **Whitelisting:** Running from a person's page allows for First Person ("I") perspective, which increases trust and relatability.

## Core Story Beats (Internal Reference)

### Persona 1: Sarah Jenkins (The Postpartum Nurse)
1. **Hook:** "I didn't recognize the woman in the nursing station mirror."
2. **Incident:** Trying to fit into her pre-pregnancy scrubs for a 12-hour shift and the zipper breaking.
3. **Escalation:** Avoiding family photos, feeling like she's "failing" as a mom because she has no energy to play.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** "Healthy" meal prep (no time), caffeine addiction, expensive gym membership she never used.
5. **Discovery:** Coworker (another nurse) mentioned she was getting medical support delivered to her door.
6. **Mechanism:** 100% online medical review + personalized care plan + anti-nausea meds included.
7. **First Results:** Waking up without that "heavy" feeling. Fitting into those scrubs.
8. **Transformation:** Having energy for her son after a double shift. Confidence at work.
9. **Price Anchoring:** $58/week vs. the hundreds spent on supplements and wasted gym fees. $0 down with Afterpay.
10. **Close:** "I finally feel like *me* again. Check if you qualify."

### Persona 2: David Thompson (The Insurance-Frustrated Owner)
1. **Hook:** "My insurance agent told me my health wasn't 'a priority' for coverage."
2. **Incident:** Being quoted $1,200 at the pharmacy counter for the prescription his doctor wrote.
3. **Escalation:** Worrying about his heart, his kids, and not being around to see them grow up.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Trying every "fad" diet his employees mentioned. Weight loss "pills" from the drugstore.
5. **Discovery:** A buddy from a business mastermind group mentioned Elevate and the "no insurance" model.
6. **Mechanism:** No insurance red tape + direct-to-door shipping + provider oversight.
7. **First Results:** The mental "food noise" finally going quiet. Feeling "lighter" on his feet at the office.
8. **Transformation:** Playing catch in the yard without needing a break. Focus returning at work.
9. **Price Anchoring:** Programs from $58/week. Less than his monthly lunch meetings.
10. **Close:** "Don't let insurance dictate your health. See if you're eligible."

### Persona 3: Maria Garcia (The Menopause Teacher)
1. **Hook:** "I've taught for 25 years, but I couldn't learn how to fix my own metabolism."
2. **Incident:** Realizing she had bought a size she never thought she'd see in her closet.
3. **Escalation:** The constant "food noise" during class—thinking about lunch while teaching.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Weight watchers (for the 4th time), "hormone balancing" teas, extreme calorie counting.
5. **Discovery:** Reading a review from another teacher who felt the exact same "biological wall" at 50.

## Concept Validation

### 7-Test Quality Bar

1. **Pain point specificity** — Sarah: Nursing exhaustion + postpartum body disconnect. David: Career success vs. health failure + insurance red tape. Maria: Mental weight of food noise + biological "betrayal" at 50.
2. **Strategic coherence** — Whitelisted persona pages provide the "I" voice needed for these intimate stories.
3. **Differentiation** — Elevate's $0 down + payment plan focus is the conversion moat.
4. **Format ambition** — Long-form story (1,500+ words) is a deliberate choice for high-intent conversion.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — Uses "food noise" and "eligibility" as hooks rather than just "weight loss."
6. **Testable hypothesis** — Hypothesis: Long-form storytelling from whitelisted personas will outperform generic brand ads in ROAS for high-BMI segments.
7. **Producible** — AI-generated native photos are fast to produce.

## Script & Visual Criteria

**Baseline:** All rules in `00 Global/Criteria/` apply (Universal Copy Rules, Long-Form Primary Text Criteria).

**Brand-specific overrides:**
- **Whitelisting:** ALWAYS use First Person ("I", "my") for these personas.
- **Compliance (CRITICAL):** 
    - No drug names (GLP-1 is a category, not a product).
    - NO specific weight numbers (lbs, %).
    - Experience language only (happier, healthier, clothes fit, food noise gone).
    - Pricing: "Programs starting at $58/week."
    - Disclaimers: Designers add mandatory brand disclaimer to images.
- **Payment Plans:** Mention Affirm/Klarna/Afterpay and "$0 down" in every story (Beat 9/10).
- **Discovery:** Through a "friend who finally found a way" or "seeing someone else's transformation," never "searching online."

**Image Generation Rules (The "Gritty Native" Aesthetic):**
To ensure generated images don't look like smooth CGI, ALWAYS append this aesthetic block to the prompt:
> *"A raw, unedited low-quality iPhone photo... Harsh fluorescent lighting, heavy grain, pixelated, natural skin texture with visible imperfections and pores. Absolutely no smooth CGI texture, no airbrushing, no professional lighting, looks like a real messy native social media photo."*

**Character Consistency Strategy (For Whitelisting):**
To ensure Sarah, David, and Maria look like the same person across their 10 respective ads:
1. **The Base Portrait:** Generate and lock in a "Base Character Portrait" for the persona first.
2. **Image Referencing:** Pass this base portrait into the fal.ai generation script using the `--images [url]` flag to anchor the facial structure.
3. **Locked Trait Modifiers:** Define 4-5 highly specific physical traits (e.g., "Caucasian, messy dark brown hair in a low bun, heavy under-eye bags, light freckles") and include them verbatim in every prompt for that persona.
