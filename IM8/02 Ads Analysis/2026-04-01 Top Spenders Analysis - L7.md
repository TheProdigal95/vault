1. **The GLP-1 companion angle is a top performer.** Three of the eight top spenders target GLP-1 users specifically. This audience has high intent, specific pain points (nausea, hair loss, nutrient depletion), and is growing rapidly. Brief around this aggressively.

2. **Specificity beats vagueness every time.** Not "packed with vitamins" but "Vitamin D3 (250% DV), Magnesium Bisglycinate, MSM (1,500mg)." Not "lots of users" but "516,000 users and 11,723 five-star reviews." Every top spender uses concrete numbers and named ingredients.

4. **Whitelisted creator ads work at different production levels.** Dr. James (lo-fi whiteboard) and Jake Heyen (green screen commentary) are both top spenders. The key is not production quality but perceived authenticity and authority. Brief creators to educate, not pitch.

5. **The "overwhelm then simplify" structure is extremely powerful for an all-in-one product.** IM8 has 92 ingredients — use that complexity as an asset. Show the viewer how impossibly complicated it would be to do this themselves, then reveal IM8 as the single solution.

6. **Static images can compete with video when copy is sharp.** The three static ads work because they have strong copy hooks (Notes app disguise, narrative twist, clinical specificity). For AppLovin statics, invest in copy and concept, not just design.

7. **Every video ends with a proof barrage.** The last 20-30 seconds of top-performing videos stack: authority (NASA/Mayo Clinic), social proof (516K users), certification (NSF), guarantee (30-day refund), and offer (free welcome kit). This is the conversion closer — don't skip it.

8. **Native/disguised formats stop the scroll.** The Notes app image (Ad 05) and the "unusual endorsement story" framing (Ad 04) both work by not looking like ads initially. Brief creative that mimics organic content formats.
