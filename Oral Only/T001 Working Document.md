## Compliance Guardrails — Active for T001

Do not write ads that say or imply:

- No side effects / significantly fewer side effects / side effects will not appear.
- No suppression / fertility preserved / not tied to TRT for life.
- Hematocrit or estrogen remain unchanged as a guaranteed outcome.
- Benefits do not fade / works forever / perpetual effectiveness.
- Guaranteed 30/60/90-day outcomes for energy, sleep, clarity, strength, adaptation, body composition.
- Peer-reviewed research backs Oral Only unless the research is explicitly about Oral Only’s compounded formulation and delivery route.
- Superiority vs injections, Kyzatrex, or other TRT routes unless approved support exists.

Allowed / safer lanes:

- Physician-guided compounded oral TRT option for eligible men.
- Route/convenience: no needles, daily oral routine, online care, labs, consult, shipping.
- Customer/problem language around injection fatigue, protocol burden, delivery-curve curiosity.
- Dr. Jaquish’s teaching as education, not broad unsupported claim stacks.

Required framing note:

- Nandrogen is compounded, not FDA-approved. If final creative makes product claims, include disclosure where format allows/needs it.

## Dr. Jaquish Clip Strategy

### Clip lane A — Short authority hits, 10-20s

Purpose: one scroll-stopping expert idea, fast.

Use for:
- No injections.
- Standard TRT timing problem.
- Fast-acting testosterone.
- Delivery curve / AM timing.
- 60-day plateau curiosity.

Structure:
1. Dr. J says one sharp idea.
2. Captions simplify the idea.
3. End card routes to quiz/qualification/physician-guided oral TRT option.

### Clip lane B — Mini-explainers, 30-40s

Purpose: give Dr. J enough room to make the mechanism feel credible without becoming a seminar.

Use for:
- 60-day plateau.
- SHBG / body “catching up” explanation.
- Standard TRT vs fast-acting testosterone.
- Timing / delivery-window explanation, without claiming Oral Only mimics natural rhythm or avoids nighttime/high-level problems as a proven outcome.
- Founder/teacher credibility, tied to the product carefully.

Structure:
1. Hook with product-aware problem.
2. Dr. J explanation using transcript source language.
3. Simple visual overlay: curve / timeline / standard TRT vs fast-acting.
4. Close with quiz/qualification/physician-guided oral TRT option.

Do not use direct phrases like “muscle-growing machine,” “no side effects,” “works forever,” or “TRT is a scam” as unqualified ad claims.

### Dr. J edit authorship rule

- **Dr. J spoken VO must come from his actual clips/transcripts verbatim.** Do not write new Dr. J lines, paraphrase him as spoken VO, or make him appear to say words he did not say.
- **Mixing clips is allowed.** We can stitch the best bits from different Dr. J videos if the final edit is clearer and more compelling.
- **Added copy is allowed only as editorial layer:** on-screen captions, simple explainers, lower-thirds, end cards, disclosures, CTA, and visual overlays. These should clarify or frame the clip, not pretend to be Dr. J’s exact words.
- **If a caption summarizes his point, attribute carefully:** use framing like “Dr. Jaquish’s argument:” or “The question he raises:” rather than presenting a paraphrase as a direct quote.

### Dr. J editor reference requirement

- Every Dr. J video brief/script must include **editor-facing source video links**, not just local transcript filenames.
- Every selected clip must list the **exact timestamp range** the editor should pull.
- If one final video stitches multiple Dr. J videos together, list each source separately with: source link, timestamp range, verbatim transcript excerpt, and compliance note.
- Local transcript paths can be used for internal planning, but final editor-facing References/Footage sections must use YouTube/Motion/Drive links that the editor can open.

### Native format principles for this batch

- **X/Twitter posts:** short. Hook first, one compact reframe/info beat, one soft close. Target 35-50 words. Use an embedded image when useful: Dr. Jaquish still, simple delivery-curve graphic, or needle-drawer/product-process visual.
- **Each native concept needs three hook variations** that all work with the same middle and close. Do not approve a hook in isolation. In the map, stack the three hooks with blank lines instead of labeling them.
- **Reddit posts:** slightly more room. First person, title as the hook, body as short lived experience / question. No branded CTA inside the post.
- **Notes/checklist:** use when the point is protocol burden. The format should feel like a quick before/after routine list, not a testimonial.
- **Compliance pass:** preserve hook voice, but avoid turning problem-side language into product claims. No side-effect, fertility, hematocrit, “works forever,” or guaranteed outcome claims.
