# Native Screenshot Ad Criteria

Rules for ads that mimic organic social media posts — Tweet/X, Reddit, Facebook Group, Instagram Story screenshots. Apply on top of [[Universal Copy Rules]].

Derived from Lifeforce T002.

---

## Universal Native Voice

These rules apply to ALL native screenshot formats. Platform-specific rules follow.

- Copy must read like an organic post from a real person on that platform, not ad copy. No exclamation marks. No emojis in the post text.
- First person is the default for Reddit, Facebook, and Instagram Story. Tweets can use first person or third-person stats.
- Line breaks are critical across all formats. One thought per line. Scannable on mobile.
- Don't name-drop the brand inside the post where it sounds inauthentic. Mention the brand in the primary text or platform headline, not forced into the post. Exception: when it's a natural part of the story (factual attribution like "That's why [person] created [Brand]" works).
- The post text should be 3-6 lines. Not a wall of text. Not a single sentence.
- The hook is the first line. It must create a curiosity gap, challenge a belief, or name an experience the reader identifies with.
- The post close should point toward a solution without sounding like an ad.

---

## Specificity in Native Ads

Baseline specificity rules (name ingredients, cite dosages, personal-story numbers, research-backed stats, no absolute doctor claims) live in [[Universal Copy Rules]]. Native-specific note: generic symptom lists underperform vs specific numbers by ~2x on ROAS. Default to specificity.

---

## Images Inside Posts

- Tweet and Facebook Group posts must have an embedded image (before/after, infographic, lab results, etc.). Text-only tweets don't scroll-stop as well.
- Reddit posts can be text-only or have an image — depends on the concept. Text-only is completely native for Reddit personal stories.
- Instagram Stories: the image/meme fills the top of the story, text flows below it.
- Before/after photos should use people in the target demographic. Relatable physique, not fitness models.
- Do NOT use celebrity faces in memes. Use a regular person — stock photo is fine.

## Platform-Specific Rules

### Tweet/X Screenshot

- Dark-mode Twitter/X UI.
- Profile picture and username must be blurred.
- Post text styled as native X typography.
- Embedded image below the text.
- Engagement bar at bottom (likes, retweets, views — realistic but not specific numbers).
- **Tweet copy: 35-50 words max.** The winning formats average ~41 words. Tweets are screenshot images — longer text = smaller font = harder to read on mobile. Rule: hook (1-2 lines) + supporting stat or reframe (1-2 lines) + close (1 line).
- **Tweet closes must bridge through a person or the brand before dropping price/biomarker count.** "50+ biomarkers. $49 to start." out of nowhere feels disconnected. Bridge it: "That's why [person] created [Brand]. $49 to start." Vary the bridge across the batch.
- All education and selling goes in the primary text, not the tweet itself.

### Reddit Post Screenshot

- Reddit dark-mode UI with subreddit header visible.
- Username blurred, subreddit name visible.
- Always first person. Reddit is personal storytelling.
- Posts have two parts briefed separately:
  - **Post Title** — the hook. What people see in the feed and decide whether to click. Should read like a real Reddit headline: first person, specific, often a mini-story in one sentence. "At 41, I thought the brain fog was just stress. My testosterone was 340. Nobody had ever tested it."
  - **Post Body** — the detail underneath. Line breaks between thoughts, scannable.
- Post titles that sound like blog posts or editorials ("Here's what happens when testosterone drops") must be rewritten in first person.
- Reddit posts can be text-only (no embedded image) — native for personal stories and educational posts. Only include images when it makes sense.
- Upvote count and comment count visible (realistic numbers).

### Facebook Group Post Screenshot

- Facebook light-mode UI (default FB look).
- Group name visible at top — use a generic name relevant to the topic (e.g., "Men's Health & Optimization"). Not a real group.
- Profile picture and username blurred.
- First person. Conversational, advice-giving tone. More structured than Reddit but still personal.
- Embedded image below text.
- Reaction bar at bottom (realistic numbers).

### Instagram Story Screenshot

- Instagram story UI overlay (top bar with profile pic circle, username, timestamp).
- Profile picture and username blurred.
- Full-screen vertical format.
- First person. Casual, typed-on-phone energy. One flowing thought across the story slide.
- Line breaks between thoughts for scannability.
- No formal punctuation between list items. "The soy sauce the plastic containers the third beer the seed oils" not "The soy sauce. The plastic containers. The third beer."
- Copy flows underneath the main image/meme.
- Each variation is one continuous story slide, not separate paragraphs.
- For memes: the meme image IS the story, with text overlay.

## Designer Notes — Native Screenshots

Designer notes for native screenshot ads are precise platform direction. The designer executes the UI mock-up exactly as described. No "come up with something better" — we're telling them exactly what we want. The only creative latitude is in the embedded image (before/after photo, lab results, etc.) when applicable.

---

## References — What to Include

- Only reference things relevant to the format being designed. Don't reference a tweet format ad in a Reddit-format brief unless you're specifically pointing to the image inside.
- Don't include "no existing top spender reference" notes — that's internal context, not useful to the designer.

---

## Quality Checklist (Native Screenshot Ads)

1. Copy reads like an organic post, not ad copy
2. Word count within platform limits (tweets: 35-50 words)
3. Hook is the first line and creates a curiosity gap or names an experience
4. Post close bridges to brand/solution without sounding like an ad
5. Line breaks make it scannable on mobile
6. No exclamation marks or emojis in post text
7. No false dichotomies or "It's not X" lists
8. No swearing, even censored
9. At least one specific number in the post (age, level, amount, count)
10. Brand name-drop is natural, not forced (or absent from post, present in primary text)
11. Embedded image present where required (tweets, FB group posts)
12. Image uses target demographic, not fitness models or celebrities
13. Platform UI elements correct (dark/light mode, blurred username, engagement bar)
14. Each headline/tweet variation is structurally different from the others
15. Post and primary text don't repeat the same opening hook
