# Long-Form Primary Text Criteria

Rules for long-form primary text (the "caption" beneath image ads on Meta). These are the 600-2,200 word stories that do the actual selling when paired with scroll-stopping images. Apply on top of [[Universal Copy Rules]].

Originated in FitSleeps T001 (1,500-2,200 word stories), adapted and refined in Lifeforce T002 (600-1,200 words).

---

## The 10-Beat Story Template

Not every ad needs all 10 beats. Story-driven ads should hit 7-8. Educational or listicle formats can use a subset.

1. **Opening hook** — a stat, a shocking moment, or a question that doesn't repeat the image/headline. The primary text is a SECOND hook for people who read past the image.
2. **The incident** — the moment that forced action (got bloodwork, doctor dismissed symptoms, hit a wall, partner said something, almost lost a job).
3. **Stakes escalation** — what's at risk (career, relationship, identity, health trajectory). Make it personal and specific.
4. **Failed alternatives parade** — what they tried that didn't work. Name them generically if competitors (not specific clinic or brand names unless the brand permits). Include prices and timeframes where possible.
5. **Word-of-mouth discovery** — how they found the product. Friend, authority figure, ad, article, coworker on the same shift. NOT "searching online."
6. **Mechanism education** — why this product is different. This is where we teach. Keep it conversational, not clinical.
7. **First results moment** — the specific improvement or symptom relief. Use numbers where available. Describe beat-by-beat (e.g., "first morning" moment described in detail).
8. **Transformation cascade** — the ripple effects. Show that fixing the root cause fixes multiple symptoms.
9. **Price anchoring** — what they were spending before vs this product's cost. Or: what the consequences of NOT acting would cost.
10. **Close** — CTA with offer, guarantee mention if applicable, and an emotional payoff line.

## Voice

- **Third person when running from a company page.** These ads run from the brand's account, not a whitelisted creator. Write like a knowledgeable narrator telling someone else's story: "He was 39. Doing everything right. Training four days a week. And his testosterone was 362 ng/dL." The narrator is the brand (or a knowledgeable voice) telling a member's story. NOT first person ("I was 39...").
- **First person when whitelisted through a person's page.** When the ad runs from a creator's or customer's personal account, write in their voice: "I was 39. Doing everything right..." The story becomes theirs directly.
- No em-dashes. Use commas, periods, natural sentence breaks.
- No swearing, even censored.

---

## Scannability

This is critical. The reader is on a phone scrolling fast. If they see a wall of text, they skip it.

- **15-25 words per paragraph.** FitSleeps primary texts use single-line paragraphs constantly and they convert. If your paragraphs are averaging 40-57 words, break them up.
- **Price must be scannable.** Give price its own line or its own short paragraph. Don't bury "$49" inside a 60-word paragraph. It should jump out at the reader who's scanning.
- Same for key proof points (biomarker count, customer count, guarantee, certifications). These should jump out, not be hidden in narrative.
- Lots of line breaks. One thought per line. Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max).

---

## Story Specifics

- Each ad must have a unique persona (different job, name, stakes from other stories in the batch).
- Character names must be phonetically distinct from names in other briefs in the batch.
- Failed alternatives should be named with specific prices and failure details where possible.
- Discovery must be through word-of-mouth or personal referral, not "searching online."
- The "first results" moment should be described beat-by-beat, not summarized. The reader should feel the moment.
- Partner's or family member's reaction should be included where the persona has one.

---

## Length Guidelines

- FitSleeps-style (maximum selling power): 1,500-2,200 words
- Standard story-driven: 800-1,200 words
- Humor-led or lighter formats: 600-800 words
- Branded comparison / feature-forward: 150-300 words (checkmarks and feature lists, no long-form story)
- The story should be as long as it needs to be. Don't pad to hit a number. Don't cut a good story short to hit a number.

---

## What NOT to Do

All AI-tell bans (no writerly metaphors, no stock transformation beats, max one analogy per primary text, no fourth-wall breaks, no false dichotomies, no "It's not X. It's not Y. It's not Z." lists, no swearing, no censored profanity) are canonical in [[Universal Copy Rules]] → Banned Constructions. Long-form has no additional format-specific bans beyond those.

---

## Quality Checklist (Long-Form Primary Text)

1. Opens with a different hook type from other ads in the batch (stat, story moment, question, empathy)
2. Primary text opening doesn't repeat the image headline or tweet text
3. Written in correct person (third from company page, first from whitelisted page)
4. Follows 10-beat template, hitting at least 7-8 beats for story-driven ads
5. Paragraphs average 15-25 words (scannable on mobile)
6. Price has its own line or short paragraph
7. Key proof points (customer count, guarantee, certifications) are scannable, not buried
8. Character has a unique persona, name, and stakes vs other ads in the batch
9. Character names phonetically distinct from other briefs
10. Failed alternatives are specific (with prices and timeframes where possible)
11. Discovery is through word-of-mouth, not "searching online"
12. "First results" moment described beat-by-beat
13. Partner/family reaction included (where persona has one)
14. Word count within target range for the format

Generic rules enforced via [[Universal Copy Rules]] (no em-dashes, no false dichotomies, no writerly metaphors / stock transformation beats, max one analogy, no fourth-wall breaks, mechanism connectors, no absolute doctor claims, dollar amounts as numerals in written text) apply but aren't re-listed here.
