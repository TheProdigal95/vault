## 1. Older Women / Menopausal

### One-Line Summary

Women in perimenopause or menopause (40-65+) experiencing fatigue, brain fog, sleep disruption, joint stiffness, and hot flashes who are frustrated by a growing cabinet of menopause-specific supplements that aren't solving the multi-system breakdown of hormonal change.

### Demographics

**Source: Review data (362 menopause-tagged reviews, avg rating 4.36)**

- **Primary countries:** USA, Canada, UK, Australia, New Zealand, Switzerland
- **Age signals from reviews:** 51 (Belinda Champness), 52 (Amy Cunningham, MR Mark Richard Upton, Peter Luciano), 53 (Katarina Kaljuste), 54 (Michelle Kelava), 57 (Laura Davis), 59 (Theresa O'Connell), 60 (Joan Vanderzalm), 64 (Joanne Gagne), 65 (Kim Crawford)
- **Life stages mentioned:** "entering perimenopause," "late stage peri-menopause," "post menopause," "in the heat of menopause"
- **Lifestyle descriptors:** working full time, part-time fitness instructor, competitive pickleball player, strength training, aquatic fitness teacher — these are active women, not sedentary

### Pain Points

*Source: Review data — verbatim language from reviewers*

1. **Energy depletion from hormonal changes:** "As I enter perimenopause my energy levels have been depleted" (Ann Halton). "70-80% of menopausal women report fatigue as their #1 symptom" (Landing Page).
2. **Brain fog and cognitive decline:** "the brain fog was most intense in the mornings" (Ann Halton). "Forgetting words mid-sentence" (Ad Library hook).
3. **Sleep disruption and night sweats:** "waking up drenched at 3am" (Sandra K., Landing Page testimonial). "I cried the first morning I woke up without night sweats" (Michelle T., Landing Page testimonial).
4. **Joint stiffness and pain:** "less sore joints, less stiffness" (Joan Vanderzalm). Declining estrogen linked to inflammation (Landing Page, Reason 6).
5. **Growing supplement cabinet:** "I had been looking for some kind of answer to the list of supplements I apparently needed as menopausal woman" (Justine Boone). Multiple bottles for bone health, sleep, energy, mood, joints — separately.
6. **Feeling dismissed by healthcare system:** "'You're just tired.' 'It's stress.' 'It's in your head.' Menopause is often dismissed." (Ad Library hook, 46 uses).
7. **Skin, hair, and nail changes:** "better skin, hair and nails" improvement noted by Joan Vanderzalm, Kim Crawford.
8. **Bone density concerns:** D3-K2 calcium-directing synergy, "calcium alone makes it worse" (Landing Page, Reason 2).

### Current Behavior / Solutions Tried

*Source: Review data — what customers mention using before IM8*

- Multiple individual supplements: "I previously took a fair few supplements and was doing fine" (Ericka Bryant). "I had been looking for some kind of answer to the list of supplements I apparently needed as menopausal woman" (Justine Boone).
- Standard multivitamins that don't address menopause-specific needs
- Mark Manson referral as discovery channel: "on the say-so of Mark Manson I tried IM8" (Ericka Bryant)
- Other probiotics with limited results: "I've been using other probiotics etc for years to no real benefits" (Kim Foster)
- AG1 and other greens powders: multiple reviews mention switching from AG1
- HRT alongside supplements: some reviewers are on HRT and using IM8 as complementary support (inferred from mentions of hormonal treatment)
- Adjusting dosage: "I only do 1/2 packet a day or one full every other day" (Michelle Kelava) — self-titrating because of body size/sensitivity

## 2. GLP-1 Support

### One-Line Summary

People currently taking GLP-1 receptor agonist medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro/Tirzepatide) who need comprehensive nutritional support to counteract the side effects of drastically reduced food intake — muscle loss, hair thinning, energy crashes, GI discomfort, and nutrient depletion.

### Demographics

**Source: Review data — very limited direct GLP-1 mentions (7 reviews explicitly reference GLP-1 medications)**

- **Countries identified:** USA (Ronald Huss, Pamela Taylor, Beverly Robison), UK (Richard Platts, Andrew Fleet, Elizabeth Jarvis), Norway (Heidi Hovdahl Gaustad)
- **Age signals:** Limited. Richard Platts mentions "over 40 years" as a pharmacist, suggesting 60+. Pamela Taylor had weight loss surgery 12 years ago.
- **Professional signal:** Richard Platts is a UK pharmacist — a healthcare professional using IM8 alongside Mounjaro
- **Also relevant:** Weight loss surgery patients (Beverly Robison, Pamela Taylor, Sherry Sheaves with gastric bypass, Elizabeth Jarvis with gastric sleeve) share the same core need: nutritional supplementation when food intake is restricted

**Important note:** The review corpus has very few direct GLP-1 mentions (7 explicit references). This is a growing audience that IM8 is clearly targeting aggressively (125 ads, 8.3% of ad library, dedicated landing page) but the existing customer base that self-identifies as GLP-1 users in reviews is still small. This means the persona is built more heavily from landing page content and ad strategy than from organic customer language.

### Pain Points

*Source: Review data (where available) + Landing Page (GLP-1 page)*

1. **Muscle loss from reduced eating:** "Up to 40% of weight loss on GLP-1 is muscle, not fat" (Landing Page, Reason 1). No direct review mentions of this specific concern.
2. **Hair thinning and nail weakness:** Positioned as "signals of nutrient collapse" on Landing Page (Reason 2). No direct GLP-1-specific review mentions, though general hair/nail improvement is praised across reviews.
3. **GI discomfort from medication:** "40% of GLP-1 users experience nausea, bloating, constipation" (Landing Page). Ronald Huss directly confirms: "Ozempic gives most users issues like tummy aches and irregular bowel movements. IM8 has eliminated those problems."
4. **Nutrient depletion from reduced food intake:** "11 critical nutrient deficiencies from reduced eating" (Landing Page, Reason 3). Richard Platts: "I was concerned about my diet since it reduces your appetite." 2026 Cambridge University study cited: "GLP-1 patients receive zero nutritional guidance."
5. **Energy crashes:** Landing Page frames this as a consequence of nutrient depletion. General energy improvement is the most praised benefit across all reviews.
6. **Cost of supplementing separately:** Landing Page (Reason 6): "$315+/month in separate supplements vs. $78/month for IM8."
7. **Need for simplicity while on medication:** Andrew Fleet: "still having to take some separate supplements as advised by pharmacist prescribing Mounjaro" — wants consolidation.

### Current Behavior / Solutions Tried

*Source: Review data*

- **Taking GLP-1 medication (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Tirzepatide, Wegovy):** Directly mentioned by Ronald Huss (Ozempic), Richard Platts (Mounjaro), Andrew Fleet (Mounjaro), Heidi Hovdahl Gaustad (Tirzepatide)
- **Weight loss surgery patients sharing same nutritional gap:** Beverly Robison (weight loss surgery), Pamela Taylor (weight loss surgery 12 years ago), Elizabeth Jarvis (gastric sleeve), Sherry Sheaves (gastric bypass)
- **Supplementing alongside medication:** Andrew Fleet lists: "Glutathione, Magnesium Glycinate, OptiBac Daily Extra, and 400mg CoQ10" as pharmacist-prescribed supplements alongside Mounjaro
- **Seeking doctor/pharmacist guidance:** Richard Platts specifically mentions pharmacist prescribing context; Beverly Robison got vitamin levels checked

- **No before/after data for GLP-1 users specifically:** The weight loss surgery patients provide the closest proxy (Beverly Robison's improved vitamin levels, Elizabeth Jarvis's nutritional deficiency support).
- **No demographic detail beyond country:** We can't determine age distribution, gender split, or which specific GLP-1 medication is most common among IM8 customers.

Currently both groups are served by the same GLP-1 landing page and ads — worth considering whether they need separate creative.
