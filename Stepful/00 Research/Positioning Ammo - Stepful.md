## U.S. Career Institute (USCI)

**Stats:** No public Trustpilot page with critical mass. Reddit and BBB feedback primary. Programs: Medical Assistant, Pharmacy Tech, Dental Assistant, Medical Billing & Coding. Self-paced online. Price: ~$1,300–$1,500. Switch-mentions in Stepful reviews: **0**.

**Fatal Flaw:** No Externships — and Employers Know It

### Theme A: The Externship Gap Disqualifies Students From Jobs

- "Do not choose USCI, I had a bad experience. My certificate is worthless bc employers dont like their low standards of teaching. Its cheap bc its worthless for many." — Reddit/r/MedicalAssistant, ~1★ `usci-ws-001`
- "Purchased an online MA program on 5.13.25. Did program and testing, never received certification. However I find out that this program is useless to me as I do not have clinical hours. I would like a partial or full refund as I will need in person clinical hours." — BBB, 1★ `usci-ws-006`
- "Also, AFAIK USCI doesn't do externships so that might be something to consider!" — Reddit/r/MedicalAssistant, 2★ `usci-ws-009`
- "I want to be able to get my hands on externships, which I have heard USCI does not offer, but Stepful is very fast-paced and strict on scheduling." — Reddit/r/MedicalAssistant, 2★ `usci-ws-010`
- "I forgot to mention if you have the option to do Stepful, it might be better since they offer externships. I did the USCI program in like 4 days then bought the smarterMA study course and I feel like I've learned more from it than USCI." — Exciting_Spirit_9639, Reddit, 2★ `usci-ws-008`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful includes a coordinated externship as part of the program — students don't graduate without real clinical hours. USCI students often discover the gap only after they've paid and completed.

### Theme B: Credential Credibility Problems

- "I wouldn't recommend it as it's not AHIMA accredited and it gives you a credential that is useless. Healthcare systems don't recognize the CBCS certification. They want CCS, CCS-P or CPC etc. from AAPC or AHIMA." — Racinginger1, Reddit/r/CodingandBilling, 1★ `usci-ws-004`
- "The US Career Institute Course will only give you a certificate in medical billing and coding. Please know this certificate is not an actual credential. You will then need to go on and study for a credentialing exam through either AHIMA or AAPC. You may be able to get a basic coding education through the US Career Institute however I do not believe that they prepare you for any type of credentialing exam." — DearMisterKitty, Reddit/r/MedicalCoding, 2★ `usci-ws-005`
- "I saw somewhere people had been denied a job simply for attending that program, its not accredited, have poor customer service... and I don't want to take my chances with it." — Reddit/r/MedicalAssistant, 2★ `usci-ws-007`
- "I did not have a good experience with this school and legitimately its the lowest standards so if you are fine being low standard and poorly trained go for it but I suggest a school with higher accreditation standards." — SoObvi, Reddit, 1★ `usci-ws-002`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful prepares students for nationally recognized NHA (CCMA, CPCT) and PTCB (CPhT) exams. The credential is the exit product — students know exactly what exam they're prepping for before day one.

### Theme C: Coursework Doesn't Prepare for Real Exams

- "I got my training from them. They do not adequately train for the cpc exam. I had to teach myself and buy the AAPC study guide and practice exams." — FunAmount248, Reddit/r/CodingandBilling, 2★ `usci-ws-003`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's curriculum is built backwards from the certification exam — every module maps to an exam objective. Students don't need to buy a third-party study guide to pass.

---

**Quick-Reference — Top 3 positioning lines vs U.S. Career Institute:**
1. "USCI gives you a certificate. Stepful gives you a credential — the CCMA or CPhT that employers actually hire for."
2. "You can finish USCI in 4 days. You still can't get hired without clinical hours. Stepful's externship is built in."
3. "USCI students find out about the externship gap after they've paid. Stepful students complete their placement before they graduate."

## MedCerts

**Stats:** Trustpilot ~4.2★ but with notable BBB complaint volume. Programs: Medical Assistant, Phlebotomy, Surgical Tech, Pharmacy Tech, and others. Online, self-paced with advisor support. Price: ~$2,500–$4,000+ (program-dependent; consultation required). Switch-mentions in Stepful reviews: **0**.

*Note on price tier: MedCerts pricing exceeds the ±50% ceiling for some programs (Stepful ~$1,100–$1,450 avg). Strategist confirmed inclusion based on same pain/job qualification.*

**Fatal Flaw:** You Pay $4,000, Find Your Own Externship, and No One Calls You Back

### Theme A: Students Are Responsible for Finding Their Own Externship

- "I was not aware that I would have to find my own extern site for surgical tech nor was it an accredited program like they claimed it was." — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-003`
- "I've passed the certification exam in June and can't even attain my credentials because I can't complete required clinicals. I was told on a recent interview there are no programs available for MedCerts students. I was also told by the MedCerts employee [Sr. Advisor] that they have had problems getting REMOVED students placed for clinicals. WHY WAS I NOT TOLD THIS FROM THE BEGINNING?" — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-004`
- "When I told my clinical advisor that I was having a hard time finding placement... all she said was 'all our students find placement'. She was no help at all." — BBB complainant, 2★ `mdc-ws-005`
- "It is credible, however be prepared to wait months (2 months with no word of progress and I ended up having to drive an hour) for your externship." — Trustpilot reviewer, 2★ `mdc-ws-006`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful coordinates externship placement directly — students are placed at a partnered clinic near them. No searching, no driving an hour, no "you're on your own."

### Theme B: Paid Thousands, Still No Job

- "Unfortunately I did the program a year ago… & still haven't gotten a job. I definitely think I wasted my money & time doing this program." — Reddit, 1★ `mdc-ws-001`
- "I paid for an online program for ST. I completed the course and since 9/23 I have been reaching out to my advisor... The issue is - you pay $4k for the course and cant use it anywhere. The program is not accepted as I have no hands on experience and no where in REMOVED will hire me with that online program." — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-002`
- "Every student we get from them is horribly under certified to be in the OR. The CSTs have to teach them everything! Even scrubbing your hands and gowning and gloving." — Healthcare employer, Reddit, 1★ `mdc-ws-012`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful students graduate with completed clinical hours, not just a certificate. That's the difference between a resume that gets ignored and one that gets an interview.

### Theme C: Customer Service Disappears After Enrollment

- "Constantly giving Misinformation, poor communication and terrible customer service. Extremely combative advisors who never seem to have answers and it takes over 48 hours to get any question answered." — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-007`
- "After that 21 day period it takes weeks for your advisor to reply to calls, emails, text messages etc." — Trustpilot reviewer, 2★ `mdc-ws-008`
- "My main issue was that no one seemed to want to talk to me. I finished the course in under a month. If there was any technical help I needed, I had to figure it out on my own." — Trustpilot reviewer, 1★ `mdc-ws-009`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's cohort model means students have live instructors and real-time support throughout the program — not an advisor who ghosts you after week 3.

### Theme D: Refund Traps and Collections

- "Despite following all refund policy requirements, I was told I did not qualify with no explanation, and my repeated emails requesting clarification have been ignored." — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-013`
- "When I decided I no longer wanted to go through with the program they sent my account to collections without an explanation or warning!!!" — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-014`
- "They wont give me my money back because I passed the 21 days but it takes longer than that to even hear the word phlebotomy so how is one to know." — BBB complainant, 1★ `mdc-ws-015`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's payment plans and refund terms are straightforward. Students aren't ambushed by a 21-day window that runs out before they've even started the program.

---

**Quick-Reference — Top 3 positioning lines vs MedCerts:**
1. "MedCerts charges up to $4k and tells you to find your own externship. Stepful includes placement — coordinated, near you, before you graduate."
2. "MedCerts students pass the exam and still can't get hired. Stepful students have clinical hours on their resume on graduation day."
3. "MedCerts advisors go silent after the 21-day window. Stepful students are in live cohorts with instructors every week."

## CareerStep

**Stats:** BBB accredited with complaint history. $43.5M FTC settlement (2023) over false job placement claims and targeting of military families. Programs: Medical Assistant, Phlebotomy, Pharmacy Tech, Medical Billing & Coding, Surgical Tech. Self-paced online. Price: ~$3,500–$4,500. Switch-mentions in Stepful reviews: **0**.

*Note on price tier: CareerStep's standard pricing significantly exceeds the ±50% ceiling vs Stepful (~$1,100–$1,450). Strategist confirmed inclusion based on same pain/job qualification. Log this context for copy use: some CareerStep students paid with MyCAA/military benefits, meaning out-of-pocket cost may have been lower.*

**Fatal Flaw:** FTC-Fined For Lying About Jobs — Less Than 10% Ever Got Placed

### Theme A: Job Placement Promises Were False — FTC Said So

- "As someone who completed this course, you are not likely to walk into a job." — former student, Reddit, 1★ `cs-ws-003`
- "I just found out that career step is not accredited. I really don't know if that makes a difference Because I cannot find any of these reviews were anyone has got out and actually got a job." — Reddit, 1★ `cs-ws-001`
- "I never got useful help, such as an externship or job placement assistance, after completing the program." — Reddit, 1★ `cs-ws-004`
- "They said Psych tech certificate was approved in [my state]... Why lie why make people believe all this and take their money.. long story short I paid for nothing cause this certificate doesn't work for me! People please don't let these people fool you I just want my money back!" — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-002`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's job placement rate is a real, verifiable number — and placement is supported through the externship network, not just resume tips. No FTC investigation, no false claims.

### Theme B: Externship Placement Rate Was Effectively Zero

- "At least they pay for the test, but I know finding an externship to do my 400 hrs afterwards is completely on me." — Reddit student, 2★ `cs-ws-006`
- "Career step is a scam. I did my phlebotomy internship and they stated over the phone which they have records of that the internship is 18months. No it's 12 months. They tried blaming me for not knowing." — Trustpilot, 1★ `cs-ws-005`
- "A student on Reddit reported being deemed not eligible for the externship process because half of my work is miraculously missing from grade book." — Reddit, 1★ `cs-ws-007`
- "I was told by the recruiter that they were nationally accredited. Lied. Also the lawsuit for the lies being told to Veteran families." — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-015`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful coordinates all externship placements in-house. Students don't finish a course only to learn their 400 clinical hours are "on them."

### Theme C: Customer Service Is Unreachable

- "They are not helpful... contacting operations which is where you order them is IMPOSSIBLE to get a hold of. I let messages and numerous emails and they never contacted me back. I called about 50 times in 4 days." — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-008`
- "Someone of behalf of career step LOST a $2000 check sent directly from an employer and refuses to let me speak to a manager or even file a complaint. Zero communication, zero effort to fix the issue, absolutely negligent." — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-009`
- "I attempted to go through the course which was difficult to navigate and could never find anyone to help with questions I had. The guidance, promises and clarity on their end was horrible." — BBB complainant, 2★ `cs-ws-010`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's cohort model means students always have a point of contact — a live instructor, not a support queue that never answers.

### Theme D: Hidden Cancellation Terms → Debt Collections

- "I called to cancel and they said I couldn't because I had to cancel before 14 days, I didn't know, now I have collections calling me that I owe $3,700.00... I offered to pay $1,500 to close my account and they refused what a nightmare." — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-013`
- "I didn't recall getting told about a 14 day trial and when I called to get out of my contract, they said there was nothing they can do. Now, I got debt collectors calling my phone every single day harassing me about money I can't give them." — BBB complainant, 1★ `cs-ws-014`

**Stepful positioning angle:** Stepful's payment plans are transparent and listed on the website. No hidden 14-day windows, no debt collection.
