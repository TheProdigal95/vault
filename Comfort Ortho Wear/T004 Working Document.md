## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

T004 is a reset based on the Visual Ads Analysis (06-01) and fresh Top Spenders Analysis (06-05). The diagnosis: our creatives die at $20-$100 spend because AI B-roll breaks trust and kills engagement signals before the algorithm can test the concept. Partner winners use real footage, contrarian hooks, and competitor-naming as creative targeting.  We had 1 creative in the top 30. They have 29.

### Key Shifts from T003

1. **Visual grammar:** Real footage over AI B-roll. Brand library clips + editor-sourced TikTok/YouTube Shorts/Instagram footage first. Avoid stock. AI-generated only when unavoidable, and only if it looks perfectly real.
2. **Hook default:** Contrarian/myth-busting first. Lead with what NOT to do, challenge a belief, name a competitor in the hook.
3. **Competitor names in hooks:** Targeting tactic. "H0kas, 5.6 out of 10" self-selects every Hoka owner in the feed.
4. **Travel expansion:** 3/15 in T003 → 5/15 in T004.
5. **Senior angle:** New persona lane. Podiatrist format + "Safest" reframe.
6. **Caption strategy:** Allow captions to mirror VO for sound-off readability in native formats.
7. **Spanish-language concepts:** Own scripts in proven persona lanes (nurse + travel), not translations. Native VO.
8. **Production style:** Phone-quality, single camera, clip-bank feel. No color grammar, no AI character continuity.

### Exclusions

- **No direct identity hooks** — "12-HOUR SHIFT SHOES" style dies at 1.3-1.6% CTR
- **No CGI mechanism animations as default** — physical demonstrations only. Max 1 CGI insert per video.
- **No stock footage as default** — source native-looking footage from TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram/Reels, or similar platforms whenever possible.
- **No obvious AI footage** — if AI footage is used, it must be indistinguishable from real phone footage.
- **No color-graded pain states** — no desaturated pain / warm solution transitions
- **No consistent AI character** — mixed sources, mixed quality, clip-bank feel
- **No seasonal/Memorial Day** — evergreen only
- **No founder story as default** — only where it directly answers price skepticism

### Hooks Deep Dive — Pattern Interrupt per Concept

> 3 hook variations per concept in the scripts. Up to two of the three can be **pattern interrupts** — visual/verbal/caption blueprints grounded in specific organic TikTok references or proven visual techniques. Concepts marked **2 PIs** in the TikTok Visual Hook Reference Map ship two pattern interrupt hooks (Hook A and Hook B). The remaining hook is written by the script-writer agents as an alternative approach.
>
> **Comment stitch format:** When a hook uses comment stitch, it must use TikTok-native comment UI — the "Reply to @username" bar at top with the original comment text visible, exactly as it appears on TikTok. Not a generic text overlay or custom-designed comment bubble.
>
> **Hook B/C quality bar:** Hook A is not the only performance hook. Hook B and Hook C must each have their own first-frame visual premise, verbal angle, caption, and reason the right viewer stops. No reworded duplicates. Each alternate hook must shift at least 2 of 4 elements from Hook A: first-frame visual, tension type, target self-identification, or proof object. Hook C must use a different visual style from A/B per global Video Script Criteria.
>
> **Hook set stress test:** Before a script ships, each of the three hooks must answer: what is visible in the first 2 seconds, who self-selects, what belief or competitor is being challenged, and why this hook is meaningfully different from the other two?

## Concept Validation

> Runs after the Batch Plan is drafted, before script assignments begin. Each concept passes the 7 tests individually; the differentiation rule is a batch-level cross-check.

### 7-Test Quality Bar (per concept)

1. **Pain point specificity** — built around a specific human tension, not a product feature.
2. **Strategic coherence** — audience, message, structure, and hook work together.
3. **Differentiation** — couldn't be used by a competitor without modification.
4. **Format ambition** — visual format chosen intentionally, not by default.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — hook execution creates felt tension.
6. **Testable hypothesis** — grounded enough to succeed or fail clearly.
7. **Producible** — creative team can execute without major clarification.

### Differentiation Rule

Each concept must shift at least 2 of these 4 axes from every other concept in the batch:

- Intended audience / angle
- Messaging angle
- Visual format / structure
- Hook tactic

### T004 Visual Direction Rules (NEW — overrides T003 defaults)

**Footage sourcing hierarchy:**
1. **Brand B-roll library** — 235 nurse clips, 303 product clips mapped in `00 Assets/B-Roll Inventory.md`. Reference specific clips by filename when useful.

3. **AI-generated footage** — only when native footage cannot solve the beat. Must look perfectly real, phone-shot, and indistinguishable from sourced footage.
4. **Stock footage** — avoid by default. Use only if native social footage cannot cover the beat and AI would look fake.

**Critical gap: travel footage does not exist in the brand library.** The Cobblestone folder is empty. All travel B-roll (cobblestone walking, European streets, airports, museum interiors, hotel balconies) must be sourced by editors from TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram/Reels, or similar native platforms. Strategist does not source these clips manually.

**Editor sourcing directive:** Editors own generic B-roll sourcing. Footage entries should include brief alternatives ("alt: any hotel fatigue shot") so editors have fallback options. If the script cites a specific creator, ad, or reference, include the real URL. Otherwise, generic proof footage is delegated to editors.

**Editor Notes (required in every script):** Each script must include an `### Editor Notes` section (between References and Footage) with:
- 5K follower rule, no-face-as-focal-point rule, blur logos/handles
- Provided TK clips are pre-approved (where applicable)
- No stock; AI B-roll only as last resort

**References formatting:** Every sub-bullet under a reference link must use "Use:" or "Skip:" prefixes. No spend data, CTR, ranking positions, or strategy notes in References — visual/editorial takeaways only.

**Native footage safety:** Source only from creators with under 5,000 followers. Prefer POV, feet, hands, environment, backs of people, product handling — no shots where the creator's face is the focal point. Blur competitor logos and platform handles (except intentional comment stitch UI).

**"Still needed (AI-generated)" elimination:** Every visual column line that would previously say "Still needed (stock or AI-generated)" must instead specify a sourcing path: brand library clip name, editor-source native footage label, or a clearly marked AI fallback. Do not default to stock. If AI is used, it must look perfectly real.

**Production style:**
- Phone-quality, single-camera talking head for authority formats
- Mixed shot quality acceptable: direct-to-camera, phone product handling, feet POV, simple overlays
- No color grammar (desaturated pain / warm solution). Natural lighting throughout.
- No consistent AI character across scenes. The argument connects clips, not visual continuity.
- **All talent is AI UGC.** No filming briefs or footage requests. Visual column uses "AI UGC [character type]" (AI UGC nurse, AI UGC doctor, AI UGC podiatrist, AI UGC creator) — not "creator" or "reactor."

**Per-spoken-clause rule:**

### Creator Performance Direction (T004, applies to all creator-led and authority concepts)

Derived from deep analysis of COM49 Teacher Traveling Yapper (#22, 6.58% CTR). This ad works because of how the creator performs, not just what she says.

**Casting direction:**
- Prioritize real people who match the target demographic. The COM49 creator is a woman in her 50s-60s, the actual customer. Not a young UGC creator performing for a different audience.
- The creator must handle the product like a familiar item, not a prop. Holds it from frame 1, gestures naturally, references it casually.
- "Teacher" and "nurse" identities work because they're on their feet all day, price-conscious, and perceived as trustworthy. Cast people whose real profession stacks with the product story.

**Emotional arc pattern (brief this, don't leave it to chance):**

| Beat | Energy | What the face does | What the voice does |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warning/plea | Urgent, leaning in | Widened eyes, serious mouth, eyebrows raised in concern | Firm and deliberate. "I'm begging you" drops in pitch like a confessional aside |
| Credibility | Knowing, relaxed | Slight smirk, knowing nod, "I've been there" expression | Conversational, slightly quicker, matter-of-fact |
| Pain story | Embodied discomfort | Grimace, narrowed eyes, slight head shake, shoulders slump | Slows down, draws out the pain words ("done," "couldn't walk"), volume drops |
| Pivot to solution | Instant brightening | Entire face lights up, eyes widen, hopeful smile appears | Picks up pace, buoyant energy, one-word transitions carry the shift ("So...") |
| Proof/triumph | Confident, joyful | Broad smile, sparkling eyes, firm triumphant nod | Punches the payoff word ("fine!"), upward inflection |
| Versatility/confidence | Playful, proud | Mischievous smirk, playful head tilt, proud chuckle | Singsong rhythm for lists ("dressed them up, dressed them down"), amused tone |
| Authority reveal | Direct, definitive | Direct eye contact, firm nod, "I know something you don't" expression | Clear, slightly slower, confident. The "built for nurses" line is a quiet mic drop |
| Close/CTA | Warm urgency | Eyes brighten for the deal, then settle into warm satisfaction | Speeds up for price and guarantee, then slows to sincere warmth for final line |

**Authenticity rules:**
- Direct eye contact with camera throughout. She's talking to one person, not performing for an audience.
- Natural hesitations and inflections. If the delivery sounds like a teleprompter read, it fails. The voice should have the rhythm of someone telling a story at dinner.
- Product held casually from frame 1. Not revealed dramatically. It's already in her hand because she's about to warn you about your shoes.
- The emotional shifts must be visible on the face. The pain grimace at "my arches were done" and the instant brightening at "So for Portugal" are what create 6.58% CTR. Script-writer agents must describe the facial expression at each shift beat in the Visual column.

**What NOT to brief:**
- Don't brief a polished, energetic performance. Brief a conversational one.
- Don't brief dramatic reveals. The product is in hand from the start.
- Don't brief multiple locations for the creator. She stays on the couch. B-roll inserts handle location variety.
- Don't brief the creator to list features. A second person in scrubs handles the product demo section. Two creators, two roles.

### Copy Rules (T004)

- **No em dashes.** Never use em dashes in VO, captions, hooks, or any viewer-facing copy. They look like AI. Use natural connectors: "and," "but," "so," "because," commas, or periods.
- **Yapper language.** VO must sound like a real person talking to a friend — short sentences, direct, conversational. Target is COM49 "Teacher Traveling Yapper" energy. Split compound sentences into 2-3 short ones. Replace clause structures (", which means...", ", and the...") with separate sentences. "Feels great hour one. By hour four? Wobbly feet. Knees hurt." — not "Cushioning feels incredible at hour one. By hour four your feet are unstable and your knees start taking the hit."
- **First person.** "I did X" not "You should X." Third person sounds like a brand wrote it.

### Hook Rules (T004 — overrides T003 defaults)

- **Contrarian/myth-busting is the default hook type.** Lead with what NOT to do, challenge a belief, name a competitor. "The worst travel advice" > "Top 5 shoes."
- **Competitor names belong in hooks.** Starting with "H0kas, 5.6 out of 10" self-selects owners of that brand. This is targeting built into the creative.
- **Each competitor gets one specific failure.** Never generic dismissal. Time-stamped failures preferred ("collapses by hour 8", "no arch support by hour 3").
- **Direct identity hooks ("12-HOUR SHIFT SHOES") are excluded.** They underperform at 1.3-1.6% CTR vs contrarian at 5.3-5.5%.
- **At least 1 of 3 hooks must promise a payoff.** A forward-looking line that tells the viewer what they'll learn or see ("Here is what actually works", "Let me show you why", "Here is what changed"). Creates an expectation gap that pulls the viewer into the body. Hooks that only state problems or consequences without a forward promise don't retain.

### Caption Strategy (T004 — updated from T003)

- **Native/evidence-dense formats:** Captions may closely mirror VO for sound-off readability. Partner winners prove this works for this account. Comprehension > channel separation.
- **Educational/authority formats:** Two-channel separation still applies. Captions add context, scores, or verdicts while VO explains.
- **Persistent verdict captions** for ranking formats: red X + failure summary stays on screen for each competitor segment. Green check + positive summary for ComfortWear.

### Editing Styles (T004 — replaces T003 defaults)

- **Authority rating format:** Rapid-fire competitor ratings (2-3s per shoe: graphic appears → score → one-line clinical knock → next) per TK9 cadence, then transition to slower proof section (2-3s proof clips, feature demos, review screenshots). The doctor/podiatrist never fully leaves the screen (PiP during demo section). The 2-3s cadence is proven by @thesoledoctor's format — 8 shoes in 21 seconds, each with a distinct facial reaction and dismissal energy.
- **Creator-led format:** Selfie/couch for majority. Real B-roll inserts only as proof shots (cobblestone walking, archway, feet on terrain). Phone-quality. Comment stitch UI when applicable.
- **Countdown ranking:** 2-4s per beat. One visual event per spoken clause. Each competitor segment gets a holding summary caption that stays on screen.
- **All formats:** Dense 1-3s cuts during pain/failure sections. No prescribed pacing formula — let the argument drive the rhythm.
