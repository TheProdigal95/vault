# Script Structure: AI Animated Character

AI-generated character composited over B-roll via green screen. Character alternates between full-frame (direct address) and corner-box (B-roll proves the mechanism). The character is a visual anchor — always present, never gone for more than 5-8 seconds. Best for mechanism education where the viewer needs a face to trust but the brand has no real spokesperson.

### **Core Breakdown**
- **Structure:** Character appears full-frame in hook → mechanism explanation with full-frame/corner-box alternation → product bridge → close with character full-frame.
- **VO register:** Character-dependent. Doctor/authority (warm, clinical), narrator (conversational, direct), sardonic expert (irreverent, confident). The character's personality IS the VO register.
- **Time to benefit:** 15-20s (educational mechanism: education before product).
- **Rhythm:** Alternate full-frame and corner-box every 5-8s. Character holds for 3-4s at key points. B-roll cuts at 2s during mechanism beats. Never let the character disappear for more than one beat.
- **Duration:** 30-60s.
- **Close type:** Authority close or urgency/offer.
- **Visual grammar:** 3+ visual layers per body beat of ~10s (character frame + B-roll + overlay/CGI). B-roll = ingredient labels, nutrient tables, product shots, lifestyle, certifications. No stock-only beats — character must be in at least corner-box.
- **Hook defaults:** Character appears from frame 1. Reframe hook ("Hot flashes aren't just hormones"), provocation ("If supplements worked, why are you still awake at 3am"), or sardonic pattern interrupt ("Congrats on the smaller pants").
- **Caption strategy:** Two-tier: dark blocks for narration + bold serif keyword callouts for mechanism terms. Or clean minimal depending on brand.

### When to Use
- When the brand has no real spokesperson or creator pipeline
- Mechanism-education products (supplements, health-tech) where science credibility matters
- When you need a consistent "face" across multiple scripts without filming
- AppLovin and other platforms where AI characters perform well

### Key Rules
- **Full-frame/corner-box alternation is mandatory.** Character full-frame for hook, transitions, and close. Corner-box during B-roll proof and mechanism beats. Never static for more than 8 seconds.
- **3+ visual layers per ~10s body beat.** Character frame + B-roll + overlay/CGI minimum. A body beat with only the character talking to camera is a flag.
- **Hand gestures must NOT be repetitive.** AI UGC hand loops are the most common tell. Vary the gesture type per beat.
- **No uncanny valley tolerance.** If the face looks wrong at any point, that clip gets cut. Brief the editor to flag and replace.
- **Character casting matches the target demo.** A 50+ female doctor for menopause. A 40s male for GLP-1. Not a young generic AI face.
