# Three Selves

Scores whether the targeted self (Actual/Ideal/Ought) is consistent throughout the copy.

---

## What This Dimension Evaluates

Three Selves (from self-discrepancy theory) is an orthogonal targeting axis applied per concept. Each self has a distinct emotional register and language pattern. The assigned self must be consistent from headline through CTA — unintended self-switching mid-copy creates emotional whiplash that breaks persuasion.

---

## Required Reads

- `00 Global/Process/Creative Strategy Matrix.md` — Section "Three Selves Integration" (definitions, language patterns, when to use, distribution guidance)

---

## Self Definitions

| Self | Register | Language Pattern | Emotional Core |
|------|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Actual** | Reflective, validation | "You're not wrong." "This is happening." "It's not just you." | Recognition before action — they need to feel seen |
| **Ideal** | Aspirational, identity | "The person who..." "Your best..." "Imagine waking up..." | They want to see the version of themselves the product creates |
| **Ought** | Imperative, obligation | "You should." "Stop doing X." "It's time to..." | They need a nudge to act on something they already believe |

---

## Scoring Criteria

### What earns points

1. **Consistent register throughout** — every line (headline, body, CTA) operates from the same self's emotional register
2. **Language patterns match the self** — Actual Self uses reflective/validating language, Ideal Self uses aspirational/identity language, Ought Self uses imperative/obligation language
3. **Emotional coherence** — the reader's emotional journey stays within the self's territory from first word to last
4. **Self-appropriate CTA** — Actual: "See what's really going on" / Ideal: "Become the person who..." / Ought: "Start now" or "Stop waiting"
5. **Intentional bridging when needed** — if a concept requires touching an adjacent self (rare), the bridge is explicit and purposeful, not accidental drift

### What loses points

1. **Unintended self-switching** — headline operates from one self, body from another, with no bridging
2. **Register wobble** — the copy is mostly in one self but individual lines break register (an Actual Self ad that drops into "you should" imperative language)
3. **CTA-body mismatch** — body validates (Actual) but CTA pressures (Ought), or body commands (Ought) but CTA aspires (Ideal)
4. **Muddled register** — copy mixes language patterns from multiple selves such that no single self is dominant
5. **Ideal Self without base desire** — per Creative Strategy Matrix, Ideal Self must tap base human desires (looking good, control, freedom, energy), not product-centric aspiration ("confidence from a scoop" fails)

## Calibration

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 60 | Primary self present but the copy switches register mid-ad. The opening and closing target different selves. | Assigned: Actual Self. Headline: "You're not imagining it. Your sleep changed." (Actual). Body pivots to: "You should be taking magnesium glycinate before bed" (Ought). Close: "It's time to fix this" (Ought). Two-thirds of the copy is in the wrong self. |
| 80 | Consistent self throughout, one minor register wobble. The dominant self is clear but a single line breaks pattern. | Assigned: Ought Self. Headline: "Stop blaming your mattress" (Ought). Body: all imperative, obligation-driven. One line in the middle: "Imagine waking up actually rested" (Ideal Self bleed). Close returns to Ought. |
| 95 | Every line reinforces the same self. Emotional register is seamless from headline through CTA. A reader would feel one consistent emotional tone. | Assigned: Actual Self. Headline validates: "8 hours of sleep and you still feel like garbage. That's not laziness." Body: "Your gut lining is inflamed. That's why your melatonin stopped working. It's not you." CTA: "See what's actually happening." Every line reflects and validates. |

---

## Instant Fail (score capped at 59)

- **Headline targets one self but body pivots to a different self with no bridging.** Example: Headline targets Ought Self ("Stop doing X") but body pivots entirely to Ideal Self aspirational language ("imagine the version of you who...") without an explicit bridge. This creates emotional whiplash — the viewer was nudged to act, then suddenly asked to dream.

---

## Feedback Format

When score is below 90, feedback must include:
1. The assigned self and which self the copy actually executes in each section (headline, body, CTA)
2. The specific lines that break register, with the language pattern they use vs. the pattern they should use
3. A concrete direction for realigning — not "make it more Actual Self" but "rewrite the CTA from 'Start now' (Ought) to 'See what's really going on' (Actual) to match the validating register in the body"
