# Persona Deep Research — Stepful

## The Affordable Fast-Track Seeker — Journey Map

### Trigger (what starts the buying journey)
- **Signal from reviews:** "I was scared as shit before starting the program I kept contemplating bc I didn't want to get scammed out of 1100. I did reviews for month and kept coming back to the website just checking periodically." — Lyric Dean, 4★ `67d1c475c9bdbd9ffe4bc087`
- **Signal from reviews:** "I'm glad I seen the advertisement on TikTok and decided to sign up." — Nyazia Lackey, 5★ `69c6717d8f6ffa05db808b34`
- **Signal strength:** 12 reviews reference TikTok or social media discovery; 34 reference price consideration as a trigger

### Awareness → Consideration
- **Query language:** Likely "how to become a medical assistant online," "cheap pharmacy tech certification," "NHA CCMA online program," "medical assistant program under $2000"
- **Competitors compared:** No named competitors in reviews. CVS/Walgreens employer-training mentioned by 2 reviewers as alternative path for pharmacy tech track.
- **Information gaps:** "I wish they had included that our assignments would be getting graded by AI" (Isabella H., 1★ `69b97c1ea1bd6a6c4a480ebd`). Many students unclear on exam fees not being included in tuition until late in program.

### Evaluation
- **Deciding factors:** Price (grants available), online format (can work while studying), short timeline (4-5 months), pass rate stats, NHA affiliation
- **Anxieties that almost stopped purchase:** "I didn't want to get scammed out of 1100" — skepticism about legitimacy of online programs is the primary pre-enrollment barrier
- **Decisive moment:** Reading Trustpilot reviews. Multiple reviewers mention spending weeks reading reviews before committing. One reviewer "kept coming back to the website just checking periodically."

### Purchase
- **Preferred path:** Biweekly payment plan (many references to installments); grants applied to reduce upfront cost
- **Offer sensitivity:** Grant availability is a significant factor. Multiple reviewers mention affordability of payment plan as a key reason they enrolled.

### Use / Post-purchase
- **Month 1:** "The program is well-structured, easy to follow" — multiple 5★ reviews. Instructors named positively in early weeks.
- **Month 3-5:** NHA exam prep becomes focus. Mock exams, practice tests, instructor-led review. "The amount of practice and mock exams they do is worth it. It guarantees you will pass the exam." — Nebi, 5★ `698cdab899eb324fbdd120ea`
- **Ritual language:** "Every day is a step to improving myself" / "two live classes per week" / "daily assignments"
- **Positive surprise:** The clinical kit. "Does the Clinical Kit contain real medical equipment? Yes." Getting professional-grade equipment at home exceeded expectations.
- **Negative surprise:** NHA exam fee not included in tuition; AI grading of assignments.

### Advocacy / Churn
- **Why they stay:** High exam pass rates, instructor quality, structured path. "The program teaches you so much about the ins and outs of what it takes to be a CCMA." — John, 5★ `698818be0b7607f75d4b6e7b`
- **Why they leave:** Externship delays after graduation sour the experience. "the experience was rather lackluster and gave me quite a few headaches" — Renee, 2★ `677da76811ce16543f8fba53`
- **What they tell others:** "I'm glad I seen the advertisement on TikTok and decided to sign up" / "I would definitely recommend Stepful to my friends and family"

## The Externship & Job-Placement Deceived — Journey Map

### Trigger (what starts the buying journey)
- **Signal from reviews:** "one of the main reasons of me choosing stepful was the opportunity to not only learn and become a phlebotomist but the chance to have a guaranteed job after the program." — Samantha K., 2★ `63095c096a3e1ed2c3c1e7c9`
- **Signal from reviews:** "It sounds really cool when they say they have coaching and externships, but there are so many rules and problems that will end up making you not get an externship or job." — customer, 1★ `6667bf6105e20237ab4dce94`
- **Signal strength:** 47 reviews reference externship placement as primary enrollment motivation; 22 reference job placement promise specifically

### Awareness → Consideration
- **Query language:** Likely "online medical assistant program with job placement," "CCMA program guaranteed job," "pharmacy tech training with externship placement"
- **What they say they searched:** No direct review evidence. Inferred from what they say disappointed them: "they say they have coaching and externships" suggests they found this in marketing copy.
- **Information gaps:** Many did not know the 80% job placement rate has multiple conditions attached (must complete clinical hours AND enroll in job coaching within a specific window).

### Evaluation
- **Deciding factors:** The "job coaching + externship booking platform" promise is the deciding factor for this persona — not price or speed.
- **Anxieties that almost stopped purchase:** No review signal — they trusted the job placement promise. The anxiety came post-enrollment.
- **Decisive moment:** The marketing language around job coaching and the 8,000+ clinical partners network.

### Purchase
- **Preferred path:** Often full tuition upfront (to be eligible for externship services, per some reviews). "Yall forced me to pay my whole tuition all at once just to start the externship process when other students don't have to." — customer, 1★ `69f5403511322a6500dba5e0`

### Use / Post-purchase
- **During program (positive):** Instructor experience often positive. "The career coaches after the Stepful program that help in job searches are amazing. Specifically, Crystal Rubin and Tracye Warfield." — Faith, 4★ `68b208acb9b227d9069d3e61`
- **After graduation (negative):** "Post graduation, it was nearly impossible to get any response from Stepful." — Renee, 2★ `677da76811ce16543f8fba53`. "I graduated in May and I do have a Job coach and still don't have a job." — Nigeria Brown, 1★ `65149a1219992523b1106f71`
- **Ritual:** None established — the ritual was supposed to be externship + job coaching, which didn't materialize
- **Positive surprise (for the minority who got placed):** "During my week 4 of job coaching I was able to allocate a job… truly changed my life." — Kourtney Billups, 5★ `69fcd7988079bdf981d8100c`
- **Negative surprise:** Certificate withheld after passing NHA. Coordinator reassignment. Externship site with no actual training value.

### Advocacy / Churn
- **Why they stay:** The minority who get placed in good externships and have good coaches become strong advocates. "Most of the group was hired after their first session." — Zharea Carr, 4★ `69d933d60359022ce5265af5`
- **Why they leave:** Externship placement failure, communication breakdown, coordinator turnover, feeling like "they advertised falsely"
- **What they tell others:** Negative: "Don't waste your money! This is a SCAM" — John Espino, 1★ `6768414a383410b1511cf466`. Positive (when it works): Named coaches spread by word of mouth.

## The Dead-End Job Escaper — Journey Map

### Trigger (what starts the buying journey)
- **Signal from reviews:** "I am able to get out of fast food and branch into the medical industry forefront. They are still spearheading my drive to the top." — Sean Michael Casas, 5★ `69817fd0b2ae6ce237878a9d`
- **Signal from reviews:** "Stepful helped me completely reimagine my life." — Alvin, 5★ `6966c2821add018a9f484fc0`
- **Signal strength:** 19 reviews describe a specific career from which they were escaping; 14 more use general "new career" or "change" language

### Awareness → Consideration
- **Query language:** Inferred: "how to start a career in healthcare," "become a medical assistant with no experience," "change career to healthcare online"
- **Competitors compared:** No named competitors. TikTok discovery (2+ reviews mention seeing ads there).
- **Information gaps:** Some reviewers discover post-enrollment that healthcare job market for entry-level is difficult. "Be honest to the people applying, as a certified surgery tech 17yrs. Jobs are hard to come by." — K Swish, 2★ `67926117eeee0fb9fdac2a40`

### Evaluation
- **Deciding factors:** TikTok ad (visual social proof of real students), price (accessible via payment plan), the promise of a complete path (training + externship + job coaching)
- **Anxieties that almost stopped purchase:** "I was thinking it's impossible to take this course online with Stepful. I'm like it's not real until I try." — Stacey Jean Jacques, 5★ `69d7a70422b6cffbb4f856ef`. Skepticism about whether an online program is "real."
- **Decisive moment:** The TikTok ad, peer recommendation, or reading specific positive reviews that felt authentic.

### Purchase
- **Preferred path:** Grants + payment plan. "I love how Stepful tries to work with you and give you the opportunity to pay for your tuition." — Nyazia Lackey, 5★ `69c6717d8f6ffa05db808b34`

### Use / Post-purchase
- **Month 1-2:** Positive. The program structure and instructors create momentum. "I haven't encounter any problem with the program the material or the teacher so so far so good for me." — Lyric Dean, 4★ `67d1c475c9bdbd9ffe4bc087`
- **Month 4-5 and externship:** The career changer is most at risk of the externship failure experience, since they lack the industry network to find their own site.
- **Ritual:** Two live Zoom classes/week + daily assignments become routine
- **Positive surprise:** "This course was not for the weak, but it taught myself a big lesson! I can do ANYTHING I apply my mind and heart too." — Genevieve, 5★ `69a212140fb423403279d9f9`
- **Negative surprise:** The hands-on gap. "I personally would not recommend this program for people freshly getting into Healthcare." — ASHLEY MURRAY, 3★ `6957e85dee7e587bb256c410`

### Advocacy / Churn
- **Why they stay:** The transformation narrative is the dominant retention force. "Stepful will forever be part of my success story." (Reviews page)
- **Why they leave:** Mismatch between "get hired in healthcare" promise and "entry-level with online certificate is still hard to place" reality
- **What they tell others:** "I would definitely recommend this program to anyone wanting to step into the medical field." — Barthella, 5★ `69c1899aafc1465dd0df7fb5`

## The Healthcare Credential Upgrader — Journey Map

### Trigger (what starts the buying journey)
- **Signal from reviews:** "I have been in the medical field as a Certified Nursing Assistant for 20 plus years. Most of this is a review for me, but some of it is new." — Jamie Larson, 5★ `69881357203e8c2e1f62e9be`
- **Signal from reviews:** "I already knew I would have to pay to get my certification." — Isabella H., 1★ `69b97c1ea1bd6a6c4a480ebd`
- **Signal strength:** 16 reviews reference existing healthcare background; 6 reference credential need for career advancement

### Awareness → Consideration
- **Query language:** Inferred: "CNA to medical assistant certification," "NHA CCMA exam prep online," "medical assistant certification for experienced healthcare workers"
- **Competitors compared:** No named competitors. They may already know the NHA exam pathway and are shopping for prep programs specifically.
- **Information gaps:** "If you're already in it and just need credentials this might work for you" — ASHLEY MURRAY, 3★ `6957e85dee7e587bb256c410`. The program isn't calibrated for experienced students.

### Evaluation
- **Deciding factors:** Online format (they're working), price, NHA alignment, duration
- **Anxieties that almost stopped purchase:** None evident — this persona has less skepticism than the career-changer group. They already know the credential is valuable.
- **Decisive moment:** Price and timeline comparison to alternatives.

### Purchase
- **Preferred path:** Often full upfront payment (established professional with more financial stability)
- **Offer sensitivity:** Less grant-sensitive than career changers

### Use / Post-purchase
- **During program:** Often finds content repetitive ("this is a review for me"). Engages with instructor for clarification but is less reliant on the community/peer aspect.
- **Externship:** "Going through externship training was like acting out a script that was earlier rehearsed." — customerNkem, 5★ `6a12550d8e0fed11ab1e7fdd`. Experience-based learning accelerates their externship confidence.
- **Positive surprise:** NHA exam prep quality. "Everything that was taught is and was on the NHA exam." — shakiya.hadley, 5★ `698398d159b8f8cc46cd6506`
- **Negative surprise:** "I am still not working as a CCMA — company needs person with experience — my internship did not help much." — Audrey Williams, 2★ `690650abc95980f715cda42e`. Ironic gap: experienced healthcare worker still needs externship experience to transition to a different role.

### Advocacy / Churn
- **Why they stay:** Certification success. The credential is what they came for and most get it.
- **Why they leave:** Job coaching less relevant (they often have their own network); externship process slower than expected
- **What they tell others:** Conditional recommendation — "If you're already in it and just need credentials, this might work for you."

## The Life-Circumstances Career Launcher — Journey Map

### Trigger (what starts the buying journey)
- **Signal from reviews:** "Even when I had no place to live, Stepful patiently worked with me." — Lillian B., 5★ `6a023baf2e4ec0e0fc0cf074`
- **Signal from reviews:** "I had to change classes in order to start working but i could not leave miss jolene… every morning i might not have slept because of my two babies." — melissa alfaro, 5★ `69c06627e4ba97eb1d62706e`
- **Signal strength:** 21 reviews describe significant life circumstances (children, housing, illness, financial hardship) as context; 12 specifically reference coaches or instructors helping navigate those circumstances

### Awareness → Consideration
- **Query language:** Inferred: "online healthcare certification that works with my schedule," "medical assistant program for single moms," "CCMA program with payment plan"
- **Competitors compared:** No comparisons in reviews — this persona likely has fewer viable options due to life constraints, making Stepful's accessibility more decisive.
- **Information gaps:** No review signal of specific information gaps pre-enrollment. Their discovery process focuses on "can I do this?" rather than comparison shopping.

### Evaluation
- **Deciding factors:** The flexibility (classes can be moved, coaches will work with you), payment plan (no upfront lump sum), grant availability, online format (can't be physically present at a campus)
- **Anxieties that almost stopped purchase:** Can I finish this given everything going on in my life? Will they drop me if I fall behind?
- **Decisive moment:** Often a specific person at Stepful who showed up for them — an admissions rep who found them a grant, a coach who said "we'll work with you"

### Purchase
- **Preferred path:** Biweekly installments + grants. Often negotiated accommodations during enrollment.
- **Offer sensitivity:** Highly grant-sensitive. "The dedicated staff helped me when I was in and out of the hospital and struggling with finances and classes, helping me find grants and alternatives to make my certification affordable." — Alvin, 5★ `6966c2821add018a9f484fc0`

### Use / Post-purchase
- **During program:** The relationship with specific instructors and coaches is the primary retention mechanism. Instructor changes mid-program (even to a better instructor) cause stress.
- **Externship:** Mixed — this persona's life circumstances may make the "M-F 9-5" externship availability requirement genuinely difficult. Some skip externship and find jobs directly.
- **Ritual:** "I looked forward to our class!" — deep attachment to specific instructors as weekly anchors when life is unstable
- **Positive surprise:** "The dedicated staff helped me… The instructors stayed in calls for one on one support while I was in my hospital bed until almost midnight." — Alvin, 5★ `6966c2821add018a9f484fc0`
- **Negative surprise:** The rigidity of externship scheduling (M-F 9-5 requirement) conflicts with childcare and working schedules.

### Advocacy / Churn
- **Why they stay:** Emotional investment in the transformation story. This persona experiences the most life-change from completing the program, making them the strongest advocates.
- **Why they leave:** External life circumstances that make completion impossible, or the externship scheduling requirement that conflicts with childcare
- **What they tell others:** "Since my experience at Stepful, I have been referring as many people as I can who truly want to change careers or start a career." — Lillian B., 5★ `6a023baf2e4ec0e0fc0cf074`. "I recommend stepful to who ever wants to change their career and cant go in person." — melissa alfaro, 5★ `69c06627e4ba97eb1d62706e`
