## Executive Takeaways

- **Core creative unlock:** Dr Jaquish is not just a spokesperson; he is the brand's main trust asset and can record **1–2 scripts/week**. Creative should prioritize founder-led/doctor-led whitelisted video from his profile, plus recuts from his YouTube.
- **Primary switching audience:** many new customers are **not new to testosterone**; they are switching away from injections toward oral/sublingual testosterone because of needle burden, fluctuations, estrogen complications, testicular shutdown/fertility concerns, and hematocrit/side-effect fears.
- **Main growth constraint:** CAC is too high (~$1,000 in the call); target is closer to **~$600 CAC**. LTV was cited around **~$1,500**, with churn around **~7%**.
- **Funnel direction:** Rahul is testing longer quiz funnels and long-form PDPs. Longer quiz flow was reportedly performing better at the time of the call.
- **Offer direction:** approved evergreen acquisition offer: 1 month at $189/month + lab cost, or 3 months at $126/month + initial lab cost, framed as try 2 months of Oral Only™, get 1 free, with $63/month savings, free doctor consult, free shipping, free NAD+, third month free, and free lab support.

## Dr Jaquish / Founder-Led Creative

## Product / Positioning Notes from Rahul

- Product name: **Nandrogen**.
- Described by Rahul as a liquid/gel/liposomal form factor, positioned as a different delivery route than needle-based testosterone.
- Claimed advantage vs. pills: higher absorption than a pill.
- Claimed advantage vs. injections: fewer major side-effect patterns associated with injections, including intense fluctuations, estrogen complications, and testicular shutdown. Use only if supported by compliance/source docs.
- Biggest buyer segment per Rahul: men already familiar with TRT/injections who want to move from injections to oral.
- The current brand name "Oral Only" may be rebranded because it is weak for Google search and not ideal at scale.

## Funnel / Offer Notes

- Current ad destination at call time: landing page → quiz funnel → plan choices (monthly / 6-month / 12-month).
- Rahul is testing different quiz funnels; longer funnel reportedly seeing better results.
- Rahul is building separate landers and a long-form PDP similar to high-performing telehealth/wellness PDPs.
- Approved evergreen acquisition offer:
  - 1 month = **$189/month + lab cost**.
  - 3 months = **$126/month + initial lab cost**.
  - Offer frame: **Try 2 months of Oral Only™, get 1 free**.
  - Value stack: **$63 savings per month**, free doctor consult, free shipping, **NAD+ free** to support the TRT plan, third month free, and free lab support.
  - Goal: push more buyers toward 3-month commitment while preserving cohort/LTV measurement.
- Rahul may create an acquisition funnel with only 1-month and 3-month options so CAC:LTV can be tied to cleaner cohorts rather than long upfront terms.

## Performance / Business Context from Rahul

- Brand launched around end of January.
- Spend scaled quickly from roughly $300/day to ~$100k/month+; Rahul expected the account could reach ~$200k–$300k monthly if CAC improves.
- CAC at call time: around **$1,000**, target closer to **$600**.
- LTV at call time: around **$1,500**.
- Churn: around **7%**, which Rahul framed as very low versus typical 30–35%.
- There were around **1,000 active subscribers** at the time of the call.
- When the account moved away from Dr Jaquish whitelisting/content, ROAS dropped and buyers shifted more toward monthly plans rather than 6- or 12-month commitments. Interpretation: Dr Jaquish's audience/trust makes larger commitments easier.

## Compliance / Risk Notes from Rahul

- Rahul mentioned a cease-and-desist issue, mostly related to landing-page language. He suspects some competitor C&Ds in the category may be fake, intended to make brands pause top ads/funnels.
- Still, creative should avoid repeating landing-page claim mistakes and should reference the compliance doc once obtained.
- High-risk category: telehealth / health & wellness / testosterone. Assume all claims require sourcing and conservative phrasing.

## Creative Implications

1. **Prioritize Dr Jaquish authority ads**
   - Direct-to-camera scripts for him weekly.
   - Recuts from YouTube educational content.
   - Whitelisted ads from his profile whenever he is featured.

2. **Win the injection-switcher**
   - Focus on the man tired of needles, peaks/valleys, lab concerns, and the mental load of managing injections.
   - The buyer is often solution-aware/product-aware, not fully cold.

3. **Cold audience needs lower-commitment path**
   - Dr Jaquish's warm audience can buy 6/12 months; cold traffic may need 1/3-month entry, stronger value stack, and trust-building before big commitments.

4. **Use offer-stack creative once live**
   - Sticker price vs. offer price.
   - Third month free.
   - Free NAD+ / free labs / free consult / free shipping.
   - Show value stack visually, not only in copy.

5. **Avoid polished/AI-looking medical ads**
   - The brand already has skepticism risk. Prefer real iPhone content, real founder clips, real explainers, and source-backed graphics.
