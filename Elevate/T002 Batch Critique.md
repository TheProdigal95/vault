## Cross-Batch Patterns

**1. Price-forward hooks are prone to caption-VO echo.** Script #11 (payment plan breakdown) had both Hook A and Hook C captions restating the VO's price-reveal angle. When the concept IS the price, both channels naturally default to naming the number. The fix was to make captions enter from a different angle (disbelief, question, social proof) while VO delivers the price. This pattern is likely to recur in future price-forward concepts.

**2. Long-form scripts exceed pacing limits across multiple beats.** Script #15 had 5 separate VO lines exceeding 300 spm — the most pervasive pacing failure in the batch. Writers try to fit a complete emotional arc into timestamps without accounting for syllable density. The fix required both trimming VO and extending some timestamps (pushing total from ~75s to ~87s). Future long-form scripts should budget syllables per beat during outline phase, before writing full VO.

**3. "Food noise" is the dominant pain language.** Appears in 5/7 scripts + 1 brief. This is intentional (food noise was the #1 pain point from T001 data and top spenders), but the concentration means the batch's emotional range is narrow. T003 should explore secondary pain points (self-blame cycle, insurance frustration, failed diet fatigue) as primary hooks, with food noise as a supporting beat rather than the lead.

**4. VoC headline collision across brief formats.** Briefs #5 (Frosted Glass) and #8 (VoC Editorial) drew from the same review quote pool and landed on the identical headline. Different brief formats pulling from the same VoC source need a uniqueness check before dispatch — the brief-writer agents don't see each other's output during parallel writing.
