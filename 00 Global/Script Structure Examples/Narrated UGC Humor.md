# Script Structure: Narrated UGC Humor

Humor hook earns 5-8 seconds of attention, then a straight product pitch follows. The humor comes from the narrator's delivery and relatable scenarios, NOT from filmed slapstick. VO narrates over B-roll, stock, and text overlays — no creator on screen. The gear change from humor to product is intentional: don't try to be funny during selling.

### **Core Breakdown**
- **Structure:** Humor scenario hook (5-8s) → straight product pitch with benefit stacking → partner beat (single line) → review pop-up → urgency/offer close.
- **VO register:** Male narrator, conversational, slightly amused during hook. Shifts to confident and direct for body/close. "Guy telling a funny story to his friend, then recommending something he actually uses."
- **Time to benefit:** 5-8s. Product appears immediately after the humor hook.
- **Rhythm:** Fast. 1-3s shots during hook B-roll. Body shots hold slightly longer (2-4s) to let the product register. Close is one clean shot with overlay.
- **Duration:** 20-35s.
- **Close type:** Urgency/offer.
- **Visual grammar:** Hook = chaotic (warm, messy bedroom light, shaky handheld energy). Body = calm morning light (cool, soft, steady). The visual shift mirrors the humor → product gear change. No creator on screen — VO carries everything.
- **Hook defaults:** Three different humor scenarios per script, all feeding into the same body. POV roommate alarm. Tier list of wake-up methods. Before/after chaos vs. calm split.
- **Caption strategy:** Bold white sans-serif, semi-transparent dark background, all caps for key words. Text overlays appear on beat with narration.

### When to Use
- Products with a universally relatable pain point that has natural comedy (alarms, sleep chaos, morning routines)
- When the target audience is young and humor-native (scrolling TikTok/Reels)
- Short-form placements (20-35s) where attention must be earned fast
- When the brand has proven humor formats in their top spenders

### Key Rules
- **Humor = relatable exaggeration, not jokes.** Self-deprecating builds trust. "Your roommate's alarm at 5am — it's been going off for three minutes, he hasn't moved, you're the one turning it off" is relatable. A punchline is not.
- **Don't be funny during selling.** The humor hook earns attention. The product pitch is played straight. The shift should feel like a gear change, not a crash.
- **No filmed skits.** Creator narrates over B-roll, stock, product shots. This is narrated UGC, not a comedy video.
- **Product by second 6-8.** Same tempo as the top spenders. Humor runs 5-8 seconds, then product appears immediately.
- **Three hook scenarios per script.** Each is a different humor scenario with its own narration and B-roll. All share the same body and close.
