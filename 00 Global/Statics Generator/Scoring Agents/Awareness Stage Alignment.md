# Awareness Stage Alignment

Scores whether copy matches the intended awareness stage's tactics without bleeding into adjacent stages.

---

## What This Dimension Evaluates

Each awareness stage has a specific job. Copy must execute that job cleanly — one stage per ad. The hook type, body tactics, CTA pressure, and proof elements should all match the assigned stage. Stage-bleed (education mixed with hard close, agitation mixed with product comparison) dilutes the message and confuses the viewer.

## Stage Definitions (Summary)

| Stage | Viewer State | Goal | Tactics |
|-------|-------------|------|---------|
| Unaware | Don't know the problem exists | Educate, reveal | Pattern recognition, surprising facts, "did you know" |
| Problem-Aware | Know the problem, don't know solutions | Agitate, validate | Empathy, pain callouts, "tired of...?" questions |
| Solution-Aware | Know solutions exist, exploring options | Differentiate | Category education, comparison, "there's a better way" |
| Product-Aware | Know this product, considering it | Prove superiority | Proof, comparisons, guarantees, reviews |
| Most-Aware | Ready to purchase, need final push | Close the sale | Urgency, guarantees, direct CTA, limited offers |

## Scoring Criteria

### What earns points

1. **Hook type matches stage** — the headline/hook type from the 18 core types is appropriate for the assigned stage per the Headline & Text Hook Criteria deployment table
2. **Tactics stay in-stage** — body content uses the tactics appropriate for the stage (education for Unaware, agitation for Problem-Aware, differentiation for Solution-Aware, proof for Product-Aware, closing for Most-Aware)
3. **CTA pressure matches stage** — soft/educational for Unaware, medium for middle stages, direct/urgent for Most-Aware
4. **Proof type matches stage** — mechanism education for early stages, social proof and comparisons for middle, guarantees and urgency for late
5. **No stage-bleed** — the ad does not try to serve two stages simultaneously

### What loses points

1. **Adjacent-stage bleed** — copy starts in the correct stage but drifts (e.g., Problem-Aware hook with Solution-Aware body that compares alternatives)
2. **CTA mismatch** — Unaware ad with a "Buy Now" CTA, or Most-Aware ad with a "Learn More" CTA
3. **Wrong proof type** — using testimonials and star ratings in an Unaware ad (the viewer doesn't know the product category yet)
4. **Hook-body disconnect** — hook correctly targets the stage but body shifts to adjacent-stage tactics

## Calibration

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 60 | Correct stage targeted but tactics bleed into adjacent stage. The primary intent is right, execution drifts. | Problem-Aware hook ("Still waking up exhausted?") but body pivots to Solution-Aware comparison ("unlike melatonin, this targets...") — the viewer hasn't been shown alternatives exist yet |
| 80 | Tactics match stage throughout, one minor bleed. The ad largely stays on-stage with a small drift in CTA or proof. | Solution-Aware ad with correct differentiation body, but CTA says "Buy Now with 60-day guarantee" (Product-Aware closing pressure) instead of "See how it compares" |
| 95 | Crisp single-stage execution. Hook type is appropriate per deployment table, body tactics match, CTA pressure calibrated, no bleed. | Problem-Aware: Provocation hook naming the pain, body validates and agitates with persona-specific details, CTA invites exploration ("See what's actually causing it") |

---

## Instant Fail (score capped at 59)

- **Mixing Unaware education with Most-Aware hard CTA in the same ad.** These stages are separated by three levels of awareness. An ad that opens with "Did you know your gut lining replaces itself every 3 days?" and closes with "ORDER NOW — 40% OFF TODAY ONLY" is incoherent. The viewer who doesn't know they have a problem is not ready for a purchase close.

---

## Feedback Format

When score is below 90, feedback must include:
1. The assigned stage and which stage the copy actually executes
2. The specific lines where stage-bleed occurs
3. What those lines should do instead — named in terms of the correct stage's tactics (e.g., "this comparison belongs in Solution-Aware; for Problem-Aware, agitate the pain instead of introducing alternatives")
