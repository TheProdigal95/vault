# Persona Fit

Scores whether copy speaks to the pain-trigger persona using VoC language from real reviews.

---

## What This Dimension Evaluates

The copy must make the assigned persona recognize themselves immediately. That means using the language THEY use to describe their pain, not a marketer's paraphrase of it. The stronger the VoC language match, the higher the score.

---

## Required Reads

- `[Brand]/00 Context/Persona Context.md` — persona definitions, pain triggers, language patterns
- `[Brand]/00 Research/Review Analysis.md` — Standout Language bucket (the exact phrases reviewers use that are more vivid than anything a copywriter would invent)

---

## Scoring Criteria

### What earns points

1. **Exact Standout Language phrases** from the Review Analysis appear in the copy (highest weight)
2. **Pain-trigger naming** — the persona's core pain is named explicitly, not implied
3. **Persona-specific context** — life details that only THIS persona would recognize (not generic "busy people" language)
4. **Natural integration** — VoC language woven into the copy flow, not dropped in as isolated quotes
5. **Condition/persona identifier in the hook** — per Universal Copy Rules, the viewer must know which persona this ad targets before the hook ends

### What loses points

1. **Demographic callouts** used as the primary hook or identification mechanism ("busy moms," "men over 40," "active professionals")
2. **Generic paraphrasing** — the pain is mentioned but in marketer language, not customer language
3. **Wrong persona's language** — VoC phrases that belong to a different persona in the same brand
4. **Missing pain-trigger** — the copy talks about the product/solution without establishing the specific pain this persona feels
5. **Persona established late** — pain-trigger or condition appears in the body but not in the hook

---

## Calibration

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 60 | Correct persona targeted but generic language. The pain category is right, the words are marketer-speak. | "People struggling with sleep issues" when the VoC says "I stare at the ceiling for three hours every night" |
| 80 | Persona pain named explicitly, some VoC language present. The viewer would nod, but the phrasing is partially paraphrased. | "Still waking up exhausted after 8 hours" — close to VoC but not the exact Standout Language phrase "I sleep 8 hours and wake up feeling like I pulled an all-nighter" |
| 95 | Exact Standout Language phrases from the Review Analysis. The persona would read this and think "someone wrote this about me." | Uses "I sleep 8 hours and wake up feeling like I pulled an all-nighter" directly, with persona-specific context woven naturally into hook and body |

---

## Instant Fail (score capped at 59)

- **Demographic callout as the primary hook.** If the first identification mechanism is a demographic label ("busy moms," "men over 40," "women in their 30s") rather than a pain-trigger or life situation, the copy fails regardless of how good the rest is. Personas are named by pain/trigger, never by demographics.

---

## Feedback Format

When score is below 90, feedback must include:
1. The specific text that triggered the deduction
2. What it should reference instead (exact Standout Language phrase from Review Analysis, or the persona-specific context from Persona Context)
3. A concrete rewrite direction — not "use more VoC" but "replace 'trouble sleeping' with the Standout Language phrase '[exact phrase]' from Review Analysis"
