# T004 — IM8 AppLovin Video

---

## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

**Format north star:** "How can I tell someone exactly everything they need to know about my product in 30 seconds so that they will click and buy and check out today." Every script is a complete mini-ad by the 28-second mark.

**Dual-CTA architecture:**
- CTA #1 before 30s (before they can skip the ad)
- CTA #2 at the end
- Total duration: 45-55 seconds
- Part 1 (0-28s) = complete selling unit: single pain → mechanism → product → offer CTA
- Part 2 (28-50s) = expanded proof, additional benefits, social proof → close CTA
- Hook rate is less important than angle quality and the pre-skip selling unit

**Current top spenders (last week):**
- AI character doctor — doing well
- "Most women going through menopause are missing 4 nutrients..." — doing well
- "A researcher with over 300 published papers" — doing well
- GLP-1 Muscle Loss Explainer — doing well

**Batch direction:**
- Rework winning concepts/structures to the dual-CTA architecture
- More menopause (8) — each focused on a single pain point for Part 1, can mention others in Part 2
- More gut health (4) — compressed 90-day timelines with rapid milestones, CTA before 30s, expand in Part 2
- GLP-1 (3) — rework Muscle Loss Explainer + 2 new angles
- At least 6 scripts with narrative arcs that don't feel like a hard sell
- More authority / doctors

## Script Criteria

> **Baseline:** All rules in `00 Global/Criteria/` apply automatically. Read Universal Copy Rules + Video Script Criteria + Headline & Text Hook Criteria before writing.

### Compliance

Full guardrails: [[Guardrails & Claims - IM8]]. Script-specific rules:

- **Don't over-hedge.** "Supports" is the default confident tone for structure/function claims. "May support" or "may help" only when the claim could be interpreted as treating or curing. Most of IM8's claims are standard structure/function and need no hedging at all.
- Approved stats — exact phrasing only: "95% of users felt more energy," "85% of users felt better digestion," "80% of users felt they slept better," "75% of users noticed sharper focus." Never abbreviate ("95% more energy" is a different claim).
- Beckham = "co-founder." Sabalenka = "shareholder and ambassador." Never "endorses" or "spokesperson."
- "Replaces 16+" and "90 clinically dosed ingredients" and "NSF Certified for Sport" — all approved.
- No competitor brand names in ads. Use solution categories: "probiotics," "greens powders," "single-ingredient supplements."
- No disease treatment claims. No "works like Ozempic." No specific weight loss numbers.

### Dual-CTA Architecture (T004-specific)

- **Every script follows the dual-CTA structure.** Part 1 (0-28s) is a complete selling unit: single pain point → mechanism → product name → CTA #1. Part 2 (28-50s) expands with additional proof, more benefits, social proof → CTA #2.
- **CTA #1 is an offer/action CTA** — not a soft "learn more." E.g., "Get 50% Off Now," "Claim Your Welcome Kit," "Try It Risk-Free." Must feel like a real off-ramp for viewers about to skip.
- **CTA #2 is a trust-stacked close** — layers proof types: clinical trial stat → celebrity/authority → certification → guarantee. This CTA rewards viewers who stayed.
- **Part 1 must stand alone.** If you cut the video at 0:28, a viewer should know: the problem, why it happens, what IM8 does about it, and how to get it. No "but first, let me explain" setups.
- **Duration: 45-55 seconds.** Tighter than T003's 30-60s window. Scripts over 55s must be trimmed.
- **Single pain point in Part 1.** Each menopause script owns one pain point for the first 28 seconds. Other benefits only appear in Part 2. Gut health timelines compress all milestones into Part 1 as rapid bullets.

### Platform Constraints

- **AppLovin skip point: ~30 seconds.** The entire dual-CTA architecture is designed around this. CTA #1 must land before the skip.
- Offer elements (welcome kit, guarantee) can now appear in-script at CTA #1, not just in the platform end card.

### Product Specifics

- **Ingredient count: 90** (confirmed by client April 2026). Not 92 (homepage number).
- **Product page: get.im8health.com** — source of truth for dosages, claims, and specs.
- Use "nutrients" not "ingredients" when talking about what the body needs.

### Taste Beat

- Not mandatory. Must earn its place — if timing is tight or the concept doesn't naturally lead to a sip moment, cut it. With the compressed Part 1, most scripts won't have room for a taste beat before CTA #1. Can appear in Part 2.

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

1. Ingredient count = 90 (not 92)
2. Stats match approved phrasing exactly — "X% OF USERS FELT [benefit]" not "X% [benefit]"
3. Compliance hedging appropriate — "supports" not over-hedged to "may help support" everywhere
4. Beckham/Sabalenka titles correct (co-founder / shareholder and ambassador)
5. No competitor brand names (solution categories only)
6. Duration fits 45-55s window at 250 spm
7. **Pain-first language** — flag any VO line that opens with an ingredient name or mechanism term before the pain point
8. **IM8 named before CTA #1** — product name must appear in Part 1 before the first CTA. No script where the viewer hits the skip point without knowing the product name
9. **CTA #1 is a real offer** — not a soft "learn more." Must include a specific action or value proposition
10. **Part 1 stands alone** — if you cut the video at 0:28, a viewer knows the problem, the mechanism, and the product
11. **Single pain point in Part 1** — no multi-symptom laundry lists before CTA #1. One pain, one mechanism, one product
12. **Data balance** — if VO delivers data, visuals must carry emotional engagement, not literal data visualization
13. **Visual column readability** — `<br><br>` between distinct visual beats in the Visual column

### Temporal Discounting

Show immediate or early benefits to reduce perceived wait time. With the compressed Part 1, temporal discounting language should appear before CTA #1 where relevant.

- **Menopause:** #1 ← add, #3 ← add ("week two"), #7 ← add
- **Gut Health:** #9-11 ✅ (timeline by definition), #12 ← add
- **GLP-1:** #13 ← add, #14 ← add, #15 ← add

### Early Product Mention

- **Every script must name IM8 in Part 1 before CTA #1.** Non-negotiable with the dual-CTA architecture. The viewer hitting skip at 30s must know the product.

### On-Screen Caption Variety in Hooks

- Hook on-screen captions must not all focus on the same angle across the 3 variations. At least one variation must use a winner-inspired caption pattern.
- Captions never repeat the VO verbatim — two channels, two messages.

### Data Balance Rule

- When VO delivers data/facts, visuals must carry emotional engagement — not literal data visualization.
- If all three channels (VO, captions, visuals) simultaneously deliver factual content, at least one must shift to emotional or visually engaging content.

### Narrative Arc Standard

- At least 6 scripts in the batch must build a narrative arc that doesn't feel like a hard sell. Story flow from problem to resolution, not a data dump with a CTA bolted on.
- Concepts #4, #5, #7, #8, #11, #14 are the primary narrative-arc scripts. Their Part 1 should feel like the start of a story, not a compressed pitch.
