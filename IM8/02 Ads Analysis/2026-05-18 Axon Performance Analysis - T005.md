### Menopause Four Nutrients hook 2 — T002 — Spend: $6,811 / CTR: 0.89%

**Hook VO:** "Most women going through menopause are missing four nutrients that could change how they feel in weeks. Number four might surprise you."
**Hook Caption:** "4 NUTRIENTS THAT COULD CHANGE YOUR MENOPAUSE IN WEEKS"
**Hook type:** Curiosity gap (with count tease — "number four might surprise you")
**Structure:** Numbered reasons — mini-PAS per reason (symptom → nutrient → how IM8 delivers it)
**Caption style:** Medium sans-serif on semi-transparent grey. Kinetic numbered header cards (#1, #2, #3, #4) with bold ingredient callouts as overlays.
**Persuasion techniques:** Specificity (exact doses for each nutrient), numbered listicle structure creating pacing rhythm, curiosity tease on the count, pain-first per reason, dose credibility (83X DV for B12)
**Visual approach:** Lifestyle B-roll led. No CGI. Bold numbered-header cards and kinetic ingredient callouts layered as overlays. Fast and punchy — each reason gets 7-8 seconds.
**Close type:** Ease/simplicity — "All four of those, plus 86 more nutrients, are in one scoop of IM8 every morning. No pill organizer, no guessing."
**Body scaffold:** 4 nutrients each with mini-PAS beat: (1) Saffron 30mg → mood swings; (2) B12 83X DV → bone-deep tired feeling; (3) Probiotics 10B + pre/post → bloating; (4) D3 250% DV + K2 → bones thinning. Product revealed as the delivery vehicle after all four are named.
**What likely drove performance:** The curiosity tease ("number four might surprise you") creates a forward pull that competes with the skip button through the full four-reason structure. Specificity on every dose — 30mg, 83X DV, 10 billion, 250% DV — reads as credible rather than promotional. The structure does the pacing work: viewers can track exactly where they are in the ad, which reduces drop-off at each transition.
