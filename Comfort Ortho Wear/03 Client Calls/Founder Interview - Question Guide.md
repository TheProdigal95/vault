# Founder Interview — Question Guide

**Subject:** Sam P., founder of ComfortWear
**Format:** Guided selfie-style video (Sam on camera, Marcelo directing via call)
**Goal:** Extract 5-8 usable sound bites for founder interview ads. Each question is designed to produce a self-contained clip that works as a hook or body beat.

> Sam is camera-shy and has never done this. Keep the energy conversational. Let him ramble — the best lines come from unscripted tangents. If he gives a short answer, use the follow-up prompts.

---

## Setup Instructions for Sam

- Selfie angle, natural light (face a window), quiet room
- Phone vertical or horizontal — we'll use both formats
- Casual clothes, not a branded shirt (authenticity > promotion)
- No script, no prep — just answer honestly

---

## Questions

### Block 1 — Origin Story (the "why")

- [x] **Q1: "Tell me about the people in your life who are healthcare workers."**
- We want: who specifically (mom, sister, aunt?), what they do, where they work
- Follow-up if short: "What's their typical day look like?"
- Target sound bite: *"My [mom/sister] is a nurse at [hospital]. She works 12-hour shifts on concrete."*

- [x] **Q2: "What would you see when they came home from a shift?"**
- We want: visual, physical details — limping, icing feet, complaining, kicking off shoes
- Follow-up: "Was there a specific moment that stuck with you?"
- Target sound bite: *"She'd come home and just collapse on the couch, shoes off, rubbing her feet."*

- [x] **Q3: "What did they tell you about their shoes?"**
- We want: the rotation cost insight, shoes wearing out, the 6-month cycle
- Follow-up: "How much were they spending on shoes?"
- Target sound bite: *"They told me you have to replace your shoes every six months. And good ones aren't cheap."*

- [x] **Q4: "What was happening in the world when you decided to actually do something about it?"**
- We want: COVID context — overworked nurses, crisis conditions
- Follow-up: "What did that feel like, watching them go through that?"
- Target sound bite: *"It was during COVID. They were working double shifts, completely overworked, and their shoes were falling apart."*

### Block 2 — The Mission (the "what I did about it")

- [x] **Q5: "So what did you do? Walk me through how this shoe came to exist."**
- We want: the entrepreneurial journey, working with manufacturers, the design goals (affordable + comfortable + slip-grip)
- Follow-up: "What were the three things the shoe had to do?"
- Target sound bite: *"I said, I'm going to make a shoe that nurses can actually afford, that doesn't fall apart in three months, and that keeps them safe on wet hospital floors."*

- [x] **Q6: "Your shoes are $60. Most orthopedic shoes are $130-175. Why so cheap?"**
- We want: the pricing philosophy, the "maybe I priced it too low" admission
- Follow-up: "Did people tell you you were undercharging?"
- Target sound bite: *"Honestly, I might have priced it too low. But nurses aren't rich. They shouldn't have to choose between rent and shoes that don't hurt."*

### Block 3 — The Product (what's actually in the shoe)

- [x] **Q7: "Walk me through the shoe. If I cut it in half, what am I looking at?"**
- We want: him explaining the three core components in his own words — the heel cushioning, the arch insole, the wide toe box. Not marketing language, his language.
- Follow-up: "What's the part most people notice first when they put it on?"
- Target sound bite: *"The heel is where most of the magic is — it absorbs the shock so your joints don't. And then the insole lifts your arch so your foot isn't just sitting flat."*

- [x] **Q8: "What's the deal with the stretchy material on top?"**
- We want: StretchFit Knit explained for people with bunions, swelling, wide feet — why stretch matters
- Follow-up: "Do people with bunions tell you it helps?"
- Target sound bite: *"It stretches around your foot instead of squeezing it. People with bunions love that — it doesn't press on the sore spots."*

- [x] **Q9: "A lot of your customers are nurses, kitchen workers — people on wet floors. Talk to me about the grip."**
- We want: the slip-resistant outsole story — was this something he saw nurses need? Did someone slip?
- Follow-up: "Was that something nurses specifically asked for, or did you just know?"
- Target sound bite: *"Nurses told me — you can't just make a comfy shoe. If I slip and break my hip, comfort doesn't matter."*

- [ ] **Q10: "Your shoe says it helps with plantar fasciitis, bunions, neuropathy, back pain — that's a lot. How does one shoe do all of that?"**
- We want: him connecting the mechanism to the conditions. Not a medical lecture — just how arch support + heel cushion + toe room addresses different problems differently.
- Follow-up: "Which condition do you hear about the most from customers?"
- Target sound bite: *"Plantar fasciitis is the big one. That's heel pain — and the heel cushion goes right at it. Bunions is the toe box. Back pain is alignment — the arch."*

### Block 4 — The Guarantee and the Offer

- [x] **Q11: "You offer a 45-day money-back guarantee. Why 45 days?"**
- We want: the logic behind the window — is it because it takes a few shifts to break in? Confidence in the product? What happens when people actually try to return?
- Follow-up: "How many people actually return them?"
- Target sound bite: *"Honestly, almost nobody sends them back. I give you 45 days because I know by shift three, you're not going to want to."*

- [ ] **Q12: "Your shoes are HSA/FSA eligible. What does that actually mean for a buyer?"**
- We want: him explaining the savings in plain language — using pre-tax health dollars, saving 30%, the fact that most people don't realize they can use their benefits on shoes
- Follow-up: "Do people tell you they didn't know they could use their HSA on shoes?"
- Target sound bite: *"Most people don't know this. You can use your health savings account to buy these shoes. That takes your $60 shoe down to like $40."*

- [ ] **Q13: "What's the best deal someone can get right now?"**
- We want: bundles, the 10% off when you buy two, the sock add-on. Let him pitch naturally.
- Follow-up: "Do most people come back for a second pair?"
- Target sound bite: *"A lot of people buy two right away — one for work, one for weekends. You save 10% when you bundle."*

### Block 5 — Customer Stories and Reviews

- [x] **Q14: "You've sold to over 500,000 people. Is there a customer message or review that you still think about?"**
- We want: a specific story, a specific person if possible — not generic "people love it." Push for the one that moved him.
- Follow-up: "What did they say exactly? Do you remember?"
- Follow-up 2: "Have you ever screenshot'd a review and sent it to someone?"
- Target sound bite: *"There was this nurse — she said she did her first 12-hour shift without pain in years. She ordered three more pairs the next day."*

- [x] **Q15: "What's the most surprising thing a customer has told you about the shoes?"**
- We want: unexpected use cases, unexpected conditions helped, unexpected emotions — things we can't get from reviews alone
- Follow-up: "Anything that made you think 'I didn't even design the shoe for that'?"
- Target sound bite: *"Someone told me she started going on walks with her grandkids again. She hadn't done that in two years. That wasn't even what I built the shoe for."*

- [ ] **Q16: "Do you ever hear from customers who tried expensive orthotics or $150 shoes and then switched to yours?"**
- We want: the competitor switch story from his perspective — what do people say about the shoes they left behind?
- Follow-up: "What brands do they mention? What were they paying before?"
- Target sound bite: *"All the time. They'll say 'I spent $175 on [brand] and your $60 shoe is better.' That's the whole point."*

### Block 6 — Objections He Hears

- [x] **Q17: "When someone hesitates to buy — what's the reason they give?"**
- We want: real objections from customer service, DMs, emails — price skepticism, "too good to be true," sizing worries
- Follow-up: "What do you tell them?"
- Target sound bite: *"They think it's too cheap to be good. I get it. That's why there's a guarantee — try it, and if I'm wrong, I'll give you your money back."*

- [x] **Q18: "Sizing is tricky with shoes online. What do you tell someone who's nervous about ordering the wrong size?"**
- We want: the half-size-up recommendation in his voice, the exchange process, reassurance
- Follow-up: "Is there a trick to getting the right fit?"
- Target sound bite: *"Go half a size up. If it's not right, we'll exchange it. But the stretch material adjusts to your foot, so most people are fine."*

### Block 7 — What's Different (vs. the competition)

- [ ] **Q19: "If someone's comparing you to OrthoFeet, Kuru, Dansko — the $130, $150 shoes — what would you say?"**
- We want: his competitive framing — not trash-talking, but the honest value argument
- Follow-up: "Is there something your shoe does that theirs doesn't?"
- Target sound bite: *"Those are fine shoes. But you're paying double or triple for the brand name. The technology in our shoe is the same level of support."*

- [x] **Q20: "A lot of people try insoles or memory foam first. Why doesn't that work?"**
- We want: the "failed solution" framing — why insoles are a band-aid, why memory foam flattens, why the shoe needs to be designed for support from the ground up
- Follow-up: "What happens to memory foam after a few weeks?"
- Target sound bite: *"Memory foam flattens. Insoles shift around. You need the support built into the shoe — not stuck on top of a bad shoe."*

### Block 8 — Emotional Payoff (the "what it means")

- [ ] **Q21: "What does it feel like to know you've helped half a million people with their pain?"**
- We want: genuine emotion, pride, surprise at scale — this is the "zoom out" moment
- Follow-up: "Did you ever think it would get this big?"
- Target sound bite: *"Honestly, no. I thought I was making a shoe for nurses. Now it's grandmothers, teachers, warehouse workers — people I never expected."*

- [x] **Q22: "If a nurse is watching this right now and she's about to start another shift in shoes that hurt — what would you tell her?"**
- We want: direct-to-camera address, empathy + solution. This is the CTA beat.
- Follow-up: "Pretend you're talking to your [family member]."
- Target sound bite: *"I'd say — I built this for you. Literally. Try it. If it doesn't work, send it back. But I think you'll feel the difference by hour six."*

---

## Production Notes

- This is a longer interview now — plan for 45-60 min. Best lines come from minutes 15+ when he's relaxed.
- Blocks 1-2 (origin + mission) are warm-up territory. Don't rush to product — let him settle in on the story he knows.
- Blocks 3-6 (product, guarantee, reviews, objections) are where the usable body beats live. Push for specifics here — names, numbers, exact stories.
- If Sam freezes on a question, reframe it: "Just tell me the story like you'd tell a friend at dinner."
- After each block, let him sit in silence for 3-4 seconds — people often add their best line right after they think they're done.
- Flag any moment where his energy shifts (gets quiet, gets passionate, laughs) — those are the clips.
- If time is short, cut Block 7 (competition) — we can frame that in post. Blocks 3-5 are non-negotiable.
