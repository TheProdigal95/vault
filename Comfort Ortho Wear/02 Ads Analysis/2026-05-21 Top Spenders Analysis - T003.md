## Key Learnings for T003

**T003 should be evergreen video only.** No Memorial Day, holiday, or dated seasonal-sale concepts. The live angle read points to enduring pain, trust, value, and professional-use messaging; seasonal urgency would distract from the strongest signals and age out quickly.

**Delegate 15 videos, not a mixed static/video batch.** T002 established that AI-native video formats can scale production without waiting on UGC footage. T003 should use that unlock for focused evergreen testing: 15 delegated videos with each concept built around a clear angle.

**Recommended split: 6 nurse pain / 3 trust-proof / 3 value / 3 travel.** Pain Elimination is still the spend leader, so nurses and long-shift pain get the largest allocation. Authenticity & Trust and Affordability & Value are the fastest-growing strategic signals, so each gets a controlled 3-video lane. Travel gets 3 because the owner wants more testing, even though current spend is tiny.

**No dedicated retargeting quick-action slot in this batch.** T001/T002 show RTG can convert hard, but T003 has only 15 delegated video slots and the owner-requested third travel concept takes the available flex slot. Retargeting quick-action can return when the batch is not being used to extend travel learning.

## Carry-Forward Creative Learnings

**Ranked comparison and competitor elimination still work.** T001's biggest spend came from ranking popular shoes against ComfortWear. T002 evolved that into three-problem debunking and podiatrist/authority-style ratings. T003 should keep the structure, but vary the problem set so the batch does not become another Hoka/Skechers foam-collapse remix.

**Nurses remain the profit center.** Prior winners are strongest when they name the working context: 12-hour shifts, concrete or hospital floors, swelling by hour eight, plantar fasciitis, wet floors, and limping to the car. T003 nurse videos should stay situational, not generic "comfortable shoe" ads.

**First-person narrative works when features become story beats.** The T002 nurse narrative converted because arch support, wide toe box, cleaning, slip resistance, price, guarantee, and coworker proof appeared inside a believable shift story. T003 trust and value scripts should use this pattern instead of listing product features.

**Travel needs specificity, not broad vacation advice.** The strongest travel read was a story arc: failed shoes in specific cities, cobblestones, benches, ruined afternoons, then the nurse-friend bridge. T003 travel videos should use concrete trips, surfaces, and consequences; avoid generic "best travel shoe" framing unless it has a ranked/test structure.

**RTG learnings are useful, but not the T003 allocation.** T001/T002 warm-audience ads proved quick recognition, UGC proof, and CTA proximity can work. Those lessons should inform closes and proof moments, but the batch itself is TOF/planning-focused evergreen video because the third travel slot replaced the RTG quick-action test.

## Risks / Guardrails

**Cap the nurse-friend / coworker discovery bridge.** It worked in T002, especially for travel, but overuse will make the batch feel templated. Maximum three scripts should use that bridge.

**Cap foam-collapse framing.** Foam dying by hour six is a proven mechanism, but T003 should also test shift durability, swelling, value, grip, and end-of-day relief. Maximum three scripts should lean on foam collapse as the main mechanism.

**Do not default to the founder story.** Use founder/origin material only when it directly answers trust or price skepticism. It should not become a generic layer inside every video.

**Compliance caveats stay active.** Use exact approved guarantee language from the working doc, avoid fabricated doctors/studies/certifications, keep competitor logos blurred, caption competitor names with masking, and verify any current discount before scripts go live.
