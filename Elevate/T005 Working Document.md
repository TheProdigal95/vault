## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

Full analysis: [[2026-05-26 Top Spenders Analysis - T005]]

### What the L7 data says

- **Financial Accessibility = 90% of account spend.** Every converting creative is built on price access, payment plans, or insurance alternative. Nothing else converts at scale.
- **"This Failed You" split screen is the #1 ad** ($12K spend, 15 purchases, $803 CPP). Pure typographic blame framing — "Your Insurance Failed You | Our Payment Plans Won't." No photography, no product. The anger-at-the-system narrative hasn't been tested in video yet.
- **Shame to Pride Selfie Walk is the only converting video** ($602 spend, 2 purchases, $301 CPP, 3.07 ROAS). Hold 79, Thruplay 79%. Emotional vulnerability hook ("I was so nervous to start medical weight loss") + inspirational IG audio + text wall + aspirational b-roll. Structure is proven — needs more emotional entry points.
- **Eligibility Payment Logos is the most efficient converter** ($986 spend, 4 purchases, $247 CPP, 1.51 ROAS). Information gap headline + real person photo + payment logo wall. Low spend but highest ROAS.
- **Real People Results V2 is the highest ROAS in the account** ($378 spend, 1 purchase, $378 CPP, 3.97 ROAS). Pure price-hero typographic — "$8/Day. That's It." Untested at scale.
- **Whitelist native photos killed.** Maria Desk ($11.6K, 2 purchases, $5,799 CPP) and Maria Lunch ($2.6K + $1.3K, 0 purchases). Massive CTR/engagement but doesn't convert. Already killed.
- **No video converts at scale.** Shame to Pride is efficient but at $602 total spend. The video side of the account needs a scalable winner — that's the job of this batch.

### T005 direction

**Part 1 — Video (14 scripts, delegated):**

Three format tracks:

**Track A: Selfie walk expansion (5 scripts)**
- Proven structure: inspirational IG audio + text wall + aspirational b-roll + emotional arc

- Every script carries: financial accessibility angle, price point ($58/week or $8/day), payment plan mention, eligibility CTA

**Track C: Net-new formats from IM8 (5 scripts)**
- Formats proven on IM8's account, untested on Elevate. Format variety play.
- **Progression Timeline (3 scripts):**
  - *Benefits Timeline* — "What happens to your body after starting medical weight loss." Emotional through-line from the start (mirrors the selfie walk's vulnerability opening), milestone cards layered with both physical changes (food noise, energy, clothes) and emotional beats (relief, confidence, wanting to tell people). ## Concept Validation

> Runs after the Batch Plan is drafted, before script/brief assignments begin. Each concept passes the 7 tests individually; the differentiation rule is a batch-level cross-check.

### 7-Test Quality Bar (per concept)

1. **Pain point specificity** — built around a specific human tension, not a product feature. Complete: "This concept exists because [specific person] is struggling with [specific experience]." If the pain could apply to any brand in the category, not specific enough.
2. **Strategic coherence** — audience, message, structure, and hook work together. No component feels bolted on or generic.
3. **Differentiation** — couldn't be used by a competitor without modification. Specific to this brand's positioning, product, or audience.
4. **Format ambition** — visual format chosen intentionally, not by default. The format meaningfully contributes to attention or persuasion.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — the hook's execution creates felt tension (how it presents the pain, not what the pain is). Names a problem, challenges a belief, or makes the viewer feel personally implicated.
6. **Testable hypothesis** — "Why it can work" is grounded in behavioral or pattern insights. Specific enough to succeed or fail clearly.
7. **Producible** — a creative team can execute without major clarification.

### Differentiation Rule (batch-level cross-check)

Each concept must shift at least 2 of these 4 axes from every other concept in the batch:

- Intended audience (persona)
- Messaging angle
- Visual format / structure
- Hook tactic

### Conflict Resolution

When inputs conflict (data patterns suggest vulnerability, brand tone suggests authority):

- Persuasive sharpness > brand softness
- Pattern-backed > untested brand preference
- Specificity > generality
- If unresolvable, favor what triggers a reaction

### COMPLIANCE (Read First)

**Before writing anything for Elevate, read [[Compliance - Marketing Rules]].** Every script, headline, caption, and VO line must pass the compliance checklist. Compliance rules override every other criteria doc. Non-compliance is an automatic fail.

Summary of the hardest constraints:
- **No "GLP-1" as a product name.** Not in VO, captions, or close cards. Can appear in biological/educational context only.
- **No brand names** (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza) anywhere except the mandatory brand-ownership disclaimer.
- **No "same active ingredient as [brand]"** — FDA warned companies for this exact phrasing.
- **No specific weight numbers, percentages, or timeframes** as results claims.
- **No drug imagery.** No vials, syringes, pens, medication packaging in any Visual column.
- **No "safe," "effective," "well-tolerated," "zero side effects," "clinically proven."**
- **Pricing decoupled from drug.** "Programs starting at $58/week" — never "GLP-1 for $58/week."
- **CTAs lead to medical review.** "See if you qualify" / "Take the quiz" / "Check eligibility."
- **Testimonials describe experience, not results.** "I finally felt supported" ✓. "I lost 50 lbs" ✗.

### Elevate-Specific Rules

- **First-party source of truth.** Every factual claim must be verified against [[Product Context - GLP-1 Programs]]. Never copy facts from existing ads.
- **Price framing.** "Programs from $58/week" is the default. Never tie pricing directly to a drug.
- **Shipping framing.** "Ships in 3-7 business days after approval."
- **Payment plan logos are a design element.** Affirm, Afterpay, Klarna, HSA/FSA — prominently featured in close cards and overlays, not buried.
- **VoC quotes must come from Elevate Trustpilot reviews or research sprints.** Experience only, not results. Don't invent quotes.
- **Tonal range:** Empathetic, grounded, validating. Audience skews female. Provocative OK. Absurdist humor is not.
- **Compliance is a rulebook, not a vibe check.** If a line doesn't break a specific rule in [[Compliance - Marketing Rules]], keep its edge. Don't pre-emptively soften punchy lines.

### Whitelisting Rules (from T003)

- **Always use First Person ("I", "my")** — these run from persona pages, not the brand account.
- **Discovery:** Through a friend, coworker, or family member who mentioned it — never "I was searching online."
- **Payment plans mentioned in every script.** $58/week, $0 down, Affirm/Klarna/Afterpay. Beat 9/10 minimum.
- **Experience language only.** "The food noise stopped," "I feel like me again," "I have energy." Never pounds, percentages, or timeframes.
- **Character consistency:** Each character voice has locked avatar, voice, and background settings. AI avatar must match the defined profile for all scripts within a group.

### Video-Specific Rules (from T002/T004 learnings)

- **Caption-VO angle separation.** Captions and VO use different hook types per [[Headline & Text Hook Criteria]]. When the concept is price-forward, captions enter from a different angle (disbelief, social proof, question) while VO delivers the number.
- **Syllable budgeting for long-form.** Long-form scripts must have syllable counts per beat verified against timestamps before writing full VO. 300 spm hard limit, 250 spm body target.
- **Close caption stacks proof points** (patient count, bloodwork, payment plans) while VO delivers the emotional beat. Two channels, two messages.
- **Food noise spread.** Food noise may appear as a supporting beat in multiple scripts but as the PRIMARY hook/angle in no more than 2 scripts across the batch. T002 overindexed on this — diversify.
- **"Medical weight loss" category anchoring.** Every hook (either VO or caption) must anchor the viewer in the category.
- **Caption-VO echo ban.** Captions must NOT be third-person translations of first-person VO. Each channel carries genuinely independent information.
- **Product bridge uniqueness.** Each script's bridge to the product must be concept-specific (cost savings, biological plateaus, skepticism, timing) — not an interchangeable placeholder.
- **Offer hook variation.** Every concept must include at least one hook variation with price-focused on-screen captions ($58/week, $0 down, ships in 3-7 days). The VO can enter from a different angle while the caption leads with the offer.
- **$8/day price reframe test.** ~30% of offer-focused hook captions use "$8/DAY" instead of "$58/WEEK" — mirrors the Real People Results V2 price-hero format (3.97 ROAS). Applied to 10 hooks across all three tracks. Remaining hooks keep $58/week for A/B comparison.
