- **Music:** Upbeat, motivational, rhythmic instrumental. Approximately 120-125 BPM. Modern, energetic, slightly futuristic feel. Cuts synced to beat.

**Ad Breakdown**

Paid Partnership with IM8 Health. Posted December 16, 2025.

- **Visual:** Simple looping close-up of Tavi Castro pouring IM8 red powder from a sachet into the red I·M·8 branded tumbler. Product box visible on the left. Minimal production — feels authentic, not scripted.
- **Primary text (caption):** `I've been using IM8 for 10 months, and I feel the difference: energy, clarity, recovery—all elevated. Use code TAVI10 for 10% off your first order.`
- **On-screen caption (large text overlay on semi-transparent dark background):**

> 10 months using @im8health daily. Gut health has been flawless. Immune system has been 100% even through intense ocean expeditions, Freediving, and travel. Recovery injuries / infections 100%. Sleep HRV is higher. Dehydration control has been solid and cramping during training eliminated. The easy sachets have helped me stay more consistent than ever.

- **CTA bar:** `Invest in your Health now -10%: TAVI10` with a `Try now` link button
- **Sound:** Muted by default (speaker icon with X)

**Why it works:**

- **10-month window** — Not 30 or 90 days but almost a year. This is the longest timeframe in the top 8 and builds massive credibility. He stuck with it and has sustained results.
- **Athlete-specific extreme use cases** — ocean expeditions, freediving, intense training. Makes casual users think "if it works for him under those conditions, it'll work for me."
- **Stacking specific results** — gut health, immune system, recovery, sleep/HRV, dehydration, cramping. Six concrete outcomes, each one verifiable. Not vague "I feel better" — it's "HRV is higher" and "cramping during training eliminated."
- **Long-form on-screen caption does the selling** — the video is just a loop of product pouring. All persuasion happens through reading. Works perfectly with sound off.
- **"Consistent" as the close** — "The easy sachets have helped me stay more consistent than ever" reframes the product as a consistency tool, not a miracle cure. Underpromises, which increases trust.

**What the metrics tell us:** Lowest hook score (43) but highest watch score (66) in the top 8. The simple looping video doesn't stop scrollers as effectively, but the ones who do stop read the full caption and are highly engaged. The 10-month realistic timeframe builds credibility — people who engage are more convinced. The gap between hook and watch suggests this format could be improved by adding a more attention-grabbing visual hook (split screen, CGI, text flash) while keeping the long-caption testimonial body.

### Patterns Across Plateful References — What IM8 Should Clone

**1. Structural formula (replicate this arc):**
Hook (0-3s, pattern interrupt or curiosity gap) → Problem/Agitation (3-15s, name the enemy) → Solution + Mechanism (15-25s, explain WHY it works) → Proof/Benefit Stack (25-45s, rapid-fire demonstrations) → CTA + Urgency (final 5-10s, risk reversal + scarcity + command).

**2. Shot length: 1.3-2.0 seconds.** This is significantly faster than current IM8 ads. No deceleration at any point — the pace starts fast and maintains or accelerates. AppLovin audiences scroll at the first lull.

**3. Hard cuts dominate.** No fancy transitions. Split screens are the only visual technique beyond straight cuts — used for before/after, good-vs-bad, and showing multiple benefits simultaneously.

**4. Numbered frameworks.** "Better for three reasons" gives viewers a mental map. Makes ads feel educational rather than salesy. IM8 could use "3 things that happen when you drink IM8" or "3 reasons doctors are switching to IM8."

**5. Color-coded problem vs. solution.** Red overlays, B&W, and desaturation for the "before" state. Warm, bright, saturated for the product. Visual contrast is systematic across all ads.

**6. Hooks never start with the presenter talking to camera.** They start with motion, visual interest, or text that creates a reason to stop scrolling: 3D anatomy animation, action shots, urgency text, or a shocking statement.

**7. Full synced captions with visual emphasis on key words.** Color-coded (green = benefit, red = problem) or background boxes. Non-negotiable for AppLovin where many viewers watch without sound.

**8. Multi-layered CTA stack:** Emotional benefit recap → product name → risk reversal → urgency/scarcity → direct command. The most effective closes switch the value framing (percentage → dollar value) at the decision moment.

**9. UGC + polished B-roll intercut.** Presenter grounds the ad in authenticity; product B-roll elevates quality. Neither dominates — the rapid intercut between the two is what works.

#### Patterns — What IM8 Should Replicate for End Cards

**1. Pulsating CTA is non-negotiable.** 4/5 top AppLovin end cards use continuous pulsating animation on the CTA button. It's the only persistent motion after the build sequence. It keeps the eye on the action without distracting.

**2. Sequential element build under 2 seconds.** Elements cascade in with purpose: headline → product → value props (staggered) → social proof → CTA. Never all at once.

**3. Exactly 3 value props in pill/badge format.** Every end card uses 3 scannable, pill-style value props. IM8 should pick its strongest 3 (e.g., "NSF Certified," "92 Nutrients," "Money-Back Guarantee").

**4. Social proof as a distinct visual block.** Star ratings + review count, or a shipped/sold number. Animated star fill is a nice touch.

**5. One hero product image, clean background.** Simple gradient or solid color. Let product and copy do the work. No photographic backgrounds.

**6. CTA color is unique on the page.** The one color not used anywhere else — creates instant visual separation. Always bottom-center or full-width.

**7. Overshoot/ease animations.** Elements settle with slight bounce — gives a satisfying sense of weight and polish. No linear/mechanical motion.

**8. The "static ad that moves" principle.** If you pause the end card at any point after the build, it should function perfectly as a static ad. The motion adds polish and guides attention but the information architecture must stand alone.

**9. Full-width CTA button at the bottom.** Standard mobile convention. Maximizes tap target. Bold, all-caps, short text: "Shop Now," "TRY TODAY," "START FREE TRIAL."

**10. Disclaimer/sub-copy below CTA in smallest text.** "Free shipping" or guarantee language goes here — reassurance after the CTA, not competing with it.
