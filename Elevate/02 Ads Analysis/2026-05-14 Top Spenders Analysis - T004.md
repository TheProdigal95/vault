## What Changed Since T001/T002 Analysis

### 1. Our T001 VoC Frosted Glass is now the #1 spender in the account.

$10,873 spend, 20 purchases, $544 CPP, 1.26 ROAS. This is our creative — the premium frosted glass with VoC headline + payment plan logos. It's outspending everything else by 38%. The T001 test log noted "frosted glass static got to #1 spot L14" — it's held that position and scaled.

**What this validates:** VoC + payment plans + premium design = the account's conversion engine. First-person experience quotes on premium frosted glass with Affirm/Afterpay/Klarna badges.

### 2. Our Insurance Testimonial UGC is the only video getting meaningful spend.

$5,721 / 4 purchases / $1,430 CPP / 0.41 ROAS. This is almost certainly our T002 Script #9 (Insurance Hook Talking Head) that got produced and launched. Dated 05.06 — lines up with T002 going to production.

96% first-frame retention and 19% thumbstop — the insurance hook grabs attention. But $1,430 CPP means it's not converting efficiently. The talking head format + insurance hook = strong attention, needs conversion optimization (likely more prominent payment plan messaging in the close, or shorter path to CTA).

**What this validates:** Insurance hook works for video attention. UGC talking head format gets spend. But the gap between attention and purchase is still wide for video in this account.

### 3. Kids/Longevity is a dead angle — kill it.

Two creatives (#7 and #12), $4,697 combined spend, **ZERO purchases**. The "being around for my kids" and "longevity" framing doesn't convert in this account. People buying medical weight loss from Elevate are motivated by personal pain (food noise, body shame, insurance frustration, cost), not by family/longevity aspirations.

**Implication for T004:** Any persona whose primary angle is "I need to be healthy for my kids" (David Thompson's #3 escalation beat, "worrying about his heart, his kids") should not lead with that. Kids can be a supporting beat in the transformation payoff, never the hook or primary angle.

### 4. Summer Bod statics show early positive signal.

Three variants launched (V1/V2/V3). V1: 1.07 ROAS. V2: 1.10 ROAS, $534 CPP. V3: no purchases yet. Low spend across all three ($2,178 combined), but the ROAS direction is positive — both converting variants are above 1.0.

**What this could mean:** Seasonal/summer framing resonates. Beach, vacation, "summer body" pain points may be working. This is the closest data point to a "travel" angle in the performance data.

### 5. Education/mechanism videos don't work.

Leptin Resistance Voiceover V4 (61s+ education video): $519, 0 purchases. Aspirational TikTok: $978, 0 purchases. POV Before/After: $1,235, 0 purchases. The audience doesn't want education or aspiration — they want access and affordability.

### 6. The eligibility angle without payment logos remains an attention trap.

Unaware Eligible V5 + V6: $7,776 combined, 3 purchases, ~$2,600 avg CPP. Same pattern as T001/T002: eligibility grabs attention, payment logos close the sale. Neither without the other.

### Dead angles (don't touch)

### What this means for T004 personas

**Nurses:** No direct "nurse" data point in the top spenders, but the insurance testimonial UGC (#3) proves that first-person complaint about insurance → discovery → quiz works in video. The nurse character adds specificity to that pattern ("12-hour shift," "scrubs," "no time") without relying on a dead angle. The postpartum / body disconnect pain cluster is adjacent to what "Real People Real Results" (#2) is selling. **Supported by inference.**

**Travel:** The summer bod statics (#15, #17) are the closest data point — seasonal/body-confidence angle with early positive ROAS. Travel pain points (seatbelt, beach avoidance, vacation photos) are a specific version of this summer/body-confidence cluster. **Supported by adjacent signal (summer bod ROAS).**

**Kids / Longevity:** **DEAD.** Do not build a persona around "being around for my kids" or "longevity." The data killed this angle twice.

**Food noise / Willpower reframe:** Validated across multiple data points (T001 test log, T002 top spender #11 caption arc, VoC Frosted Glass VoC language). This is Maria Garcia's entry point. **Supported by data.**

**Insurance frustration:** The #3 spender in the current dataset. Validated as a video attention hook. Present in nurse persona (coworker's advice), travel persona (can afford $58/week but not med spa). **Supported by data.**
