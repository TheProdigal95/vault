## Deep Strategic Analysis

### 1. The "Text Wall" Phenomenon (`vid_10`, `static_v3`)
Why is the "Text Wall" format dominating spend?
- **High-Intent Filter:** Unlike polished creative, a wall of text *repels* casual scrollers and *attracts* high-intent sufferers. If you are truly struggling with chronic insomnia, this isn't a wall of text—it's a lifeline.
- **Native Disguise:** The simple, almost "ugly" native font and lack of professional polish (3am bedroom photo) lowers the "ad guard." It feels like a "truth bomb" from a friend or a Reddit post, not a sales pitch.
- **Lock and Key Mechanism:** 
    - **The Lock:** The first half of the copy uses hyper-specific **VoC (Voice of Customer)** to perfectly describe the user's pain (the "lock" they feel trapped in). 
    - **The Key:** Just as the user feels "seen," the ad introduces the "key"—**Harvard-developed CBT-I**. The authority provides the "permission" to believe a solution actually exists.

### 2. The Harvard CTR Driver (`static_harvard`)
Why did the Harvard hero card hit a massive 5.3% CTR?
- **Cognitive Shortcut:** "Harvard" is a global symbol for prestige and truth. It bypasses the user's skepticism instantly. A claim like "80% improve sleep" sounds like marketing coming from a brand; it sounds like *fact* coming from Harvard.
- **The Curiosity Gap:** The "Harvard" branding makes the viewer stop, but the *mystery* of the protocol drives the click. It sells **access to a valuable secret** (the clinical protocol used by elite institutions).
- **Split-Screen Efficiency:** Parallel processing of an emotional hit (the aspirational result) on one side and a logical hook (the authority/stat) on the other.

### 3. Visual Pacing for Scroll-Stop
- **Mirror Image vs. Aspirational:** Top performers don't lead with "look how good you could sleep." They lead with a **mirror image** of the user's current misery (rumpled bed, 3:47 AM clock).
- **Immediate Validation:** The first 3 seconds of the text walls don't sell the app; they validate the user's struggle. This creates an immediate bond of trust.
