## Raw Notes — Batch Strategy

Full analysis: [[2026-05-14 Top Spenders Analysis - T004]]

> **Core archetype for every script:** "The woman who was skeptical, couldn't afford it, almost didn't try, and now wants to tell everyone." Every converting creative in Elevate's account carries this arc: skepticism → access barrier (insurance/cost) → almost didn't try → took the quiz → wants to tell everyone. Character voices are costume changes on this archetype — they determine WHO stops scrolling, but the psychological journey is fixed.
>
> T004 tests new character voices distinct from T003 (Sarah Jenkins / David Thompson / Maria Garcia). T002 proved video structures convert (talking head, selfie walk, long-form). T003 proved first-person storytelling from persona pages drives engagement. This batch merges both: 15 video scripts across 4 character voices, whitelisted on persona pages.

**What the T004 top spenders data says:**
- **Our T001 VoC Frosted Glass is the #1 spender in the account** ($10,873 / 20 purchases / 1.26 ROAS). VoC + payment plans + premium design is the conversion engine.
- **Our Insurance Testimonial UGC (T002 Script #9) is the only video getting real spend** ($5,721 / 4 purchases / 96% first-frame retention). Insurance hook grabs attention in video, needs conversion optimization.
- **Kids/Longevity is DEAD.** $4,697 across 2 creatives, 0 purchases. Don't lead with "being around for my kids."
- **Summer Bod statics show early positive ROAS** (1.07 and 1.10). Seasonal/body-confidence framing has legs.
- **Education/mechanism videos don't work.** Leptin Resistance 61s+ video: $519, 0 purchases. Don't educate — sell access.

**From T003 (carry forward):**
- **Whitelisting:** First-person "I" voice from persona pages
- **Character consistency:** Each character voice has locked avatar + voice + background settings
- **Native feel:** AI avatars should read as real people talking to camera, not polished ads
- **Story arcs:** 10-beat structures adapted for video pacing

**Production model — AI-generated UGC:**
- **No creator casting or shooting.** All videos produced with AI avatars (talking heads) or AI voiceover + B-roll.
- **Three structures:** Talking Head (avatar talks to camera, single background), Green Screen (avatar composited over B-roll/imagery), VO + B-Roll (no avatar, AI voice over footage/imagery).
- **Green Screen is untested.** No performance data yet — testing on 5 concepts this batch to build the data set.

**From T002 video learnings:**
- **Eligibility + payment plans = conversion.** Every script carries eligibility angle or quiz CTA + $58/week + $0 down.
- **Self-disqualification reversal** is the strongest hook: "I almost didn't take the quiz."
- **Diversify pain points.** T002 used food noise in 5/7 scripts — this batch spreads across med spa waste, gym futility, late-night impulse, and summer shame.
- **Long-form scripts need syllable budgeting.** Budget syllables per beat in outline phase before writing full VO. 300 spm hard limit, 250 spm body target.
- **Price-forward concepts need caption-VO angle separation.** When the concept IS the price, captions enter from a different angle while VO delivers the number.

**Data-backed angle guardrails:**
- ✅ Insurance frustration (validated — #3 spender in video)
- ✅ Food noise / willpower reframe (validated — VoC language in #1 spender)
- ✅ Payment plan as conversion lever (validated — every top converter carries it)
- ✅ Price comparison / cost reframe (validated — #1 spender uses payment plan anchoring)
- ✅ Summer/body confidence (early positive — Summer Bod 1.07-1.10 ROAS)
- ✅ Skeptic-to-convert arc (validated — core of every converting creative)
- ❌ Kids/Longevity as primary hook (dead — $4.7k, 0 purchases)
- ❌ Education/mechanism (dead — $519, 0 purchases)
- ❌ Aspirational/POV dancing (dead — $2.2k, 0 purchases)

## Character Profiles (AI Avatar & Voice Direction)

### The Med Spa Quitter — Late 30s

- **Avatar:** Late 30s woman. Polished but not glamorous — highlights, light makeup, the kind of put-together that costs money. Not a model. Relatable-attractive.
- **Talking Head background:** Clean, well-lit apartment — kitchen island or living room couch. Warm natural light. Comfortable, not aspirational-rich.
- **Green Screen backgrounds:** Med spa waiting room, bathroom vanity with skincare products, car interior (errand energy).
- **Voice/delivery:** Confident, slightly exasperated. Eye-roll energy when talking about past spending. Shifts to genuine relief. Think "telling her best friend over wine."
- **Wardrobe (bust-up):** Casual blouse or oversized sweater. Hair done but not "done up." Visible jewelry — small hoops or chain.

### The Plateau Gym Girl — Early 30s

- **Avatar:** Early 30s woman. Clearly fit lifestyle — not thin, not heavy, just stuck. Minimal makeup, hair in a claw clip or ponytail. Looks like she just finished a workout.
- **Talking Head background:** Apartment living room or home gym corner. Post-workout energy — slightly flushed, water bottle visible. Real and unpolished.
- **Green Screen backgrounds:** Gym interior (real gym, not luxury), kitchen with meal prep containers, scale/bathroom.
- **Voice/delivery:** Frustrated but controlled. The "I've earned the right to be annoyed" tone. Shifts to calm vindication, not excitement. Think "finally being proven right."
- **Wardrobe (bust-up):** Sports bra strap visible under oversized gym tee, or athletic tank. Apple Watch. No glam.

### The Late-Night Scroller — 40s

- **Avatar:** 40s woman. Tired but sharp — has opinions. Natural hair, no styling. Reading glasses on or pushed up on head. The "I've seen it all" face.
- **Talking Head background:** Dark bedroom with warm lamp glow (night version) or couch with morning coffee (day version). Intimate framing — whisper-close, like she's telling a secret.
- **Green Screen backgrounds:** Phone screen glow in dark room, Trustpilot review page, 2 AM clock.
- **Voice/delivery:** Dry, skeptical, almost conspiratorial. Like she can't believe she's recommending something. Shifts from eye-roll to quiet conviction. Think "the friend who never recommends anything finally recommending something."
- **Wardrobe (bust-up):** Oversized sleep tee or robe. Messy bun. Morning version: joggers and coffee mug in hand. Off-duty, guard-down energy.

### The Summer Comeback — Late 20s

- **Avatar:** Late 20s woman. Warm, social, expressive face. The friend who used to say yes to everything. Not model-pretty — girl-next-door pretty.
- **Talking Head background:** Bright bedroom or living room with summer light. Trip photos on wall or fridge visible. Warm tones — golden hour feel.
- **Green Screen backgrounds:** Beach scene, group text screenshot, closet with swimsuit, pool party from a distance.
- **Voice/delivery:** Vulnerable but not fragile. Starts avoidant/sad, builds to genuine joy. Think "the friend who finally showed up to the beach trip and can't stop smiling."
- **Wardrobe (bust-up):** Sundress or casual summer top. Bright colors. Hair down. The visual read is "summer energy."

## Core Story Beats (Internal Reference)

> All 4 character voices run the same core archetype: skepticism → access barrier → almost didn't try → took the quiz → wants to tell everyone. The beats below give each voice its specific costume.

### The Med Spa Quitter (5 Scripts)

1. **Hook:** Monthly med spa receipt or bank statement — $400, $450, $480. "I was spending more on weight loss than my car payment."
2. **Incident:** Sitting in the med spa waiting room, doing the math on the last 6 months. Realizing she's spent $3,000+ and is exactly where she started.
3. **Escalation:** The "I should just accept my body" phase that isn't acceptance — it's defeat after spending money she didn't have.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Med spa injections, boutique programs, nutritionist at $200/session, Noom subscription, "hormone balancing" supplements.
5. **Discovery:** A friend posts about taking a 2-minute quiz. She almost scrolls past it. But the price catches her — "$58/week? I spend more on my med spa parking."
6. **Mechanism:** 2-minute online quiz → provider review → personalized care plan → ships to her door. No waiting rooms. No appointments.
7. **First Results:** The food noise stops. She's not thinking about dinner at 10 AM anymore. She cancels the med spa.
8. **Transformation:** Same woman, new math. $58/week vs $400+/month. Actually feeling different, not just paying to feel different.
9. **Price Anchoring:** $58/week with Affirm/Afterpay. $0 down. "I was already spending 3x this on things that didn't work."
10. **Close:** "I almost didn't take the quiz because I thought it was too cheap to be real. See if you qualify."

### The Plateau Gym Girl (4 Scripts)

1. **Hook:** Gym selfie or workout tracker — "875 workouts logged. Same weight."
2. **Incident:** Standing on the scale after 6 months of discipline — meal prep, 5 AM alarms, no cheat days — and seeing the exact same number.
3. **Escalation:** People telling her "you just need to be more consistent." She IS consistent. Her body just doesn't care.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Macro tracking (obsessively), personal trainer, cutting carbs, adding cardio, protein shakes, "just eat less."
5. **Discovery:** A gym friend mentions Elevate quietly in the locker room. "It's not a shortcut — it's the thing your willpower can't fix."
6. **Mechanism:** Quick online quiz → medical team reviews her profile → treatment ships discreetly. No gym-bro advice. Actual medicine.
7. **First Results:** The background hunger shuts off. She still works out, still eats well — but now it actually works.
8. **Transformation:** Same gym, same routine. Different results. The scale finally moves. Not because she tried harder — because her biology stopped fighting her.
9. **Price Anchoring:** $58/week. "I was spending more on supplements that did nothing."
10. **Close:** "It wasn't willpower. It was biology. See if you qualify."

### The Late-Night Scroller (3 Scripts)

1. **Hook:** Phone screen at 2:17 AM. "I found Elevate the way I find everything — doom-scrolling at midnight when I should be sleeping."
2. **Incident:** Seeing the ad, rolling her eyes, almost scrolling past. "I've been burned by every 'program' on the internet."
3. **Escalation:** But she can't stop thinking about it. Opens Trustpilot. Reads reviews for an hour. The real stories sound like her.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Every subscription, every app, every "doctor-approved" program that turned out to be a glorified meal plan.
5. **Discovery:** Takes the quiz at 12:47 AM because "at least it's free and no one's watching."
6. **Mechanism:** Quiz → provider reviews it → real medication, not supplements → ships to her door. She checks the reviews three more times before she believes it.
7. **First Results:** Two weeks in. The 9 PM snack run doesn't happen. She notices because she's still awake at midnight — but she's not eating.
8. **Transformation:** The woman who trusted nothing is telling everyone. Group chats, coworkers, her sister.
9. **Price Anchoring:** $58/week. $0 down. "I've wasted more on programs I quit after a week."
10. **Close:** "I almost didn't take the quiz. I'm so glad 2 AM me did. See if you qualify."

### The Summer Comeback (3 Scripts)

1. **Hook:** Group text: "Beach trip Memorial Day weekend??" Her stomach drops. "I used to be the first one to say yes."
2. **Incident:** The swimsuit in the back of the closet, tags still on from two summers ago. Bought it, never wore it.
3. **Escalation:** Three years of "maybe next summer." Declining pool parties. Cropping herself out of vacation photos. Wearing the coverup the entire trip.
4. **Failed Alternatives:** Pre-summer crash diets. "Beach body in 30 days" programs. Skipping meals before the trip then bingeing on vacation.
5. **Discovery:** A friend who went on the same beach trip last year looks different — not skinny, just… free. She asks. "I took a 2-minute quiz."
6. **Mechanism:** Online quiz → provider match → treatment arrives before the trip. No gym ultimatum. No starvation countdown.
7. **First Results:** Packing for the trip and reaching for the swimsuit instead of the coverup. Not thinking about angles.
8. **Transformation:** In the group photo. Not hiding. Not sucking in. Just… there. "I stopped avoiding my life."
9. **Price Anchoring:** $58/week. Less than two summer cocktails. $0 down with Afterpay.
10. **Close:** "Summer's coming. Don't sit this one out. See if you qualify."

## Concept Validation

> Runs after the Batch Plan is reviewed. Each concept passes the 7 tests individually; the differentiation rule is a batch-level cross-check.

### 7-Test Quality Bar (per concept)

1. **Pain point specificity** — built around a specific human tension, not a product feature.
2. **Strategic coherence** — audience, message, structure, and hook work together.
3. **Differentiation** — couldn't be used by a competitor without modification.
4. **Format ambition** — visual format chosen intentionally, not by default.
5. **Persuasive sharpness** — the hook creates felt tension.
6. **Testable hypothesis** — grounded in behavioral or pattern insights.
7. **Producible** — editors and creators can execute without major clarification.

### Differentiation Rule (batch-level cross-check)

Each concept shifts at least 2 of these 4 axes from every other concept:
- Intended audience (character voice)
- Messaging angle
- Visual format / structure
- Hook tactic

## Script Criteria

### COMPLIANCE (Read First)

**Before writing anything for Elevate, read [[Compliance - Marketing Rules]].** Every script, headline, caption, and VO line must pass the compliance checklist. Compliance rules override every other criteria doc. Non-compliance is an automatic fail.

Summary of the hardest constraints:
- **No "GLP-1" as a product name.** Not in VO, captions, or close cards. Can appear in biological/educational context only.
- **No brand names** (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza) anywhere except the mandatory brand-ownership disclaimer.
- **No "same active ingredient as [brand]"** — FDA warned companies for this exact phrasing.
- **No specific weight numbers, percentages, or timeframes** as results claims.
- **No drug imagery.** No vials, syringes, pens, medication packaging in any Visual column.
- **No "safe," "effective," "well-tolerated," "zero side effects," "clinically proven."**
- **Pricing decoupled from drug.** "Programs starting at $58/week" — never "GLP-1 for $58/week."
- **CTAs lead to medical review.** "See if you qualify" / "Take the quiz" / "Check eligibility."
- **Testimonials describe experience, not results.** "I finally felt supported" ✓. "I lost 50 lbs" ✗.

### Elevate-Specific Rules

- **First-party source of truth.** Every factual claim must be verified against [[Product Context - GLP-1 Programs]]. Never copy facts from existing ads.
- **Price framing.** "Programs from $58/week" is the default. Never tie pricing directly to a drug.
- **Shipping framing.** "Ships in 7-10 days after approval." Do NOT use "3-7 days."
- **Payment plan logos are a design element.** Affirm, Afterpay, Klarna, HSA/FSA — prominently featured in close cards and overlays, not buried.
- **VoC quotes must come from Elevate Trustpilot reviews or research sprints.** Experience only, not results. Don't invent quotes.
- **Tonal range:** Empathetic, grounded, validating. Audience skews female. Provocative OK. Absurdist humor is not.
- **Compliance is a rulebook, not a vibe check.** If a line doesn't break a specific rule in [[Compliance - Marketing Rules]], keep its edge. Don't pre-emptively soften punchy lines.

### Whitelisting Rules (from T003)

- **Always use First Person ("I", "my")** — these run from persona pages, not the brand account.
- **Discovery:** Through a friend, coworker, or family member who mentioned it — never "I was searching online."
- **Payment plans mentioned in every script.** $58/week, $0 down, Affirm/Klarna/Afterpay. Beat 9/10 minimum.
- **Experience language only.** "The food noise stopped," "I feel like me again," "I have energy." Never pounds, percentages, or timeframes.
- **Character consistency:** Each character voice has locked avatar, voice, and background settings. AI avatar must match the defined profile for all scripts within a group.

### Video-Specific Rules (from T002 learnings)

- **Caption-VO angle separation.** Captions and VO use different hook types per [[Headline & Text Hook Criteria]]. When the concept is price-forward, captions enter from a different angle (disbelief, social proof, question) while VO delivers the number.
- **Syllable budgeting for long-form.** Long-form scripts (#4, #9, #11) must have syllable counts per beat verified against timestamps before writing full VO. 300 spm hard limit, 250 spm body target.
- **Close caption stacks proof points** (patient count, bloodwork, payment plans) while VO delivers the emotional beat. Two channels, two messages.
- **Food noise spread.** Food noise may appear as a supporting beat in multiple scripts but as the PRIMARY hook/angle in no more than 2 scripts across the batch. T002 overindexed on this — diversify.
