## Core Technology: How Vibration Waking Works

### The Mechanism
Traditional audio alarms rely on sound to trigger wakefulness. The problem: the brain can habituate to audio signals over time, especially in heavy sleepers. Repeated exposure to the same alarm sound reduces its effectiveness as the brain learns to incorporate or dismiss the stimulus during sleep.

Vibration-based waking delivers a **distinct neural signal** — a tactile/somatosensory stimulus rather than an auditory one. Physical vibration on the wrist or ankle activates a different sensory pathway, one that is harder for the brain to habituate to during sleep. This is why vibration alarms are used in clinical and accessibility contexts (e.g., for deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals).

### Standard: Fixed Vibration
The Standard model delivers a single, strong vibration intensity. It activates at the set alarm time and vibrates continuously at full strength for up to 2 minutes, then stops automatically. There is no escalation and no manual intensity adjustment.

### Pro: Escalating Vibration
The Pro model uses an **escalating vibration pattern** — it starts at low intensity and automatically increases to high intensity over the 2-minute window. This serves two purposes:
1. **Gentler wake-up** — lighter sleepers wake during the low-intensity phase without being jolted
2. **Heavy sleeper guarantee** — the vibration reaches maximum intensity if the initial levels don't work

### The 2-Minute Window
Both models vibrate for exactly 2 minutes per alarm, then stop. This is a fixed behavior — it cannot be extended or shortened. If the wearer does not wake within 2 minutes, the device stops vibrating and no further alerts are given until the next scheduled alarm.

### Wrist vs. Ankle Placement
The device can be worn on either the wrist or ankle. Ankle placement is sometimes recommended for extremely heavy sleepers, as the ankle may be more sensitive to vibration during deep sleep (less habituation from daily wrist activity).

## Known Issues & Limitations

### 1. Charger Not Included
The USB wall adapter is NOT included with the device. Only a USB charging cable is included. The wall adapter costs $8.80 (sale) / $20 (regular). This is a common source of customer surprise and frustration — buyers expect a complete charging solution in the box. Most households have spare USB adapters, but the expectation mismatch is a friction point.

**Impact on creative:** Do not emphasize "everything you need in one box." Be transparent about what's included or avoid the topic. If selling bundles, including the adapter removes this objection.

### 2. Standard Version Lacks Escalating Vibration
Many buyers don't fully understand the difference between Standard and Pro before purchasing. The Standard has a fixed, strong vibration — it does not escalate. For heavy sleepers, this may be less effective than the Pro's escalating pattern. The distinction is often unclear in marketing materials until you read the fine print.

**Impact on creative:** If targeting heavy sleepers, always push the Pro. Be explicit about the Standard's fixed vibration to set correct expectations and reduce returns.

### 3. No Waterproofing by Default
Neither version is waterproof out of the box. Waterproofing requires a separate $7.95 upgrade. For a wearable device that's worn during sleep (and potentially in the shower before/after), this is a notable gap.

**Impact on creative:** Do not imply water resistance. If selling bundles, the waterproof upgrade is a natural add-on.

### 4. No Manual Vibration Intensity Adjustment
Users cannot manually set the vibration intensity on either model. The Standard is fixed at strong. The Pro escalates automatically from low to high. There is no app, no settings menu, and no way to customize the vibration pattern or strength.

**Impact on creative:** Don't promise "customizable" vibration. The Pro's escalation is automatic, not user-controlled.

### 5. The 2-Minute Window
Both models vibrate for exactly 2 minutes, then stop. If the user doesn't wake up in 2 minutes, there is no fallback — no repeated alarm, no snooze, no escalation beyond the 2-minute cycle. The next alarm will only fire at its scheduled time.

**Impact on creative:** The "99% wake rate" claim mitigates this concern, but it's a potential anxiety point for extremely heavy sleepers. With up to 3 (Standard) or 5 (Pro) alarms, users can set staggered alarms as a workaround.

### 6. No Connectivity
No Bluetooth, no WiFi, no app. This is a feature (standalone simplicity, no phone dependency) and a limitation (no firmware updates, no data tracking, no remote configuration). Users who want sleep tracking, smart home integration, or app-based control will be disappointed.
