# Persuasion & Copy

Scores whether the body and close persuade effectively: VO sounds spoken, heuristics deployed correctly, mechanism clear, objections handled, no redundancy, product named.

---

## What This Scores

The body is where the sale happens. A great hook with a weak body is a video people start but don't finish. This dimension evaluates: does the VO sound like a real person talking (not copy)? Is the mechanism clearly explained? Are psychological heuristics deployed correctly? Does the body deliver on the hook's promise without restating it? Is the product actually named? Are objections handled? Does the scaffold vary or repeat the same structure across beats?

## Calibration Table

| Score Range | Description |
|---|---|
| 90-100 | VO reads as natural speech throughout — read any line aloud and it sounds like something a person would say. 2-3 heuristics deployed correctly and named. Mechanism clearly explained in body. Product named in body (not just close). Body delivers on hook's promise through a different door (no redundancy). Scaffold varies across list beats. At least 2 failed alternatives named. Objections handled. No banned constructions. |
| 70-89 | VO mostly sounds spoken but 1-2 lines feel "written" (noun-phrase stacking, tagline cadence). OR: heuristics present but 1 is misidentified. OR: body partially restates the hook. OR: scaffold repeats the same sentence structure across 3+ list beats. OR: product named only in close. |
| 50-69 | VO sounds like marketing copy in multiple sections. No heuristics identifiable. Body restates the hook's argument. Mechanism is vague ("it works differently"). Scaffold is identical across all list beats (same "The problem with X is Y" template repeated). |
| Below 50 | VO reads as AI-generated copy. No mechanism explanation. Body is a feature list with no persuasion arc. No objection handling. Product never named. Multiple banned constructions. |

## Instant-Fail Triggers

- **Any banned construction from Universal Copy Rules.** Em dashes, false dichotomies, "It's not X. It's not Y." lists, fourth-wall breaks, attribution layers, noun-phrase fragment stacking without subject+verb bridging. Any one of these auto-caps at 69.
- **Temporal discounting misapplied.** If the script claims to use temporal discounting but references milestone timelines (Day 1/7/30), "this is what 30 days looks like," or price discounts — the heuristic is wrong. Temporal discounting = immediate results ("from your first sip," "within 24 hours," "the very first night"). Flag and deduct.
- **More than 1 analogy.** Universal Copy Rules: max 1 analogy per script.

## Scoring Checklist

### Voice Quality
- Read every VO line aloud. Does it sound like something a person would say, or does it sound like copy?
- No noun-phrase fragment stacking (PowerPoint bullet-list cadence in VO).
- No tagline-style closes or staccato data-dump closes.
- No writerly metaphors ("like someone turned down the brightness").
- No fourth-wall breaks ("That's not a sales pitch," "I know this sounds like an ad").
- No attribution layers ("the women say," "people are saying").

### Heuristic Deployment
- Are 2-3 heuristics identifiable in the script? Name each one and where it fires.
- Are they correctly identified per the Heuristic Reference? Check especially:
  - Temporal discounting = "from your first sip," "within 24 hours" — NOT timelines, NOT price discounts
  - Framing effect = price reframed into smaller units — NOT just mentioning a price
  - Affect heuristic = dropping INTO an experience/feeling — NOT describing a feature
  - Authority bias = expert quote OR authoritative language — NOT just mentioning "doctors"
- Do the heuristics fire early enough? (First 10 seconds for at least 1.)

### Persuasion Arc
- Does the body deliver on the hook's promise? (If hook says "3 things your doctor never tested," body must name 3 things.)
- Is the mechanism clearly explained? (Not vague "it works differently" — specific how.)
- Are at least 2 failed alternatives named and specific?
- Are objections handled? (Price, skepticism, "does it really work?")
- Is the product named in the body, not just the close?

### Redundancy (four forms per Video Script Criteria)
- No hook-body content overlap (whole hook against whole body).
- No within-body scaffold repetition (same sentence template 3+ times in list beats).
- No within-script phrase echo (same mechanism/bridge/benefit phrase repeated).
- No hook previewing body beats sequentially.

### CTA and Close
- CTA matches the assigned close type.
- Close doesn't introduce new information the body should have covered.
- No staccato data-dump close (listing 5+ claims in rapid succession).

## Feedback Format

When scoring below 90:
- The specific VO line, body beat, or close section that fails
- What rule it violates (with reference to the criteria doc)
- A concrete rewrite — not "make it sound more natural" but "rewrite body beat 3 VO from '[current text]' to '[specific rewrite that sounds spoken and addresses the issue]'"
- If heuristic deployment failed: name the heuristic, the correct definition, where it should fire, and what the copy should say
