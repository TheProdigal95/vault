## Customer Pain Points Addressed

1. **"I've tried everything and nothing works."** Melatonin, sleep hygiene, white noise, pills, meditation — all symptom-level. CBT-I targets the psychological and behavioral root cause. This is the brand's core existential claim.
2. **"I can't get an appointment with a sleep therapist."** CBT-I therapist waitlists run 6+ months. Stellar Sleep delivers the same clinical protocol in 10 minutes/day on their phone.
3. **"My doctor just wants to give me pills."** Sleeping pills treat the symptom, create dependency, and don't address the conditioned thought patterns sustaining insomnia. Stellar Sleep is the evidence-based alternative.
4. **"I'm scared I'll never sleep again."** The anxious thought spiral ("What if I can't sleep?") is itself a driver of insomnia. The program directly addresses catastrophic sleep thoughts through cognitive restructuring.
5. **"My bedroom has become a place I dread."** Conditioned arousal — where the bed/bedroom triggers anxiety instead of sleepiness — is a core mechanism Stellar Sleep's program reverses.
6. **"I'm exhausted all day but wired at night."** Hyperarousal from chronic insomnia creates a cruel paradox. Sleep Restriction Therapy and the CBT-I protocol directly address this cycle.
7. **"Calm/Headspace didn't help my insomnia."** Generic meditation manages anxiety but doesn't retrain sleep drive and sleep architecture. Stellar Sleep is built specifically for chronic insomnia, not general wellness.
8. **"I'm dependent on sleep medication and want off."** The program provides a credible, structured path off sleeping pills — something pills themselves can't offer.

## The Insomnia Cycle Framework

Stellar Sleep's core educational model — used in product onboarding, content marketing, and ad creative. Understanding this framework is essential for all creative work.

**The 4-Stage Insomnia Cycle:**

### Stage 1: Trigger
A stress event disrupts sleep. Could be anything: new job, relationship breakdown, health scare, new baby, grief, a single bad night that spirals. Sleep was fine before. Now it isn't.

### Stage 2: Counterproductive Fixes
The person tries to recover sleep using intuitive (but wrong) strategies:
- Going to bed earlier to "catch up"
- Napping during the day
- Spending more time in bed hoping to sleep more

These backfire. The longer a person lies awake in bed, the more the brain learns to associate the bed with wakefulness. Quote from their content: "The longer you stay in bed awake, the more your brain starts to expect that outcome."

### Stage 3: Anxious Thought Patterns
Racing thoughts begin to dominate bedtime. The mind starts catastrophizing:
- "What if I can't sleep again tonight?"
- "What if I never sleep well again?"
- "I need to be sharp tomorrow — I have to sleep."
- Performance anxiety about sleeping itself becomes the obstacle to sleeping.

### Stage 4: Conditioned Dread
The bedroom itself becomes a stress trigger. Bedtime — which used to signal rest — now signals anxiety. The body's arousal system activates at the cue of nighttime. The brain has been conditioned to be alert in the one context it needs to be calm. The cycle becomes self-sustaining, independent of the original trigger.

**The exit:** CBT-I (and Stellar Sleep) breaks the cycle at every stage — Sleep Restriction Therapy rebuilds sleep drive (Stage 2), Cognitive Restructuring interrupts catastrophic thinking (Stage 3), stimulus control re-conditions the bedroom association (Stage 4).
