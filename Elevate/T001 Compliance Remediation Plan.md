# T001 Compliance Remediation Plan

Date: 2026-04-14
Trigger: New compliance documents received (Best Practices for Marketing, Compounded GLP-1 Marketing Guidelines, Creative Update Guide for Compounded GLP-1 Marketing).

**Purpose of this document:** Audit the existing T001 Working Document and Briefs against the new compliance rules, propose specific changes, and flag which concepts need full rework vs. wording adjustments vs. removal. This is a PLAN. No edits to the Working Document, Briefs, or Top Spenders Analysis until Marce approves the approach.

---

## 1. The New Compliance Rules (What Changed)

### Language that is now BANNED

**Drug-related terms:**
- "GLP-1" as a product name (e.g., "GLP-1 that works," "compounded GLP-1," "GLP-1+") — can still reference the biological concept or receptors in educational context, but NOT as a product being sold
- "GLP-1 that works"
- "Stronger / better / next-gen GLP-1"
- "Same as Wegovy / Ozempic / Mounjaro / Zepbound"
- "Same active ingredient as [brand]" — FDA warned Ozempen for this exact phrasing
- Any brand name (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza) — trademark infringement risk
- "Generic" [any GLP-1 brand] — there is no such thing as generic Wegovy, etc.

**Results/outcome claims:**
- "Lost X lbs" — any specific weight number
- Percentages ("15-20% body weight")
- Time-based promises ("94 lbs in under a year," "30 lbs in 6 months")
- "Guaranteed weight loss"
- "Clinically proven results"
- "Before & after" framing
- "Results you can see"
- "Safe" or "Effective" — legal terms reserved for FDA-approved drugs
- "Well-tolerated" — no studies have been performed on compounded semaglutide
- "Zero side effects" — implies well-tolerated

**Advertising (per Florida Board of Medicine Rule 64B8-11.001):**
- Cannot promise specific results
- Cannot raise unreasonable expectations
- Cannot claim rapid, dramatic, incredible, or safe weight loss
- Cannot state or suggest diets or exercise are not required
- Cannot suggest weight loss is effortless or magical

### Visuals that are now BANNED

- Drug vials, syringes, pens
- Medication packaging
- Before/after photos
- Side-by-side transformation imagery
- People pulling oversized jeans
- Any graphic that implies "old body → new body"

### Approved Creative Angles

- Eligibility & access
- Personalized care
- Provider-led evaluation
- Convenience & affordability
- Support & guidance
- Lifestyle improvement (non-quantified)

### Approved Copy Patterns

- "Medical weight loss, personalized for you"
- "Provider-led weight loss care"
- "A weight loss plan made for your body"
- "Prescription weight loss support, if eligible"
- "Medical care designed around you"
- "Some patients experience appetite changes"
- "Weight loss may occur"
- "Results vary by individual"
- "No two care plans are the same"

### Approved CTAs

- "See if you qualify"
- "Get started"
- "Begin your medical review"
- "Check eligibility"
- "Explore care options"
- CTAs lead to intake, not checkout

### Approved Pricing Language

- "Programs starting at $X/week"
- "Care plans available with payment options"
- "Affordable medical weight loss support"
- **Pricing must NEVER be tied directly to a drug.** "$54/week for GLP-1" is out. "Programs starting at $54/week" is in.

### Mandatory Disclosures (supportive only — do not fix non-compliant claims)

- "Compounded medications are not FDA-approved"
- "Medications prescribed only if clinically appropriate"
- "Results vary by individual"

### The Golden Rule (from the Creative Update Guide)

> "If the creative would still make sense without mentioning GLP-1 at all, it's probably compliant."

## 2. What We Can Still Use (Per Marce)

- **Voice of customer** — but experience-based, not results-based. "I finally felt supported" ✓. "I lost 34 lbs" ✗.
- **Food noise** — this is a symptom/experience, not a drug claim. Allowed.
- **Pain points** — seatbelt, chair with arms, shoe-tying stool, fitting room, food noise. All allowed.
- **Payment plans** — allowed, but decoupled from drug pricing.
- **"Medical weight loss"** — the phrase itself is allowed.

## 5. Working Document Changes Required

### Script Criteria (major updates)

**Currently says:**
- "Compounded medications disclaimer required on every ad." (exact text from current winners)
- "Never claim equivalence to Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound... Don't say 'same as Ozempic' or 'generic Ozempic.'"
- Allows "same active ingredient" framing
- "Price framing: weekly > monthly > total. $54/week is the default."

**Must change to:**
- **New disclaimer wording:** "Compounded drug products are not approved or evaluated for safety, effectiveness, or quality by the FDA." + the full Novo Nordisk / Eli Lilly brand disclaimer where the page discusses weight loss medications.
- **Stronger brand/equivalence rule:** "Never use brand names (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, Victoza) anywhere — not in copy, not in image, not even in 'disclaimer' context. Never use 'same active ingredient as [brand].' Never use 'generic [brand].' The only way to reference the FDA-approved drugs is in the required brand-ownership disclaimer."
- **NEW rule — no "GLP-1" as a product:** "Never use 'GLP-1,' 'compounded GLP-1,' or 'GLP-1 + [anything]' as a product name. The term can appear in educational/biological context (e.g., 'GLP-1 receptors' in a mechanism explanation) but not as a product being sold. Pricing and CTAs should never tie to 'GLP-1.'"
- **NEW rule — no quantified results:** "Never use specific weight loss numbers, percentages, or time-based promises in headlines, subheadings, primary text, or VoC quotes. This includes pounds lost, percentage of body weight, and timeframes ('in 6 months'). Use experience-based language instead."
- **NEW rule — no drug imagery:** "Never show drug vials, syringes, pens, medication packaging, or before/after photos. Lifestyle imagery only."
- **NEW rule — price decoupling:** "Price must never be tied directly to a drug. 'GLP-1 for $54/week' is out. 'Programs starting at $54/week' or 'Care plans starting at $54/week' is in."
- **NEW rule — no safety/efficacy claims:** "Never use 'safe,' 'effective,' 'well-tolerated,' 'clinically proven,' 'proven results,' 'zero side effects,' or any variation. These terms have FDA legal meaning reserved for approved drugs."
- **NEW rule — Florida Board of Medicine compliance:** "Never promise specific results, raise unreasonable expectations, claim rapid/dramatic/incredible weight loss, suggest diet/exercise isn't required, or imply weight loss is effortless or magical."
- **CTAs must lead to medical review:** "See if you qualify," "Begin your medical review," "Check eligibility," "Get started." Never "Order now," "Lose weight fast," "Get semaglutide."

## 7. What We Do NOT Lose (Preserving the Edge)

The compliance rules don't kill the batch. They narrow the language. The winning elements still work:

- **Food noise** stays. It's a lived experience, not a drug claim.
- **Pain point imagery** stays. Seatbelt, diet cycle, fitting room, kitchen counter — all fine.
- **VoC framing** stays. We just pivot from results VoC ("I lost 50 lbs") to experience VoC ("I finally felt supported," "The process was clear," "The plan felt personalized").
- **Payment plan messaging** stays. Just decoupled from "GLP-1 for $X/week" to "Programs from $X/week."
- **"No insurance" angle** stays. This is about access, not the drug itself.
- **Premium Frosted Glass, Grimy Poster, IG Stories Widget, VoC Editorial formats** all stay. They just lose drug vials and specific numbers.
- **Provider-led / personalized care** becomes the unlock. This is the new positioning: "medical care designed around you" vs. one-size-fits-all.
