### IM8 Visual Universe (End Card & Static Variety)

The brand's visual identity extends far beyond dark maroon + light cream flat backgrounds. End cards and statics should draw from the full palette to avoid compositional monotony. Source: IM8 website, brand campaign imagery, T003 REFs canvas.

- **Dark maroon (#4A1A22)** — the core brand dark. Works for authority, clinical, premium. But not every card.
- **Light cream (#F5F2F3)** — the core brand light. Clean, editorial. Pair with warm photography, not just flat.
- **Blue molecular** — glass bubble structures, water droplets, translucent blue. Scientific trust. Completely different temperature from the warm palette.
- **Terracotta/natural** — red tiles, overhead product shots, glass teapot, green leaves. Warm, lifestyle, editorial.

**Texture resources:**
- **Red organic macro** — mushroom gill photography, coral structures. Full-bleed backgrounds with text/portraits composited on top. The brand's most distinctive visual asset.
- **Cellular/microscopic** — golden cell structures on dark maroon. Scientific, abstract.
- **Petri dish / lab** — warm-toned science. Dropper, glass dish, clinical but not sterile.

## Script Criteria

> **Baseline:** All rules in `00 Global/Criteria/` apply automatically. Read Universal Copy Rules + the format-specific doc(s) for this batch before writing.

### Compliance

Full guardrails: [[Guardrails & Claims - IM8]]. Script-specific rules:

- **Don't over-hedge.** "Supports" is the default confident tone for structure/function claims. "May support" or "may help" only when the claim could be interpreted as treating or curing. Agents tend to spray "may help support" on everything — that reads as uncertain, not compliant. Most of IM8's claims are standard structure/function and need no hedging at all.
- Approved stats — exact phrasing only: "95% of users felt more energy," "85% of users felt better digestion," "80% of users felt they slept better," "75% of users noticed sharper focus." Never abbreviate ("95% more energy" is a different claim).
- Beckham = "co-founder." Sabalenka = "shareholder and ambassador." Never "endorses" or "spokesperson."
- "Replaces 16+" and "90 clinically dosed ingredients" and "NSF Certified for Sport" — all approved.
- No competitor brand names in ads. Use solution categories: "probiotics," "greens powders," "single-ingredient supplements."
- No disease treatment claims. No "works like Ozempic." No specific weight loss numbers.

### Platform Constraints

- **Duration: 30-60 seconds.** AppLovin requirement. All scripts must fit this window at 250 spm.
- Offer elements (welcome kit, guarantee) go in the platform end card, not the script.

### Product Specifics

- **Ingredient count: 90** (confirmed by client April 2026). Not 92 (homepage number).
- **Product page: get.im8health.com** — source of truth for dosages, claims, and specs.
- Use "nutrients" not "ingredients" when talking about what the body needs.

### Taste Beat

- Not mandatory. Must earn its place — if timing is tight or the concept doesn't naturally lead to a sip moment, cut it.
- When included, vary: spoken line ("and it actually tastes amazing"), reaction shot, or mixing moment.

### Production Constraints

- **T003 Specific:** Fit young male actor available. Use for hooks, b-roll, and UGC green screen.

### Editing Styles (from T003 Analysis)

Two reference pools — assignment per concept in Batch Plan under each persona heading.

**Plateful-style** (cloning their structural arc + editing flow):
- Structural arc: Hook (0-3s) → Problem/Agitation (3-15s) → Solution + Mechanism (15-25s) → Proof Stack (25-45s) → CTA + Urgency (final 5-10s)
- 1.3-2.0 sec shots. Hard cuts, no fancy transitions
- Hooks start with motion, CGI, action, or text — not face-to-camera
- Numbered frameworks ("3 reasons," "4 things that happen")
- Color-coded states: B&W/desaturated for pain, vibrant/saturated for solution
- Split screens for good-vs-bad and cause/effect
- UGC + polished B-roll intercut
- Captions with keyword emphasis — color-coded or boxed

**Top spenders style** (replicating what's already working in IM8's account):
- "What happens when" curiosity hooks for timelines
- Milestone framing with past-perfect tense ("have cleared," "have colonized")
- CGI body process visualization (single-focus per concept)
- Creator trust path: face-to-camera, no music, casual pacing, longer takes
- Social proof stacking in closes (reviews → guarantee → celebrity → expert → certification)
- 120-125 BPM upbeat instrumental for timelines, no music for creator/trust

### Brand-Specific Quality Checklist Items

1. Ingredient count = 90 (not 92)
2. Stats match approved phrasing exactly — "X% OF USERS FELT [benefit]" not "X% [benefit]"
3. Compliance hedging appropriate — "supports" not over-hedged to "may help support" everywhere
4. Beckham/Sabalenka titles correct (co-founder / shareholder and ambassador)
5. No competitor brand names (solution categories only)
6. Duration fits 30-60s window at 250 spm
7. **Pain-first language** — flag any VO line that opens with an ingredient name or mechanism term before the pain point
8. **IM8 named before the close** — product name must appear in every ad variation, not just one hook. Body mention at minimum.
9. **Hook caption variety** — not all 3 hooks use the same caption angle (all problem, all data). At least one uses a winner-inspired pattern.
10. **Data balance** — if VO delivers data, visuals must carry emotional engagement, not literal data visualization
11. **Visual column readability** — `<br><br>` between distinct visual beats in the Visual column

### Temporal Discounting

Show immediate or early benefits to reduce perceived wait time. At least half the concepts per persona must include temporal discounting language ("from the very first sip," "within hours," "by day 7"). All benefit timelines must include it by definition.

- **Fitness:** #1 ✅ (done), #2 ← add
- **Gut Health:** #4 ✅ (timeline), #6 ← add
- **Menopause:** #7 ← add, #9 ✅ (timeline), #10 ← add, #14 ← add
- **Energy:** #15 ← add, #16 ✅ (timeline)

### Early Product Mention

- At least 1/3 of the batch must name IM8 within the first 25 seconds.
- **Every script must mention IM8 before the close.** If none of the hooks name the product, the body must. The close alone is insufficient — viewers who drop before the close never learn what product they're watching an ad for.

### Hook-to-Body Transition Checkpoint

For every script, all 3 hook variations must transition cleanly into the shared body. Verification method: read each hook's last VO line → body's first VO line back-to-back. **Internal QA only — do not include the verification section in the deliverable scripts doc.**

### On-Screen Caption Variety in Hooks

- Hook on-screen captions must not all focus exclusively on the same angle (all problem-focused, all data, all curiosity gap). At least one variation must use a winner-inspired caption pattern: curiosity promise ("WHAT HAPPENS TO YOUR [X] IN 90 DAYS"), result/benefit promise, or stat-first callout.
- Captions never repeat the VO verbatim — two channels, two messages. If the VO is delivering data, the caption should reframe or add a different layer.
- Check hook captions against the winning patterns from the ads analysis.

### Data Balance Rule

- When VO delivers data/facts, visuals must carry emotional engagement — not literal data visualization. CGI, lifestyle B-roll, or aspirational footage prevents the ad from reading as a data dump in the first 7 seconds.
- If all three channels (VO, captions, visuals) are simultaneously delivering factual/data content, at least one must shift to emotional or visually engaging content.
- "Engaging" = visually stimulating (colorful CGI, a person working out, dynamic B-roll). Not forced humor — visual interest that makes the viewer feel something.

### Visual Column Readability

- Visual descriptions in script tables must use `<br><br>` between distinct visual beats, matching the readability of the On-Screen Captions column. Editors scan the Visual column while editing — clumped text slows them down.

### Plateful Reference Standard

- Every Plateful reference in the References section must include `Overall editing style.` as a sub-bullet. This tells the editor to clone the full editing approach (shot pacing, hard cuts, color coding, caption rhythm), not just the specific element called out in the first sub-bullet.
