## 🔍 Strategic Audit

### 1. The "Tony Robbins" Factor
- **Observation:** Tony remains the primary spend driver. The #1 and #2 spenders both feature him.
- **CTR vs. Conversion:** While Tony gets attention (1.2-1.5% CTR), the ROAS is lagging (0.28 - 0.36). This suggests Tony is an excellent *attractor* but the current offer/landing page bridge might be weak, or the "Sale" fatigue is setting in (Frequency was flagged in prior audits).
- **The "Billionaire" Opportunity:** Ad #9 (QuoteDream) shows the highest ROAS (0.67) in the top 10, though at lower spend. This creative leverages the "Access" and "Aspirational" angle Tony is famous for.

### 2. High CTR Native Formats (Scroll-Stoppers)
- **Top CTRs:** Outside the top 10 spend, `Core - UGC - Raising my Daughter` (2.05%) and `MD - As a Mom - C3` (1.93%) are winning on attention but getting zero spend.
- **Missed Signal:** The "Parenting" and "Mom" angles are scroll-stopping efficiently at low CPMs. We should port these native hooks into the TRT/Hormone funnels for men.

### 3. Comparison / Us vs. Them
- **Observation:** `Core - Us vs Them - $1.37 Background` is a top 5 spender with a relatively healthy ROAS (0.38). 
- **Learning:** Tangible price-per-day or feature comparison (the "$1.37" anchor) provides the logical justification needed after a Tony Robbins emotional hook.

### 4. Peptide Gap
- **Finding:** Peptides are mentioned in the Brand and Product Context files as a key differentiator (Sermorelin, Rx access), but they are **completely absent** from the top 10 performance list.
- **Opportunity:** We have a proven "Peptide Not Working" reframe structure from IM8 that could be adapted for Lifeforce to target the "Chronic Everything-Failer" who wants deeper optimization.
