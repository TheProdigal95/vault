## Brief 4 — Narrated UGC Humor

**Format:** Video — voiceover narration over B-roll and text overlays. No creator on screen. Humor scenarios told as VO while stock/product footage plays. Three different humor hooks, same body.
**Persona:** Heavy Sleeper (broad, young)
**Structure:** Narrated UGC Humor (~20-35s)
**Creator:** AI-generated male narrator
**Close Type:** Urgency/offer

### Concept

The viewer is a heavy sleeper who has lived through the universal pain of alarm chaos. This ad adapts the proven humor-skit format from FitSleeps Ads 05, 06, and 10 but replaces filmed physical comedy with narrated UGC. A male creator tells a relatable, slightly exaggerated alarm scenario as voiceover while B-roll, stock footage, text overlays, and product shots play underneath. The humor hook earns 5-8 seconds of attention. Then the narration shifts to a straight product pitch with benefit stacking, a partner beat, and a review pop-up. Three hook variations provide different humor scenarios that all feed into the same body and close. Fast time to benefit: product appears by second 6-8 across all hooks.

### Editor Notes
- Each hook is a different humor scenario with its own narration and B-roll. All three share the same body and close.
- The narration carries the humor. Edit the B-roll to match the pacing of the VO, not the other way around.
- Text overlays should appear on beat with the narration, punching up visual humor.
- The body and close are straight product pitch. The shift from humor to product should feel like a gear change, not a crash. Let the last beat of the hook breathe for a half-second before the body starts.
- Review pop-up in the body: use a real Trustpilot screenshot-style overlay. Keep it on screen for 2-3 seconds.

### Footage Needs
- **Narrator VO:** AI-generated male voice. Conversational, slightly amused during hook, shifting to confident and direct for body/close. Not a comedy voice. Think "guy telling a funny story to his friend, then recommending something he actually uses."
- **Hook A B-roll:** Dark bedroom, phone alarm blaring on nightstand, sleeping person not reacting, second person entering the room looking annoyed/exhausted, hand slapping the phone to silence it.
- **Hook B B-roll:** Quick cuts of failed wake-up methods: mom yelling from a doorway, someone throwing a pillow, a dog jumping on a bed, someone shaking the sleeper's shoulder. Each 1-2 seconds. Tier list graphic overlay with tiers (F, D, C, S).
- **Hook C B-roll:** Split or sequential: morning chaos side (alarm blaring, pillow thrown at sleeper, lights flipped on, someone pulling blanket off) then calm side (person waking up peacefully, stretching, no sound, soft light).
- **Body B-roll:** Product unboxing or band on wrist, close-up of band vibrating, person waking up calmly, partner still sleeping beside them.
- **Close B-roll:** Product on wrist, Trustpilot rating overlay (4.6 stars, screenshot-style).

### Visual Direction
- **Tone:** Amused and relatable during hooks. The narrator sounds like he is recounting his own morning to a friend. Shifts to confident and direct for the product pitch. Never salesy, never over-the-top.
- **Edit pace:** Fast. 1-3 second shots during hook B-roll. Body shots hold slightly longer (2-4 seconds) to let the product register. Close is one clean shot with overlay.
- **Caption style:** Bold white sans-serif text, semi-transparent dark background. All caps for key words. Tier list graphic for Hook B is an overlay, not a physical prop.
- **Color/light:** Hook footage should feel slightly chaotic (warm, messy bedroom light, shaky handheld energy). Body footage shifts to calm morning light (cool, soft, steady).
