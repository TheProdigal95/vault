# Script Structure: Creator-Led Contrarian

Creator on couch or selfie angle, product in hand from frame 1, opens with a contrarian warning or challenge. Real B-roll inserts as proof shots. Optional comment stitch UI to look like native TikTok. Two-creator pattern: storyteller tells the story, second person in scrubs/uniform handles the product demo section.

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Contrarian warning → pain story (brief) → proof/redemption → product demo (second creator) → close.
- **VO register:** Female 35-55, warm and urgent. Conversational, not polished. Product in hand from frame 1 — not revealed dramatically. COM49 "Teacher Traveling Yapper" energy.
- **Time to benefit:** ~15s. Pain story is compressed; proof arrives quickly.
- **Rhythm:** Deliberate and urgent on warning beats, slower on pain, upbeat on proof. Let the argument drive the rhythm.
- **Duration:** 55-75s.
- **Close type:** CTA-direct or Guarantee-forward.
- **Visual grammar:** Phone-quality selfie/couch for all creator beats. Real B-roll inserts only as proof shots (cobblestone walking, terrain, location variety). No color grading. Comment stitch UI when applicable.
- **Hook defaults:** Contrarian statement naming a competitor + a destination/context ("Please don't wear your Skechers to Europe"). Green screen reaction over organic TikTok content. Comment stitch UI.
- **Caption strategy:** Native — captions may closely mirror VO for sound-off readability.

### When to Use
- Products that replace something the buyer already uses and regrets
- Travel, lifestyle, and experience-driven categories where the emotional cost of the wrong choice is high
- When the creator's identity (teacher, nurse, traveler) stacks with the product story

### Key Rules
- Creator stays on the couch/selfie setup. B-roll inserts handle location variety.
- Product in hand from frame 1. Not revealed dramatically. It's already there because she's about to warn you.
- Two creators, two roles: storyteller tells the arc, second person in scrubs handles product demo. Don't brief the storyteller to list features.
- Emotional shifts must be visible on the face: pain grimace at the failure beat, instant brightening at the pivot. Script these in the Visual column.
