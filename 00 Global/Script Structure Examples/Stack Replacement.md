# Script Structure: Stack Replacement

"Here's what you're currently taking. Here's what replaces all of it." The body visually disqualifies 5-8 single products with individual prices, builds a running cost total, then sweeps them aside and reveals the all-in-one replacement. The overhead sweep IS the visual anchor. Different from Countdown Ranking because it replaces a category (supplement stacks, skincare routines), not rating individual competitors.

### **Core Breakdown**
- **Structure:** Price/math hook (receipt, cabinet, bottle count) → bottles enter frame one by one with price cards → running total builds → hand sweeps bottles aside → product placed center with ingredient overlays → cost comparison → ease/simplicity close.
- **VO register:** Female 40-55, matter-of-fact. Not dismissive of the individual supplements — acknowledging they work, but pointing out the cost and hassle of managing 8 bottles. Shifts to relief and simplicity at the product reveal.
- **Time to benefit:** ~18s. The disqualification sequence takes the first half; product proof and cost comparison take the second.
- **Rhythm:** Bottles enter at 1s each during the disqualification. Prices land before the VO line ends — let the math stack visually. The sweep is decisive (one motion). Product beat slows down — let the simplicity breathe.
- **Duration:** 45-55s.
- **Close type:** Ease/simplicity ("One scoop. One minute. One formula.").
- **Visual grammar:** Overhead counter/table. Real bottles, real sachet, real demonstration — no CGI. Bottles enter one by one with name + price card. Running total climbs ($18 → $43 → $65 → ... → $150+). Hand sweep clears the frame. Product placed center with ingredient overlays popping on. Cost comparison overlay at close.
- **Hook defaults:** Formula/math ($150/month receipt). VoC confessional (clearing the cabinet). Short/direct (hand sweeps bottles, sachet placed center, "here is the math").
- **Caption strategy:** ❌ prefix per disqualified bottle with price. Running total in bold. ✅ at product reveal with "ALL IN ONE SCOOP" framing. Cost comparison at close ($2.61/day vs $150/month).

### When to Use
- All-in-one products that replace multiple single-ingredient alternatives (supplements, skincare, cleaning)
- When the monthly cost of the current stack is a compelling number ($100-$200+)
- When the hassle of managing multiple products is part of the pain
- When ingredient overlap between singles and the all-in-one is demonstrable

### Key Rules
- **Real products, real bottles, real demonstration.** No CGI. The overhead sweep must look like a real person clearing their counter.
- **Prices must be accurate.** Each single supplement's price should be verifiable. The running total must be the real sum.
- **The sweep is one decisive motion.** Not animated, not gradual. Hand enters frame, pushes bottles aside, sachet placed center. The visual contrast (cluttered → clean) is the argument.
- **Don't dismiss the individual supplements.** "Eight bottles. Past 150 dollars a month. And still chasing each symptom alone." The problem is the fragmentation, not the ingredients.
- **Cost comparison is the close argument.** $2.61/day vs $150/month. The math does the selling.
