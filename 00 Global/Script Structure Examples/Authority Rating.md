# Script Structure: Authority Rating

An authority figure (doctor, podiatrist, specialist) rates named competitors with numeric scores and one-line clinical knocks, then reveals the product as the winner. The authority IS the argument — spending 30-40% of the ad dismissing competitors before mentioning the product is the confidence signal.

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Authority rates 4-6 competitors with scores (2/10, 3/10, etc.), each with a unique clinical failure. Product revealed last with the highest score.
- **VO register:** Male 40-55, clinical authority. Measured, slightly dismissive of competitors. Not promotional — evaluating honestly.
- **Time to benefit:** ~30s. First half is competitor teardown, second half is product proof.
- **Rhythm:** 2-3s per competitor (shoe graphic + score + one-line knock + next). Proven by @thesoledoctor — 8 shoes in 21 seconds.
- **Duration:** 75-90s.
- **Close type:** Value Math (price anchor vs competitor prices) or CTA-direct.
- **Visual grammar:** Single-camera talking head with competitor graphics overlaid. PiP transition when shifting from teardown to product demo. Authority stays on screen during demo (corner-box). Full-screen return for close.
- **Hook defaults:** Lead with the lowest-scoring competitor ("Skechers GoWalk. Two out of ten.") — the score IS the scroll-stop. Or grid dismiss (all competitors get red X, product appears alone).
- **Caption strategy:** Two-channel. VO delivers clinical explanation; captions show scores, verdicts, and proof points.

### When to Use
- Physical products with 4+ identifiable competitors the buyer already owns
- Categories where an authority's judgment carries weight (health, safety, professional gear)
- When the "safest" or "best for [specific use case]" reframe targets a specific buyer concern

### Key Rules
- Each competitor gets a UNIQUE clinical/practical failure — no two share the same knock
- Time-stamped failures preferred ("collapses by hour 8", "no arch support by hour 3")
- Authority is demonstrated through action (rating, dismissing), not claimed through credentials
- No fake named doctors, certifications, or implied endorsements
- Competitor brand names spoken in VO; readable character swaps in captions (H0KAS, SK3CHERS)
