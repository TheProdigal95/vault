# Script Structure: Consequence Narrative

Opens with the dollar or emotional cost of the problem — what the viewer lost, missed, or wasted because of the wrong product. The body tells the story of what was lost in specific, visceral detail. The product is introduced as the thing that prevented it from happening again. The strongest emotional hook type in the competitive comparison category.

### Core Breakdown
- **Structure:** Cost statement hook ($4,000 trip, 2 lost days) → what was planned → what actually happened (loss beat) → what went wrong (product failure) → product as prevention → proof → close.
- **VO register:** Female 40-55, raw and confessional on pain beats. Voice slows at pain words, cracks slightly at the loss moment. Pivot to solution is earned relief, not sales energy. Like telling a friend at dinner what happened.
- **Time to benefit:** ~36s. The loss narrative takes up the first half. Product proof earns the second half.
- **Rhythm:** 3-4s holds on hook images. 2-3s per clause at pain beats (slow, deliberate). Faster once the pivot happens. Let the pain breathe.
- **Duration:** 65-78s.
- **Close type:** Guarantee-forward ("Do not lose days of a trip you saved up for").
- **Visual grammar:** Hotel balcony, feet up, city behind (the "watching from a chair" beat). Cobblestone B-roll for texture and proof. Creator on couch for storytelling beats. Sound-off consequence framing — key numbers ($4,000, 2 DAYS) in large caps.
- **Hook defaults:** Hotel balcony with cost statement ("my feet cost me 2 DAYS of my $4,000 trip"). TK direct clip of soaked shoes on cobblestones. Green screen reaction over medical boot TikTok.
- **Caption strategy:** Pain captions in hook (dollar amounts, days lost). Proof and relief in body. Guarantee in close. Key numbers in all-caps for sound-off impact.

### When to Use
- When the cost of the wrong product is quantifiable in dollars AND emotional terms
- When the loss is something the viewer can viscerally imagine (missing days of a trip, sitting out while others continue)
- When the viewer has already experienced or fears the loss — not hypothetical
- Strongest when the dollar amount is large enough to trigger regret ($1,000+ trip, $500+ in failed products)

### Key Rules
- The loss beat must be SPECIFIC: "I heard them leave every morning. The door closing. And I sat there looking at a city I flew six thousand miles to see. From a chair." Not "I missed out on the trip."
- The dollar cost appears in the hook AND the close — it frames the entire ad. The close reframes the product price against the cost of not having it.
- The product failure that caused the loss must be named: "I was in Skechers. Soft foam soles. The foam just collapsed." Not "my shoes weren't right."
- The proof section must show the SAME scenario redeemed: "Six days of cobblestones. Two airports. Two museum days. Same pair. Did not miss a single thing."
- Creator stays in one location (couch, camera). B-roll handles the locations. Emotional arc is carried by facial performance and voice, not visual variety.
